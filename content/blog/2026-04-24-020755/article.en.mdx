### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine that omnipotent "butler robot" you've seen in concept art is finally becoming a reality. Previous AI was like a Q&A customer service agent: helpful, but always felt like it lacked a bit of "soul." Now, AI is racing towards "proactively solving problems for you" – this is the so-called "AI Agentification trend."

It's like this: instead of needing to issue commands to individual apps, you can directly tell your AI assistant, "Help me plan this weekend's itinerary, including booking a restaurant, buying movie tickets, reserving a shared car, and considering weather changes." Then, this AI assistant can act like a real secretary, contacting various services, handling payments, and even managing unexpected situations on its own.

**Google Gemini** and **Apple Intelligence** are prime examples evolving in this direction. They are no longer just simple voice assistants; they aim to become the core of your phone or operating system, connecting all applications and services to make your digital life more seamless. Before, we'd tap on an app on our phone; in the future, an AI Agent might open apps for us, or even directly complete tasks within them.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let's start with the "money-burning" news. **Meta** announced today that it will cut approximately **8,000 jobs** next month, representing about **10%** of its total workforce. Simultaneously, thousands of previously planned hires will also be canceled. According to an internal memo, **Meta** expects to invest a staggering **$135 billion** in AI projects in **2026**, an amount nearly equal to its total AI investments over the past three years. **Mark Zuckerberg** stated that **2026** would be a year where AI "dramatically changes how we work," as he observes significant productivity boosts from employees leveraging AI tools, allowing one person to complete projects that previously required an entire team. Even **Microsoft** is joining this wave of "AI optimization," reportedly offering voluntary buyout packages to **7%** (approximately **8,750 employees**) of its U.S. workforce.

In terms of computing infrastructure, **Google Cloud** unveiled its eighth-generation Tensor Processing Units (TPU) – **TPU 8t** and **TPU 8i** – at its Cloud Next 2026 conference on **April 22**. These two chips are optimized for AI training and inference workloads, respectively.

*   The **TPU 8t** is designed for training large AI models, connecting up to **9,600 chips** in a single superpod, offering **121 ExaFlops** of computing power and **2PB** of shared high-bandwidth memory. Its training performance is **3 times** better than the previous generation, with a **2.7 times** improvement in performance per dollar.
*   The **TPU 8i** is optimized for inference tasks, especially suited for low-latency applications where multiple AI Agents operate simultaneously. Its memory capacity is boosted to **384MB SRAM** per chip, with an **80%** improvement in performance per dollar.

Both TPUs are expected to be generally available through Google Cloud's AI Hypercomputer platform **later in 2026**. **Google** projects its TPU shipments to reach **4.3 million units** in **2026** and expand to **35 million units** by **2028**.

Concurrently, cloud service providers continue to deepen collaborations with AI startups. **Amazon** has expanded its strategic partnership with **Anthropic**, injecting an additional **$5 billion** in immediate investment, with the potential to raise the total investment to **$25 billion**. In exchange, **Anthropic** has committed to spending over **$100 billion** on AWS services over the next decade and utilizing **5 gigawatts** of Amazon AI chip capacity. This deal propelled Amazon's stock price to rebound **20%** from its March low.

On another front, **Elon Musk**'s **SpaceX** is preparing for its rumored **mid-2026** IPO, with an estimated valuation of approximately **$1.75 trillion** and plans to raise about **$75 billion**, potentially making it the largest initial public offering ever. According to an S-1 filing reviewed by Reuters, **SpaceX** is telling investors that its Total Addressable Market (TAM) is as high as **$28.5 trillion**, with over **$22 trillion** directly from enterprise AI applications. **SpaceX** completed the acquisition of **xAI** in **February** this year, a deal that valued **SpaceX** at **$1 trillion** and **xAI** at approximately **$250 billion**, bringing the combined entity's value to about **$1.25 trillion**. Despite **xAI** reporting an operating loss of **$6.4 billion** in **2025**, **SpaceX** plans to invest heavily in developing its own GPU chips and computing infrastructure, even partnering with **Cursor** to leverage its **Colossus supercomputer**, which possesses computing power equivalent to "**one million H100 Nvidia chips**," for AI programming.

Regarding the AI Agentification trend, **Google Cloud** has launched its AI Agent platform, echoing **Google Gemini**'s integration into the Android ecosystem. **Android 16** has already brought significant visual upgrades and enhanced **Gemini** features, with **Google** gradually positioning **Gemini** as the default AI assistant experience for over **2 billion** Android devices. **Apple** is not far behind; **Google** confirmed that **Siri**, enhanced with **Gemini**, and the next generation of **Apple Intelligence** are expected to launch **later in 2026**. **Siri** will gain "Agentic powers" from **Gemini**, enabling it to perform actions on behalf of users and interact better with other applications. **iOS 26.4.2** and **iPadOS 26.4.2** were released on **April 23**, while more new features for **Apple Intelligence** are anticipated with **iOS 27** in **September 2026**.

However, this ecosystem integration has also raised concerns among regulators. The European Commission is preparing to demand that **Google** open up the Android system, allowing other AI assistants (such as **OpenAI's ChatGPT** and **Anthropic's Claude**) to access Android features used by **Gemini**, including voice activation and system-level search integration.

Furthermore, AI governance and regulatory issues are receiving increasing attention. The U.S. federal government unveiled its "National AI Policy Framework" on **March 20**, aiming to establish a nationwide AI governance framework that emphasizes protecting children, strengthening communities, respecting intellectual property, and safeguarding free speech. The framework also recommends establishing unified federal standards to prevent a confusing patchwork of state AI laws.

Meanwhile, legal issues surrounding generative AI content continue to evolve. Connecticut is considering a new bill (HB 5312) that would allow residents to sue for unauthorized AI-generated sexual images, with platforms failing to remove them promptly potentially facing fines of up to **$25,000** per day. U.S. federal courts have also shown mixed rulings on whether content generated by AI chatbots is protected by "attorney-client privilege," highlighting the challenges new technologies pose to existing legal frameworks.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

So, what does all this mean for us ordinary folks?

First, you might need to prepare yourself for your smartphone to become super smart. In the future, the AI assistant in your phone will no longer just be a passive responder; it will proactively learn your habits and help you handle more complex tasks. Imagine no longer needing to compare prices for airline tickets yourself; your AI assistant will automatically monitor price changes and offer the best suggestions when you need them. Even at work, AI Agents could automatically handle initial report drafts and data analysis, freeing you from repetitive tasks. As **Mark Zuckerberg** said, **AI will significantly boost our productivity**.

However, this also brings challenges. When AI can do more, will our jobs be affected? **Meta**'s layoffs are a warning. Companies may streamline their workforce in pursuit of AI-driven efficiency. This forces us to think about how to collaborate with AI, enhance our value, and engage in creative, strategic, or interpersonal work that AI cannot easily replace.
