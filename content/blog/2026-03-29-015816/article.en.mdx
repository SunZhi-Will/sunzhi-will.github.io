### 📋 Quick Highlights
- OpenAI's Sora video platform halted operations due to colossal daily costs of **$15 million**, underscoring AI's economic viability challenges.
- OpenAI officially released a GPT-5 preview, drawing significant attention for its **agentic AI** capabilities and **multimodal reasoning**.
- Google fully launched Gemini's **personal intelligence** features, integrating user data for highly customized AI experiences.
- Despite Microsoft's massive AI investments, the market remains cautious about its **short-term returns** and **high capital expenditure**.
- AI Agents are emerging in fintech, smart homes, and healthcare, promising to streamline complex tasks.
- Global AI policy, data sovereignty, and geopolitical competition are becoming central concerns for nations.
- Smart TVs and glasses are becoming new hubs for home and personal services through deep AI integration.

### What Happened?
Friends, the recent major happenings in the AI world are nothing short of a high-stakes tech soap opera! 🎭 I'd wager the most explosive news these past few days is undoubtedly OpenAI's Sora video generation platform, which, much to everyone's surprise, has "exited the stage"! When I first heard the news, what sprang to mind wasn't its once-stunning AI-generated videos, but rather the astronomically huge "money-burning" figures behind it. This isn't just the failure of a single product; it's more like a wake-up call, reminding us that AI isn't enough just to "show off skills." Ultimately, it needs to return to reality and figure out how to "earn its keep."

Meanwhile, other AI giants haven't been idle. OpenAI itself has rolled out a more powerful **GPT-5** preview, spotlighting **agentic AI**; Google's **Gemini personal intelligence** features have also been extensively opened to US users, making AI understand you better than ever. And Microsoft, the "big spender" of AI investments, continues to pour money in, but the market is starting to question whether these "big gestures" can translate into tangible profits.

These headlines, in essence, are a microcosm of AI formally transitioning from its "showcase phase" into its "real-world application phase." We are moving from a simple AI chatbot era towards a new epoch of **agentic AI** capable of autonomously executing tasks and deeply integrating into every facet of our lives.

### Simply Put, What Is This?
Imagine no longer needing an AI assistant that's all "talk and no action," but instead having a "super agent" 🕵️‍♂️ that can truly "get things done" for you. Past AI might have been like an encyclopedia – you ask something, it answers. But asking it to complete a whole set of complex tasks, such as "plan a trip based on my email content, then automatically book flights and hotels," would have been almost fantastical.

Now, this "agent" AI, known as "**Agentic AI**" (AI Agent), has learned to break down tasks, use tools, and even self-correct.

<Callout type="info" title="The Evolution of Agentic AI">
It no longer just passively responds but can actively think, plan, and execute complex tasks, much like the robot vacuum that cleans and mops your home, except its "IQ" is hundreds of times higher, and the tasks it can handle extend from the physical realm to the digital world.
</Callout>

For instance, Google Gemini's "**Personal Intelligence**" feature is like having a private secretary. You can ask it to automatically remind you when to leave for the airport based on your flight information in Gmail, or even, when you're considering a purchase, to directly show you outfits with similar items from your Google Photos for suggestions. Isn't this much more convenient than manually searching and flipping through photos yourself? This is a true depiction of AI evolving from "providing information" to "**providing services**."

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
Speaking of "money-burning," OpenAI's Sora is a stark example. According to the latest reports, the Sora video generation platform announced it would cease services on **March 24, 2026**. Why? Primarily because its operating costs were simply astronomical! The estimated daily inference cost soared to an astonishing **$15 million**, while its total lifetime revenue amounted to a mere **$2.1 million**. These disparate figures have led the industry to describe Sora as a "resource black hole."

To make matters worse, a previously planned **$1 billion** collaboration with Disney also fell through. This agreement would have allowed Sora to leverage over 200 character IPs from Disney's portfolio, including Marvel, Pixar, and Star Wars, to generate content. Sora's user downloads also plummeted by **66%** from their peak. Beyond economic factors, challenges in auditing deepfakes and copyright issues, as well as OpenAI's need to reallocate GPU resources ahead of its IPO, also contributed to Sora's withdrawal. This shift has also seen OpenAI's Sora team pivot to robotics research.

Sora's shutdown clearly signals to the market that pure video generation applications are commercially difficult to sustain, and tech giants are reorienting resources toward more lucrative core AI tools, such as ChatGPT, and enterprise-grade solutions. In stark contrast to Sora's predicament, other AI models continue to evolve. OpenAI released a preview of **GPT-5** on **March 27, 2026**, hailed as "the most advanced AI model ever." Its key highlights include native **agentic capabilities**, **real-time web access**, and **multimodal reasoning**. This means GPT-5 can autonomously plan, use tools, and execute multi-step tasks without constant human prompting. Some enterprise clients testing GPT-5 have stated it's "really good, a substantial improvement in quality." A report by LLM Stats also indicates that OpenAI's GPT-5.4 model and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro are current market leaders.

Google's **Gemini model** has also seen significant advancements. The latest news indicates Google is fully rolling out its **Personal Intelligence** feature across the US for all free-tier users, accessible in AI-powered search, the Gemini app, and Gemini in Chrome. This feature allows Gemini to access your linked Google applications, such as Gmail and Google Photos, to provide highly personalized responses. For example, you can ask Gemini to suggest travel destinations based on your past flight confirmation emails, or to summarize specific conversations from your Gmail. This development also brings to mind Apple's planned Siri personalization features, expected later this year.

On the other hand, Microsoft, a heavyweight investor in AI, has also seen its financial situation come under scrutiny. Reports indicate Microsoft's stock price fell by **17%** in 2026, as investors grew concerned about its massive capital expenditures on AI infrastructure, particularly quarterly AI-related capital expenditures reaching a staggering **$29.9 billion**, nearly double that of the previous year. Microsoft plans to invest **$50 billion** in AI expansion in Global South countries by 2030 and accelerate data center construction. While Microsoft remains steadfast in embedding AI across its product ecosystem, such as **agentic AI** in Microsoft 365, the market is beginning to demand more substantial short-term returns from these colossal investments.

In terms of specific industry applications, AI is also shining brightly in healthcare and elderly care. Smart devices, remote monitoring, and telehealth services are expanding the accessibility of expertise and opening new rehabilitation pathways for patients with chronic illnesses and disabilities through AI-driven rehabilitation and assistive technologies. AI-powered systems can analyze data, identify patterns, and quickly detect potential risks before health issues worsen, such as monitoring changes in activity patterns, sleep quality, or daily routines, and sending alerts when abnormalities occur. Voice-activated AI assistants can even provide companionship and conversation. In the fintech sector, **AI Agents** are also beginning to emerge. For example, Espressio AI is using specialized AI agent systems to revolutionize fintech marketing, automating content production, competitive intelligence monitoring, and visual generation. There are even cases showing that an AI agent successfully negotiated a **$109** refund for a user from DoorDash in minutes, whereas human attempts failed after 20 minutes. This demonstrates the astonishing efficiency of AI Agents in handling complex, repetitive tasks.

Debates around AI policy are raging in Washington D.C., with tech giants and labor unions presenting differing visions for AI development and regulation. This reflects that AI is not merely a technological tool but also a strategic instrument in national sovereignty and geopolitical competition.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
I often reflect that Sora's departure isn't just a setback for OpenAI, but a significant milestone for the entire AI industry. It clearly tells us: **While technological breakthroughs are crucial, the health and sustainability of the business model are key to whether AI can truly land and change the world.**

For you and me, this means AI is no longer a distant sci-fi concept but is increasingly integrating into our daily lives, transitioning from "passive services" to "**proactive intelligence**."

In smart homes, trends like SDMC's **AI Home Hub** showcased at MWC 2026, or Micro-RGB TVs serving as smart home centers, indicate that your home life will become smarter and more personalized. Imagine your TV not only showing programs but also automatically adjusting lighting, monitoring home security, and even anticipating your needs based on your routine. All this benefits from AI technology's increasing ability to perform "edge computing" on end devices, reducing reliance on the cloud, and enhancing response speed and privacy.

In healthcare and elderly care, AI's development is nothing short of a godsend. AI-driven remote monitoring and preventive care, for instance, can provide greater peace of mind for elderly individuals living alone, while doctors can use AI for more accurate diagnoses and treatment plans. This will significantly alleviate the problem of uneven distribution of medical resources, allowing more people to enjoy high-quality care.

This means many repetitive, process-driven tasks will be handled by AI, allowing us to dedicate our energy to more creative and strategic work.

At the same time, we must also confront the challenges brought by AI development. Sora's shutdown reminds us that the "cost of trial and error" in AI can be extremely high. Moreover, government regulation of AI, the struggle for data sovereignty, and AI's impact on the labor market are all serious issues we must consider as we enjoy AI's conveniences.

The past few years have seen an explosive growth of generative AI, with various models vying for attention, seemingly able to lead trends just by generating content. But Sora, through its painful example, proved that without a sustainable business model, even the most dazzling technology can be nothing more than a fleeting fireworks display.
