### 📋 Quick Highlights
- AI military application ethical debates intensify, with Anthropic suing the U.S. Department of Defense over usage restrictions.
- AI is causing a structural impact on junior roles, with software development and customer service recruitment already significantly declining.
- Strong AI server demand leads Wistron and Pegatron to anticipate "double-digit" or even "tenfold" revenue growth.
- Nvidia is set to unveil new AI inference chips at GTC 2026, while Amazon partners with Cerebras to challenge its market dominance.
- AI agents are on the rise, but deepfake fraud is estimated to cost U.S. banking industries up to **$40 billion** by 2027.
- The EU actively simplifies AI regulations and explicitly bans "AI nudity" tools, strengthening AI governance.

### What Happened?

Opening the news this morning felt like stepping into an "epic AI brawl royale"! From fierce debates over national security and ethics to the white-hot arms race among tech giants for chips, and the profound impact of AI on our daily jobs and privacy—every headline makes you think: "Wow, this AI thing is really no joke; it's already deeply affecting our lives!"

Imagine you're relaxing at home on the weekend, only to scroll through your phone and find it flooded with AI-generated fake news, deepfake fraud, or even AI-generated inappropriate images. Doesn't that send a shiver down your spine? This is precisely the reality we're facing today.

### Simply Put, What Is This?

If we compare AI to a high-speed train, what we're seeing today is it accelerating on multiple tracks simultaneously, but also hitting some critical junctures where traffic lights and clashing values create a jarring screech.

**AI military applications** are like a train loaded with high-tech weaponry. Nations want control, but some companies want to hit the brakes, fearing it might run amok. It's like we've given a child an extremely clever toy, but the instruction manual reads: "Do not use for attacking others."

**AI's impact on employment** is like a major workplace reshuffle. Jobs we once considered "iron rice bowls" might now be partly replaced by AI, our "all-rounder colleague," forcing us to learn new skills. It's as if everyone was queuing for a certain amusement park ride, but AI cut in line and bought up many of the entry-level tickets.

**AI hardware demand** represents the train's engine and tracks. The world is frantically upgrading engines and laying new tracks because without this hardware, AI, no matter how smart, can't run. This is why Taiwanese contract manufacturers and chip design companies are becoming a global focal point.

Meanwhile, **AI agents and their privacy security** are like the "super service staff" on the AI train who can handle all your tasks. It sounds convenient, but you have to worry about whether they'll spill your secrets or be exploited by bad actors.

Finally, **AI governance and regulation** are the train's "traffic rules" and "train conductor." Everyone is scrambling to set rules to ensure this train can safely and fairly carry us into the future, rather than derailing into a disaster.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

Today's AI headlines are coming in waves, with various forces finding their positions and facing significant challenges in this transformation.

First, the most eye-catching is undoubtedly the ethical storm around **AI military applications**. The conflict between U.S. AI company **Anthropic** and the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) continues to escalate. **Anthropic** has sued the DoD after being blacklisted for refusing to allow its AI models to be used for domestic mass surveillance or fully autonomous lethal weapons. The DoD, citing supply chain regulations, labeled **Anthropic** a national security risk. This lawsuit highlights the escalating structural tension between state power, corporate ethics, and AI militarization. Interestingly, while Western nations debate this, Chinese commentators are gloating, viewing it as exposing inherent contradictions in U.S. AI governance. In stark contrast, **OpenAI** announced a new agreement allowing its models to be deployed in classified military environments, shocking many industry observers.

On the other hand, AI's impact on the **job market** is becoming increasingly tangible. According to the Stanford SIEPR Economic Summit 2026 report, recruitment for junior software developers has dropped by **20%**, and customer service center recruitment has decreased by **15%**. This is no longer a prediction but a "currently happening" reality. The report notes that professional fields like law, banking, and healthcare are shifting from a "pyramid-shaped" to a "diamond-shaped" career structure, with entry-level positions rapidly disappearing. This means that recent graduates will find higher barriers to entry for jobs. At the same time, AI isn't completely replacing humans; instead, it's creating new roles, projected to generate around **170 million** new jobs while eliminating **92 million** old ones, resulting in a net gain in employment opportunities. However, the demand for AI-related skills continues to rise, particularly in data and AI fields, where nearly **70%** of job demands are for mid-to-senior level talent, not junior. Therefore, cross-functional skills like critical thinking, creativity, empathy, and ethical judgment are becoming increasingly valuable in the AI era.

Now, let's look at the surging demand for **AI hardware and infrastructure**. Taiwan's Electronic Manufacturing Services (EMS) giants, **Wistron** and **Pegatron**, both anticipate significant revenue growth this year due to strong AI server demand. **Wistron** expects revenue to achieve "high double-digit percentage" year-over-year growth, while **Pegatron** even projects its AI server revenue to increase by "more than tenfold" compared to last year. This growth is primarily driven by orders from new cloud solution clients and large cloud service providers, especially for **Nvidia's GB300** server platform.

Speaking of **Nvidia**, the entire tech world is holding its breath for next week's **GTC 2026** conference. CEO **Jensen Huang** is expected to unveil a "world-shaking" new chip. The market broadly expects this to be an AI chip focused on "inference," potentially integrating technology from **Groq**, a chip startup **Nvidia** acquired last December for **$20 billion**. Additionally, the conference will feature updates on the **Vera Rubin architecture** and the next-generation **Feynman platform**.

However, **Nvidia's** dominance is also facing challenges. **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** announced a multi-year strategic partnership with **Cerebras Systems** to jointly launch "disaggregated inference" solutions, aiming to challenge **Nvidia's** stronghold in the GPU market. They claim this new architecture can provide inference speeds "21 times faster" than **Nvidia's** current **Blackwell** system. **AWS** will deploy **Cerebras CS-3** systems in its data centers and offer them via **Amazon Bedrock**, becoming **Cerebras's** first cloud provider for disaggregated inference solutions. **Amazon** also revealed that its capital expenditures are projected to reach **$200 billion** in 2026, with most of it allocated to expanding **AWS's** cloud and AI infrastructure to meet robust demand. **Microsoft** is demonstrating similarly astonishing investment levels, planning **$37.5 billion** in capital expenditures for the second quarter of fiscal year 2026 alone, and committing to invest **$17.5 billion** in India between 2026 and 2029 for AI infrastructure.

Next is the rise of **AI agents** and the accompanying **privacy and security challenges**. Agentic AI is becoming a strategic growth engine for businesses, with Microsoft actively promoting its **Copilot** and other autonomous agents to complete complex business processes. However, the proliferation of this technology also brings unprecedented risks. The development of generative AI makes cyberattacks "easier to launch and more convincing than ever before." Specifically, "deepfake financial fraud" is surging; Deloitte analysts estimate that by 2027, generative AI-driven bank fraud in the U.S. alone could amount to **$40 billion**. These are no longer opportunistic scams but "industrialized, AI-assisted operations." Social media platforms are also suffering; for instance, **X (formerly Twitter)**, facing legal pressure in the EU, is proposing changes to its blue check verification system, with **Elon Musk** arguing that paid verification helps reduce bots and spam on the platform. AI in email marketing can also generate "contextual hallucinations" or over-personalization based on opaque data, leading to brand trust erosion and legal compliance issues.

Finally, global discussions on **AI governance and regulation** continue to heat up. The EU is actively streamlining its landmark **AI Act** and has proposed a significant amendment explicitly banning the generation of "AI nudity tools," as well as other AI-generated sexual and intimate content without consent or child sexual abuse material. This ban comes after the **Grok chatbot** reportedly generated millions of non-consensual intimate images that spread globally. While the EU is at the forefront of AI governance, the Act also imposes significant compliance costs on small and medium-sized enterprises, with initial costs ranging from **€320,000 to €600,000**, plus additional annual expenses of up to **€150,000**, potentially eroding up to **40%** of profits.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

Living in this era of rapid AI development, each of us is like a passenger on an ever-accelerating boat.

For individuals, the most direct impact is the **transformation of work methods**. Repetitive, routine tasks, such as data entry, basic administrative work, and even some entry-level programming, are now being replaced or highly optimized by AI. It's as if your work efficiency has suddenly been boosted **several times over** by AI, but if your value is solely tied to these "repetitive tasks," then your job security is truly at risk. But don't worry, this also means that jobs requiring **uniquely human skills**—like creativity, complex decision-making, interpersonal interaction, and empathy—will become increasingly sought after. So, now is the time to rethink your skill tree and cultivate soft skills that AI cannot easily replace, such as communication, leadership, and critical thinking.

Regarding daily life, the convenience brought by AI is undeniable, but **privacy and security risks** are something we must always be vigilant about. The proliferation of deepfake fraud and AI bot spam makes the online world hard to distinguish between true and false. Protecting personal information, heightening vigilance, and learning to identify AI-generated fake content will become essential "digital survival skills" for everyone in the future. Just as you lock your door when you leave, you must learn to protect yourself online, or you might inadvertently become a victim of fraud.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a veteran investigative journalist, I believe the current development in the AI field is at a **critical juncture of 'rapid expansion and self-regulation'**.

**First, the ethical dilemma of military applications.** **Anthropic's** confrontation with the U.S. Department of Defense reveals the double-edged sword nature of AI technology. Companies, driven by commercial interests and national security, find it difficult to completely detach from the military sector, but at the same time, they must face societal moral scrutiny regarding AI autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. This contradiction isn't only playing out in the U.S. but also globally. China's criticism of U.S. AI governance using this incident, while politically motivated, indeed pinpoints the crux of the issue: as AI technology grows ever more powerful, how do we ensure it is not misused, becoming a tool for humanity's benefit rather than its destruction? This "ethical war" has just begun, and more companies and nations will be drawn into it.

Whoever commands powerful AI compute capacity will dictate the future AI ecosystem. **Nvidia's GTC 2026** and **Amazon's** partnership with **Cerebras** are microcosms of this compute power struggle. Taiwan's critical position in the global supply chain is thus further highlighted, but we must also consider how we can transition from contract manufacturing to becoming a provider of higher-value AI solutions amidst this wave.

**Finally, the duality of AI agents and deepfake technology.** AI agents are intended as efficiency-enhancing tools, but when maliciously exploited, especially in combination with deepfake technology for fraud, the societal harm will be immense. This tests not only the defense capabilities of the technology but also society's adaptability and widespread education. The EU's ban on "AI nudity tools" is a positive signal, indicating that regulators are attempting to draw red lines at the legal level. However, this also reminds us that legislative speed will never catch up with technological development; citizens' self-protection awareness and digital literacy remain the most crucial lines of defense.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

This AI wave is like a technological "gold rush," presenting both opportunities and traps. It's not here to steal your job, but to redefine it. It's not here to make you smarter, but to see how you can get smarter with it, while also learning to protect your "digital gold."
