### What Happened?

Hey everyone, have you also felt like the AI world is a roller coaster lately, with new things popping up daily, leaving you both excited and a little dizzy? Just opening the news recently, I found that AI competition has reached an incredible intensity! Giants are not only rapidly iterating on models but are also heavily investing or undertaking massive layoffs just to "claim territory." Even Taiwan's tech supply chain has been swept into this "compute arms race"!

Especially today, **April 26, 2026**, the global AI community is bustling with major news. **OpenAI** made another splash, releasing **GPT-5.5**, a new model touted as even more powerful in handling complex tasks. Not to be outdone, **Google DeepMind** rolled out **AI agents** that can run errands and conduct research for you. What surprised me most was **Anthropic**, whose models many users had recently complained were "getting dumber." Not only did they admit the issue and swiftly fix it, but they're also contemplating how to make AI tools more "user-friendly"! It's like my robot vacuum cleaner occasionally "acting up," but the manufacturer has to fix it quickly and also teach it to find the trash can on its own, isn't that interesting?

### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine AI as your personal team of super assistants—not just one, but a whole squad! Some specialize in coding, others excel at market research, and some can even manage production lines in a factory. Today's news is essentially telling you: these assistants are not only getting smarter (like GPT-5.5 and Google DeepMind's Deep Research agents), but their bosses are also sparing no expense to secure the best ones (Google investing **$40 billion** in Anthropic). They're even laying off human employees (thousands at Meta and Microsoft) to cut costs and hire more AI assistants.

The underlying logic behind all of this is a race for "**AI compute power**." No matter how brilliant your assistant is, they need sufficient "brain capacity" and "electricity" to function, right? That's why global demand for AI servers and GPUs has literally "exploded," leading to huge profits for Taiwanese server giants like Asus, Gigabyte, MSI, and Quanta! Simply put, this is a global "AI free-for-all," where every participant is trying to run faster and become stronger to secure a foothold in this revolution.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

The most eye-catching news these past few days has undoubtedly been the launch of **GPT-5.5** by **OpenAI**. This new model officially debuted on **April 24, 2026**, primarily emphasizing significant advancements in agentic coding, computer usage, knowledge tasks, and early scientific research. According to official data, GPT-5.5 achieved a score of **82.7%** on the Terminal-Bench 2.0 benchmark and **73.1%** on the Expert-SWE benchmark. This means it can more effectively handle complex code generation and problem-solving tasks. Currently, ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users can already access it. Moreover, API pricing is set at **$5** per million input tokens and **$30** per million output tokens.

Meanwhile, **Google DeepMind** hasn't been idle either, releasing its new **Deep Research** and **Deep Research Max** AI agents on **April 21, 2026**. The most impressive aspect of these agents, built on the **Gemini 3.1 Pro** model, is their ability to not only search information from the open web but also securely integrate private enterprise data. This is like having a super detective who can simultaneously consult public records and confidential company documents to provide you with a detailed report. Deep Research Max can even perform up to **160** search queries to deliver the most comprehensive background information, making it highly suitable for background analysis tasks requiring extensive data compilation.

However, AI model development isn't always smooth sailing. **Anthropic**'s **Claude** model recently experienced a minor mishap. Many users complained about a decline in the output quality of **Claude Code**, **Claude Agent SDK**, and **Claude Cowork**, reporting reduced reasoning capabilities, increased hallucinations, and even higher token consumption. After investigation, **Anthropic** admitted this was due to lowering Claude Code's default inference difficulty from "high" to "medium" on **March 4** to reduce latency, introducing a caching optimization bug on **March 26**, and modifying system prompts on **April 16** to reduce verbosity. These adjustments ultimately led to the model's "malfunction," but they stated these issues were corrected on **April 20** (version 2.1.116).

In terms of capital flow, the AI industry's funding heat shows no signs of cooling. **Google** is rumored to be investing a staggering **$40 billion** in **Anthropic**. This investment includes an immediate cash injection of **$10 billion** (at which point Anthropic's valuation reached **$350 billion**) and an additional **$30 billion** upon achieving specific performance milestones. Furthermore, Google Cloud will provide **5 terawatts** of computing power over the next five years. All of this comes shortly after **Amazon** pledged to invest up to **$25 billion** in Anthropic.

Another significant merger and acquisition deal involves Canadian AI startup **Cohere** acquiring Germany's **Aleph Alpha**. This transaction reportedly values the combined company at approximately **$20 billion**. German retail giant **Schwarz Group** (parent company of Lidl and Kaufland) also invested **$600 million** in this deal and will provide cloud infrastructure. The goal of this merger is to establish a "**sovereign AI**" alternative, allowing governments and regulated industries to maintain complete control over their data without relying on US tech giants. The merged company will establish dual headquarters in Canada and Germany.

Even more intriguing, behind the AI investment boom, there's also a wave of layoffs. **Meta** announced plans to cut **10%** of its workforce, approximately **8,000 employees**, and eliminate **6,000** open roles. Meanwhile, **Microsoft** offered voluntary buyout programs to thousands of US employees, representing about **7%** of its US workforce. Both companies stated these measures are aimed at streamlining operations and offsetting massive expenditures in artificial intelligence. Meta CEO **Mark Zuckerberg** explicitly stated that AI's development is enabling projects that once required large teams to now be completed by a single talented individual.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

This wave of AI has a profoundly far-reaching impact on everyone's life and work.

Firstly, for developers, the launch of **GPT-5.5** means that AI tools have taken another leap forward in automation and complex task processing. Imagine, before you had to painstakingly teach AI how to code step-by-step; now, it might only need a vague idea to plan, execute, and even review code for you, significantly boosting efficiency. And **Google DeepMind**'s **Deep Research** agents will make research much more efficient, eliminating the need to spend vast amounts of time searching and organizing enormous volumes of data. It's like how we used to visit libraries to flip through books for reports; now, with a simple command to AI, it can organize all relevant information and present it to you—how convenient!

Secondly, for businesses, this marks a massive transformation. From manufacturing to logistics, from retail to autonomous driving, AI agents and edge AI are permeating every industry. For instance, **SAP** showcased new agentic AI functionalities at the **2026 Hannover Messe**, designed to enhance manufacturing agility and supply chain resilience. This means factory robots can not only identify issues themselves but also coordinate with each other and even automatically adjust production plans within the supply chain.

> AI agents in the supply chain can reduce response times by **25%** and manual intervention by **30%**.

However, this also brings challenges. The layoff waves at **Meta** and **Microsoft** clearly illustrate AI's impact on existing work models. While some layoffs might be for optimizing company structure, the productivity gains brought by AI do render certain positions less necessary. This serves as a warning to each of us: learning to collaborate with AI, becoming an expert who "dances with AI" rather than a bystander replaced by it, is becoming increasingly crucial.

<Callout type="info" title="Personal Survival Rules Transformed">
  Facing the rapid development of AI, we can no longer passively receive information; instead, we need to actively learn how to harness these tools. Yesterday's "iron rice bowl" might no longer be secure, but a "golden rice bowl" awaits those who can collaborate with AI and solve complex problems.

Finally, the rapid iteration of AI tools has also sparked "Fear Of Missing Out" (FOMO). **Anthropic**'s **Claude Code** Product Lead, **Cat Wu**, pointed out that the AI industry's update speed is so fast that it causes user anxiety, making them feel compelled to constantly check social media to keep up with the latest features. It's like daily app updates on your phone; you click for fear of missing some black tech. This phenomenon prompts the industry to reflect that future AI product design must prioritize "intuitive guidance" and "stress reduction," allowing users to easily get started without becoming tech experts.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From a seasoned investigative journalist's perspective, what I see behind all this is an epic "**ecosystem battle**" among tech giants. Google's **$40 billion** investment in Anthropic is not merely a financial one but a profound strategic maneuver. It not only ensures **Anthropic** continues to use **Google Cloud** infrastructure and **TPU chips** but, more importantly, it allows Google to draw Anthropic into its own AI ecosystem, preventing talent and technology leakage, while competing with **OpenAI** and **Microsoft**. This is the essence of being "frenemies"—collaborating amidst competition to ensure a strong hand on multiple fronts.

> "Organizations globally are demanding uncompromising control over their AI stack." - **Aidan Gomez**, Cohere Co-founder & CEO

**Cohere**'s acquisition of **Aleph Alpha** is a very clear signal for "**sovereign AI**." European and Canadian emphasis on AI data sovereignty is increasing, as they do not want their sensitive data entirely controlled by US tech giants. This deal perfectly provides a transatlantic solution, offering governments and regulated industries a trustworthy alternative to maintain data control. This is not just a commercial consideration but, at a deeper level, a manifestation of national strategy and geopolitics.

Furthermore, while some of **Meta** and **Microsoft**'s layoffs might be attributed to overexpansion during the "pandemic boom," the more direct catalyst is their "**all-in**" strategy on AI. When a company decides to invest tens or even hundreds of billions of dollars in AI infrastructure (Meta plans to invest **$600 billion** in AI infrastructure by 2028), it naturally exercises strict cost control over other departments. This reflects a new corporate operating model: AI is not just a product but also a tool for cost optimization, capable of replacing some human labor to achieve a leaner, more efficient organizational structure.

I've also observed that **DeepSeek** is initiating a "**price war**" in AI models, with its DeepSeek-V4-Pro model's API input price being only **0.25 RMB** per million tokens, making it over **700 times cheaper** than GPT-5.5 Pro. This could significantly impact the popularization of AI services. When fundamental AI services become affordable enough, more SMEs and developers can access them, stimulating even more innovative applications. This is akin to how the widespread adoption of cloud computing drastically lowered the barrier to entry for startups.

Overall, today's AI news paints a vibrant, intensely competitive, and rapidly changing picture. Technological breakthroughs, capital flows, industry restructuring, and profound impacts on human society—all these warrant our continued attention.

### One-Liner Summary

In the AI world, it's not just about who runs fastest, but who can stand firm, and in this "Great AI Migration," only those who can adapt, innovate, and collaborate will emerge as the ultimate winners!
