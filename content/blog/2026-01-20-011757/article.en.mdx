### Simply Put, What Is This?
If we compare AI to a super-intelligent brain, in the past we could only communicate with it through text or voice, much like commanding a behind-the-scenes strategist via messages. Now, this brain doesn't just think; it's started to grow "bodies" and "limbs," capable of sensing, judging, and even "getting things done" in the real world!

"**Physical AI**" is the core of this concept. Unlike traditional robots that only execute pre-programmed instructions, Physical AI can learn, adapt, and complete tasks in complex and dynamic environments, much like humans. You can imagine it as a new driver who just learned how to operate a car; initially, they might need your guidance, but with accumulated experience, they can judge road conditions, avoid obstacles, and even plan the optimal route themselves. This is undoubtedly a revolutionary change for our lives and industries!

**1. OpenAI's "Gumdrop" Arrives, Hardware Race Heats Up!**
That's right, the **OpenAI** we know isn't just the driving force behind the chatbot **ChatGPT** anymore; they are preparing to launch their first hardware device, "**Gumdrop**," in **H2 2026**, exclusively manufactured by Taiwan's electronics manufacturing giant **Foxconn** [cite: OpenAI news, summary]. **2. AI Infrastructure Investment Boom, Chip Giants Continue to Lead**
In the AI war, underlying "**computing power**" remains a fiercely contested battleground. **2026** continues to see massive investment pouring into AI infrastructure. Chip titans like **NVIDIA**, **Broadcom**, and **AMD** are intensifying their competition in AI computing hardware (such as GPUs, ASICs). **NVIDIA's GPU** production is already in short supply, with a market capitalization reaching **$4.5 trillion**. Meanwhile, cloud service providers like **Google Cloud** and **AWS** are critical players in AI computing power leasing, attracting substantial investment. **Google Cloud's** revenue year-on-year growth reached **34%** in Q3 2025, and **AWS** achieved **20%**. These figures demonstrate the strong demand for cloud services driven by AI. Major cloud providers such as Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta are projected to invest an additional **$511 billion** in 2026.

**4. Major Leap in "Physical AI" and Robotics Technology**
AI doesn't just write articles or draw pictures; now it's going to "**do things itself**"! The concept of "**Physical AI**" is gradually maturing, meaning AI is no longer limited to generating language or analyzing data but can perceive, judge, and execute actions in the real world. As **NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang** predicted at CES 2026: "**The ChatGPT moment for robotics has arrived!**"

From autonomous driving and drones to autonomous robots in factories, and even humanoid robots, all will be crucial applications of Physical AI. For example, **NVIDIA** introduced the **Rubin** architecture and the **Isaac GR00T N1.6** model, specifically designed for humanoid robots, combining visual-language-action (VLA) models to enable robots to control their bodies more smoothly and understand complex situations. Barclays Bank predicts that the humanoid robot market size will rapidly expand to **$25 billion** by **2030**, and in the most optimistic scenario, could reach **$200 billion** by 2035.

**5. Practical Value of Specialized AI in Manufacturing**
In the manufacturing sector, AI applications are moving beyond general discourse towards in-depth development of "**specialized AI**." These AIs, designed for specific industry pain points, show significantly greater effectiveness than general-purpose AI in addressing manufacturing's unique reliance on experience and intuition, optimizing blueprint understanding, and enhancing professionalism.

For instance, specialized AI can be applied to single processes like **welding**, **polishing**, **inspection**, and **assembly**. These "out-of-the-box" solutions can deliver significant benefits from day one. Additionally, AI applications in manufacturing include:
*   **Predictive Maintenance**: AI models can predict when machines need servicing, reducing downtime by **30% to 50%**.
*   **Quality Improvement**: Deep learning systems can detect tiny defects imperceptible to the human eye, enhancing product quality.
*   **Automated Production Scheduling**: It is projected that by 2026, **over 40%** of manufacturers will upgrade their production scheduling systems to be AI-driven.

This demonstrates the immense value of deep AI application in vertical sectors and indicates that future manufacturing will increasingly rely on smart automation.

**6. Google Gemini's "Personal Intelligence" Upgrade, Your Private Brain Goes Live!**
Google's **Gemini** model continues to evolve! Today's (January 20, 2026) latest development is the launch of the "**Answer now**" button, allowing you to get answers to simple questions faster. And last week (January 14), **Gemini** further introduced its "**Personal Intelligence**" feature.

The most powerful aspect of this feature is its ability to securely connect with your personal application data from **Gmail**, **Google Photos**, **Google Search**, and **YouTube** to provide more customized and precise answers. Imagine asking **Gemini** your tire size; it won't just tell you generic dimensions, but will pull up your car model from your **Gmail** purchase receipt, combine it with the route from your travel photos in **Google Photos**, and then suggest the most suitable tire type for you. This deeply personalized service is truly like having your "private brain" online at all times!

<Callout type="info" title="Privacy Considerations for Personal Intelligence Assistant">
**Google** emphasizes that the **Personal Intelligence** feature is off by default and must be manually enabled. It will not directly use your private **Gmail** or **Google Photos** data to train its global AI models. It will only "contextually package" data relevant to specific queries, and users can manage or disconnect connections at any time.
</Callout>
