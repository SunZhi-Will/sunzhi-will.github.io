### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine you have a smart butler at home. Before, you had to tell it "turn on the light," and it would; "play music," and it would. You had to give instructions step by step.

Traditional AI is like that **"step-by-step instruction"** butler.

But now, the new generation of "**Agentic AI**" is like this butler suddenly gaining insight. You just tell it, "**I want to throw a party**," and it will plan everything itself: list guests, send invitations, order food, arrange music, and even remind you to tidy up before the party. It will decide "what to do first, then what," and even adjust the plan based on actual circumstances (e.g., a friend didn't RSVP). You no longer need to teach it every little detail; it can handle things on its own!

Isn't this autonomy great? However, for this butler to become so smart, its brain – the AI model and the hardware it runs on – needs an enormous amount of "**memory**" to store and process information. It's like if you want your butler to remember all your friends' preferences, all restaurant menus, and all music libraries; its brain capacity must be huge, and its reaction speed must be fast. This has led to an explosion in demand for high-speed memory chips like **HBM4**, with hardware manufacturers scrambling to produce them, causing significant supply chain tension.

As for **Google** pouring money into **Anthropic**, it's like two top chefs realizing that the other has a secret recipe for a better dish. Instead of researching from scratch, it's better to directly invest in the other, so everyone can enjoy good food, and they can get a share too. At the same time, it ensures Anthropic's Claude model runs on its cloud platform, which is a very clever cloud service strategy.

Regarding China's **DeepSeek V4** paired with **Huawei Ascend chips**, this is akin to a national-level chef who was banned from using certain top-tier ingredients and imported stoves. As a result, they developed their own domestic ingredients and stoves, and the dishes produced are not inferior to those of international celebrity chefs, even having a cost advantage. This is telling the U.S. chip embargo, "We can do it ourselves!"

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

Today's AI headlines are undoubtedly dominated by the "arms race" of funds and technology among tech giants:

*   **Google's Massive Investment in Anthropic Kicks Off New AI Battleground**:
    According to reports, **Google** is preparing to invest **up to $40 billion** in AI startup **Anthropic**. This deal includes a **$10 billion** cash investment, valuing Anthropic at **$350 billion**. The additional **$30 billion** is contingent on undisclosed performance targets. This investment not only expands Google's long-standing partnership with Anthropic but will also provide Anthropic with up to **5 gigawatts** of computing power over the next five years, including up to **1 million** seventh-generation **Ironwood TPU** chips. This brings Google's total commitment to Anthropic to approximately **$43 billion**, making it the largest single investment in a competitor in AI history. This move is interpreted as Google acknowledging that its own **Gemini** model alone is not yet sufficient to achieve overall leadership in the enterprise market. Anthropic's **Claude** model holds a **32%** share in the enterprise large language model API market, surpassing OpenAI's **GPT-4o (25%)**. Previously, **Amazon** also committed to investing up to **$33 billion** in Anthropic, demonstrating that both cloud giants are vying to secure this AI rising star.

*   **China's DeepSeek V4 Teams Up with Huawei Ascend Chips, Breaking Through Tech Blockade**:
    Chinese AI startup **DeepSeek** officially released a preview version of its latest-generation **V4 model** on **April 24th**. The focus of this release is **DeepSeek V4-Pro** (approximately **1.6 trillion** parameters, **49 billion** active parameters) and **DeepSeek V4-Flash** (**284 billion** parameters, **13 billion** active parameters). Most notably, these models have been **fully optimized specifically for Huawei Ascend AI chip platforms** and are the first first-tier large language models (LLMs) to achieve over **85% utilization** with Chinese AI chips.

    DeepSeek worked closely with the Huawei team to directly optimize the inference core of the Ascend **910B/950** chips. Reports indicate its inference quality is comparable to **Nvidia A100**, but with hardware costs **reduced by about 40%**. This is profoundly significant in responding to U.S. chip export bans. The V4 model series leads in Agent capabilities, logical reasoning, and world knowledge within the open-source domain. **V4-Pro** surpasses all other open-source systems in math, coding, and reasoning benchmarks, trailing only Google's closed-source model **Gemini 3.1-Pro**. Additionally, DeepSeek V4 adopts an innovative **Engram architecture**, storing static knowledge in CPU RAM and dynamic inference on the GPU. This leverages CPU RAM being **10-20 times cheaper** than GPU HBM, achieving default support for an ultra-long context of **1 million tokens**.

*   **U.S.-China AI Competition Intensifies with IP Protection Concerns**:
    Just as DeepSeek V4 was released, the U.S. government issued another stern warning to China. The White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) accused Chinese entities, primarily from China, of engaging in "**industrial-scale**" activities to extract capabilities from leading U.S. AI models through "**distillation**" techniques, **stealing U.S. expertise and innovation**. These "model extraction attacks" are seen as the latest front in China's economic coercion and theft of U.S. intellectual property. OSTP Director **Michael Kratsios** stated that these coordinated actions use tens of thousands of proxy accounts and jailbreaking techniques to evade detection, systematically extracting capabilities from U.S. AI models, enabling the launch of products with comparable performance at significantly lower costs, even if the original models cannot be fully replicated. This has prompted the U.S. Congress to consider legislation to place entities utilizing such techniques on an export blacklist. The Chinese Foreign Ministry responded by stating that the U.S. accusations are baseless and an attempt to discredit the achievements of China's AI industry.

    Interestingly, **Anthropic's Claude** model appears to be a primary target for these unauthorized users attempting to steal U.S. AI models, with reports showing tens of thousands of transactions and thousands of duplicate purchases on its platform at a fraction of the official price.

*   **The Rise of Agentic AI and Autonomous Systems**:
    The AI field is transitioning from passive chatbots to "**Agentic AI**" or autonomous systems capable of self-planning, decision-making, and executing multi-step tasks. These systems no longer require step-by-step human instructions but can receive a high-level goal, then autonomously figure out how to achieve it, coordinating work across multiple applications and tools. For example, **Adobe** is transforming its **Experience Cloud** into an **Agentic AI**-centric platform, introducing "**Coworkers**" – persistent AI agents that coordinate tasks across systems. **OpenAI** also released **GPT-5.5**, a significant upgrade that transforms the model from a passive language model into an active, autonomous system capable of planning, executing, and refining complex tasks.

*   **AI Memory and Infrastructure Challenges**:
    With the rise of **Agentic AI** and other large AI models, the demand for **memory chips**, particularly **High Bandwidth Memory (HBM4)**, is growing explosively, leading to supply chain bottlenecks. Analyst **Rihard Jarc's** analysis shows that Anthropic's AI coding agent consumes as much as **93 to 129GB** of memory during active sessions, and even in an idle state, each process still uses about **15GB**. This means a single agent at peak usage could occupy approximately **40% of a 288GB HBM4 memory stack**.

*   **New AI Models and Performance Breakthroughs**:
    Beyond **DeepSeek V4** and **OpenAI GPT-5.5** mentioned above, the AI model war continues to heat up. **Anthropic** is rumored to be developing a **10 trillion-parameter Claude Mythos** model, which has demonstrated unprecedented vulnerability detection and exploitation capabilities in cybersecurity, even autonomously identifying thousands of previously unknown, high-risk software vulnerabilities.

    To address AI's memory demands, **Google Research** launched a study called **TurboQuant** on **March 26th**, aimed at optimizing KV cache compression during inference. This technology can compress the **KV cache** in large language models (LLM) temporary memory to a **3-bit level**, **reducing memory usage by at least six times** without sacrificing accuracy. This is crucial for easing memory pressure on large models, improving long-context efficiency, and lowering inference costs.

*   **AI Market Growth and Investment**:
    The entire AI industry is experiencing an unprecedented investment boom. In addition to **Google's massive investment in Anthropic**, **Broadcom's** AI chip sales soared **65%** to **$20 billion** in **FY2025**, accounting for **31%** of its total revenue. By **FY2027**, its AI chip sales are projected to reach **$60 billion to $90 billion**, potentially even exceeding **$100 billion**. This demonstrates the strong growth in the AI chip market. Furthermore, **Tesla** also plans to invest **$25 billion** in AI and robotics.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a senior investigative journalist, I believe today's AI news reveals several key industry trends and business logics.

First, **Google's massive investment in Anthropic is far more than a financial transaction; it's a "cloud strategic defense" and "AI model ecosystem" play**. While Google has its own Gemini, Anthropic's Claude performs strongly in the enterprise market, especially in specialized areas like coding. Google's investment not only ensures that Anthropic will heavily use its cloud infrastructure and TPU chips but also indirectly participates in Claude's success. This is the ultimate embodiment of a "**co-opetition**" relationship: rather than letting a competitor dominate, it's better to become a catalyst for its growth and profit from it. This also reflects the extremely high cost of AI model development, where even Google needs to diversify risks and form alliances.

The U.S. chip embargo against China was intended to slow down China's AI development, but it has accelerated the establishment of China's autonomous ecosystem. DeepSeek V4's demonstrated performance and cost advantages, particularly its deep optimization for Huawei chips, prove China's strength in software-hardware synergy. The emergence of such a "**parallel AI stack**" could change the global AI infrastructure landscape. In the future, we might see two or more different AI technical standards and ecosystems coexisting, which will have profound implications for global technical cooperation and standardization.

Third, **the rise of Agentic AI signifies that AI applications will transform from "tools" into "partners"**. Traditional AI models require our explicit instructions, much like telling a tool what you want it to do. But Agentic AI can understand your goals and proactively execute tasks. This will not only significantly boost individual and enterprise efficiency but also foster entirely new business models, such as "digital-worker-as-a-service." However, this autonomy also brings new challenges: how to ensure AI acts in alignment with human values when performing tasks? How to regulate its behavior to prevent "AI run wild"? This will be a key focus of AI governance in the coming years.

Finally, **the "memory hog" effect of memory chips and the emergence of Google TurboQuant highlight that AI infrastructure innovation is the lifeline of AI development**. The demand for high-bandwidth memory like HBM4 by large language models and Agentic AI has become a bottleneck for industry growth. This is not only an opportunity for chip manufacturers but also drives innovation at the software and algorithm levels, like Google TurboQuant. If memory usage can be effectively compressed, larger models can run on limited hardware resources, further reducing costs and accelerating AI adoption.
