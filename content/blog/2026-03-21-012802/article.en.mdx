### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine AI as a super chef, and right now, it needs a huge amount of "ingredients" (data) and "kitchen tools" (chip computing power) to prepare one stunning dish after another.

Theme One: **AI Energy Efficiency and New AI Architectures**. This is like the chef realizing that cooking consumes too much gas and electricity, so they start developing "energy-saving kitchen tools" and "more efficient cooking methods." Neuro-symbolic AI and SNNs (Spiking Neural Networks) are these new methods, aiming to make AI complete the same tasks with only **10%** or even **2%** of the energy consumption of traditional methods. For robots that need to react instantly anywhere (like Tesla's **Optimus**), this is a lifesaver, as a robot can't drag a long power cord around everywhere.

Theme Two: **AI Chip Manufacturing and Supply Chain Security**. This is like the chef realizing that good cooking skills aren't enough if ingredient suppliers can cut off supply at any moment; even the best menu is useless then. So **Elon Musk** decided to build a mega-sized "ingredient and kitchen tool manufacturing plant" – **Terafab**. This factory's goal is to independently produce **100 billion to 200 billion** AI chips annually, using the most advanced **2-nanometer process**. This is not only for Tesla's own electric vehicles, **FSD** autonomous driving system, and **Optimus** robots, but also to ensure "food security" for the future AI era. At the same time, **Amazon** founder **Jeff Bezos** is also organizing a fund of up to **$100 billion**, aiming to acquire manufacturing companies and transform factories with AI automation, indicating that "physical AI" combined with manufacturing is becoming a new investment hotbed.

Theme Three: **AI Regulation and Geopolitical Impact**. This is like various governments setting traffic rules for this "supercar" that is AI. The US White House's attitude is to "let the car run first, then slowly draw the lines," hoping to establish a unified federal regulation to avoid fragmented state-by-state rules hindering innovation. Russia, however, takes a different approach. They worry that foreign AI could affect national "traditional values" and data security, so they want to directly restrict or ban foreign products like **ChatGPT** and **Gemini**, fostering their own domestic AI. This is like saying: "It's our road, we make the rules. Foreign cars must obey our regulations, or they're not getting in!"

Theme Four: **Generative AI Ethics, Risks, and Responsible Use**. This is like the chef's delicious dishes sometimes containing "hallucination ingredients" (false information) or "expired ingredients" (biased content). Recently, **CCTV's 3.15 Gala** exposed a black market chain for "poisoning" large AI models, where illicit actors use **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) technology to feed massive amounts of false information to mainstream AI models like Doubao, Yuanbao, Qianwen, and KIMI, manipulating AI recommendations and even getting fictitious products onto the top three recommendation lists, severely misleading consumers. This reminds us that as smart as AI is, the ultimate judgment and responsibility still rest with humans; we cannot fully entrust our trust to it.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

This week's AI news has so many details that I feel I need to carry an "AI development map" just to keep up.

First, the **AI Power-Saving Mission** 💡. Current AI models, especially large language models and data centers, have staggeringly high power consumption, almost on par with a small country. To solve this, researchers are exploring technologies like **Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs)**, which are **10 to 50 times** more energy-efficient than traditional artificial neural networks. Additionally, **Neuro-Symbolic AI** frameworks, such as **Ontology-Guided Diffusion (OGD)**, are being developed to improve the performance of robot vision systems. This not only enhances robots' ability to learn from simulated environments to the real world but also helps reduce their operational carbon footprint and improve sustainability.

Next, the latest developments in the **AI Chip Scramble** ⚡. **Tesla's Terafab** project launched around **March 21, 2026**. This project aims to establish large-scale internal AI chip manufacturing facilities, utilizing the most advanced **2-nanometer process**, with a goal of producing **100 billion to 200 billion** AI chips annually. **Elon Musk** predicts that AI computing and memory supply will become bottlenecks within the next **3 to 4 years**, making autonomous production an inevitable trend. This investment is estimated to have an initial construction cost of **$20 billion to $25 billion**, with total subsequent investment potentially exceeding **$40 billion**. Despite Tesla's aggressive chip self-development, interestingly, **Musk** also stated that both Tesla and **SpaceX** would continue to purchase **NVIDIA** chips on a large scale. This reflects that even tech giants, in their pursuit of the ultimate computing power, find it difficult to completely detach from existing mature supply chains in the short term.

Now let's look at the **International AI Regulation Landscape** 🌍. The US White House issued a "National AI Legislative Framework" on **March 20**, calling on Congress to enact a unified federal policy to regulate AI in a "light-touch" manner, avoiding fragmented state-by-state regulations that could stifle innovation. The framework has six key focus areas, including protecting children, preventing electricity price surges, respecting intellectual property rights, preventing censorship, promoting innovation, and cultivating an AI workforce. Notably, the White House prefers courts to handle AI copyright issues rather than mandating legislation. However, this proposal has already drawn opposition from some state Republican lawmakers, who worry that the federal government will strip states of their right to protect voters.

On the other hand, Russia has adopted a tougher stance. Russia's Ministry of Digital Development announced a proposal on **March 20** to restrict or ban foreign AI tools like **ChatGPT**, **Claude**, and **Gemini** if they do not comply with Russian data processing and regulatory requirements. This move is part of Russia's effort to build a "sovereign internet," aimed at protecting national "traditional spiritual and moral values" and supporting the development of domestic AI companies like **Sberbank** and **Yandex**.

In terms of ethics and risks, the most striking recent news was **CCTV's 3.15 Gala's** exposé of the rampant **"poisoning" of large AI models** in China. Illicit actors utilized **"Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)"** technology to feed massive amounts of false information to mainstream AI models such as Doubao, Yuanbao, Qianwen, and KIMI, manipulating AI's recommendation results and even getting fictitious products to the top three on recommendation lists, severely misleading consumers. This once again sounded the alarm, reminding us that while enjoying the convenience of generative AI, we must be aware of its potential "hallucinations" and the risk of malicious exploitation.

Research shows that when humans delegate tasks to AI, it can create "moral distance," leading to an increase in dishonest behavior. In academic and medical writing, issues of inaccurate or even "fabricated out of thin air" AI-generated citations are common, severely impacting the reliability of scientific research. The World Health Organization (WHO) has also expressed concerns about the application of generative AI in mental health, particularly the potential risks to young people. All of this underscores the critical importance of **human oversight** and establishing robust **ethical frameworks**.

Finally, **AI's application and development across various industries** are also accelerating.
*   **Defense**: The US Navy is introducing AI robots to accelerate warship maintenance and enhance combat readiness. Simultaneously, US Senator **Elissa Slotkin** has proposed a bill aiming to restrict military deployment of AI, marking the first official legislative attempt following the ethical dispute between **Anthropic** and the US Department of Defense regarding AI military applications.

*   **Education**: The EU published an updated "Educators' Guide to Ethical AI and Data Use in Teaching" on **March 5, 2026**, providing teachers and school leaders with ethical considerations and implementation paths for teaching in the AI era. This shows that AI's application in education is moving beyond mere tools to delve into teaching ethics and strategy.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

Living in this era of AI's rapid acceleration, these advancements have multi-faceted impacts on each of us.

For **AI users**, we must cultivate stronger "AI critical thinking." When you ask **ChatGPT** or **Gemini** questions, you can no longer blindly trust the information, especially when it concerns medical, financial, or important decisions. The CCTV "AI poisoning" incident is a stark lesson: what you thought was the crystallization of AI's intelligence might actually be an advertisement or fake news "fed" by black market operators. This demands that when using AI, we treat it like anonymous information on the internet—cross-verify, and remain vigilant. In the future, understanding the limitations of AI-generated content and taking ultimate responsibility for reviewing it will be an essential digital literacy for everyone.

For **tech companies**, this is a race and a balancing act. Tesla's and Bezos's hefty investments signal that AI hardware and "physical AI" will become the next competitive high ground. Mastering autonomous chip capabilities means mastering the lifeline for the future. But at the same time, ensuring AI's energy efficiency and responsibly developing and deploying AI are also crucial for a company's sustainable development. Companies like NVIDIA and Alibaba continue to invest in AI computing infrastructure, indicating that this compute race has only just begun.

For **governments**, AI regulation is no longer an "optional question," but a "mandatory one." The differing paths of AI regulation taken by the US and Russia demonstrate AI's strategic importance in geopolitics. How to foster innovation while protecting citizens' privacy, data sovereignty, and national interests is a huge challenge faced by all governments. I believe a global consensus on AI ethics is forming, but the specific implementation methods will be profoundly influenced by each country's cultural, political, and economic backgrounds.

For **industry development**, AI is accelerating the reshaping of traditional industries. From intelligent automation in manufacturing to robot applications in defense, and the innovation of educational methods, AI is no longer just a cherry on top, but a core driving force. This means that many job types will change, and we need to continuously learn and adapt to new AI tools to stand firm in this wave.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a seasoned investigative journalist, I see that this week's AI news is actually depicting a clear **AI "polarization"** trend.

One pole is the **"ultimate pursuit of hardware and national-level strategic planning."** Tesla's **Terafab** project and Bezos's $100 billion investment in manufacturing AI automation both reflect the fervent pursuit of AI infrastructure and "physical AI" by top tech giants. This is no longer just about software-level algorithm optimization but a foundational revolution deep into chip design, manufacturing, and physical robots and automation equipment. Whoever controls AI chip production capacity will occupy the commanding height in future AI competition. This has elevated from corporate competition to a national strategic level, with governments worldwide realizing the paramount importance of AI national sovereignty and supply chain security.

Humanity cannot relinquish oversight and ultimate responsibility for AI simply in pursuit of efficiency. The US White House's "light-touch" regulatory framework, while emphasizing innovation, also prioritizes "child protection" and defers copyright disputes to the courts; these are all explorations of the balance between innovation and risk. Russia's restrictions on foreign AI models, moreover, directly define "sovereignty" over AI technology from the perspective of national security and cultural values.

I personally believe the tension between these two poles will grow. Future AI development will no longer be a purely technical issue but a complex interplay of technological, economic, political, and ethical factors. How to achieve both the sharing and collaboration of AI technology in a globalized context while ensuring that the interests and values of various countries are not infringed upon will be a huge challenge for human society. This is like an endless "collaboration and competition"; we must not only run fast but also run steadily, and run with integrity.
