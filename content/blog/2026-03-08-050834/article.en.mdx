### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let me talk about the striking "AI ethics battle" between the **Pentagon** and **Anthropic**.

The U.S. Department of Defense has officially designated **Anthropic** as a "supply chain risk" vendor. Sounds a bit serious, doesn't it? Simply put, the military believes that **Anthropic's Claude AI** model doesn't meet their requirements in certain safety regulations, especially for military applications, and thus prohibits its use in new defense contracts. The catalyst for this dispute is **Anthropic's** insistence on two red lines: its AI model cannot be used for "mass surveillance of U.S. citizens" and "fully autonomous weapons."

According to reports, this conflict has been ongoing for several months, as **Anthropic** is unwilling to loosen its AI model's safety guardrails. Interestingly, despite the DoD's ban, major tech giants like **Google**, **Amazon Web Services (AWS)**, and **Microsoft** have all stated they will continue to offer **Anthropic's Claude AI** model to commercial clients, indicating they view this ban as limited to defense-related applications. Even U.S. President **Donald Trump** ordered federal agencies to immediately stop using **Claude**, but the DoD has **six months** to phase it out, as the product is deeply embedded in their classified military systems. In contrast, other major AI labs, such as **OpenAI**, **Google**, and **xAI**, have agreed to relax safety protections in the Pentagon's unclassified systems.

Of course, the rapid development of AI also brings impacts on the job market. ### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a tech storyteller, today's AI news reveals several deep-seated trends.

Firstly, the **"physicalization" of AI is accelerating**. In the past, when we talked about AI, it was mostly about software models and algorithms. But now, from humanoid robots (though not specifically mentioned in today's news, it's a major trend) to smart glasses, AI is moving from the virtual to the physical, from the cloud to edge devices all around us. **Qualcomm's** CFO even stated frankly that they have a "significant structural advantage" over **Nvidia** in the **"edge AI market."** He believes that while Nvidia dominates the data center AI training market, the actual inference of AI models will occur on billions of devices, making it the largest market in semiconductor history. This explains why Qualcomm is aggressively pushing chips like the Snapdragon Wear Elite—it's designed to make AI run fast and efficiently directly on your device.
