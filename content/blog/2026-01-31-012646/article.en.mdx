### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
Today's AI news is like a steaming pot of rich and flavorful stew, with plenty of good stuff!

In the consumer market, **Tuya Smart** truly stole the show at the Spielwarenmesse 2026 in Nuremberg, Germany. They showcased a range of AI products, with the most eye-catching being the **"NebulaPals Plush AI Toy."** This toy isn't just talkative; it can also make real-time LED facial expressions based on your emotions! Imagine, whether you're happy or sad, its eyes change accordingly. Plus, it has "long-term memory" and "emotional companionship" capabilities, able to tell stories, play news, and engage in games.

There's also **"Walulu,"** a personalized companion robot that supports over **60 languages** and seamlessly integrates mainstream large language models like **ChatGPT**, **Gemini**, **DeepSeek**, **Qwen**, and **Doubao**. Users can set its personality to be lively, calm, curious, and more, creating a unique "digital pet community." Meanwhile, the Photo Dance application has upgraded its **"Magic AI Video"** feature, allowing you to transform a static photo into a smooth, realistic dynamic dance video by combining it with the movements and expressions from a reference video. The impressive part of this technology is its use of a "temporal consistency algorithm" to eliminate frame skipping and artifacts, enabling ordinary people to easily create professional-grade AI dance videos. Currently, this application has accumulated over **1.2 million downloads** globally and generated over **1.8 million AI dance videos**.

Concurrently, market research firm **TrendForce** released a significant report, predicting that global AI server shipments will grow by over **28%** year-on-year in 2026. The capital expenditures of the five major North American CSPs (**Google**, **AWS**, **Meta**, **Microsoft**, and **Oracle**) are projected to increase by **40%** in 2026.

By simulating real chemical reactions, it accelerates drug discovery, particularly for cancer and infectious diseases, potentially significantly reducing drug development time and costs.

However, AI has also sparked ethical discussions in content creation. The Grammy Awards have explicitly stipulated that only **human-created** works are eligible for awards. If a work contains absolutely no human creative input, it does not qualify for any award. However, if a work includes AI elements but the human creative contribution is "meaningful and important," it can still be considered. This is like saying AI can be your band's musician, but the lead vocalist and songwriter still need to be human.

Finally, while workplace AI adoption continues to grow, its path to widespread use remains long. A Gallup report indicates that by Q3 2025, nearly half of US employees (approximately **45%**) use AI tools at work at least annually, with weekly AI usage increasing from **19%** in Q2 2025 to **23%**, and daily usage rising from **8%** to **10%**. However, only **9%** of employees reported feeling "very comfortable" using AI at work. This reveals a "comfort deficit" and a "strategic vacuum," as many companies have yet to clearly communicate their AI implementation strategies to employees.

Furthermore, an Anthropic study found that while AI-assisted programming might slightly increase task completion speed, it could negatively impact skill mastery. Programmers using AI assistance scored an average of **17%** lower on tests for conceptual understanding, code reading, and debugging abilities. This reminds us that while AI is a powerful tool, if not guided, it may affect the development of core human skills.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
For us ordinary people, AI is becoming increasingly "humanized" and "integrated into daily life." Previously, AI felt like cold, hard technology, but now it can be a plush toy that understands your emotions, or a magical app that helps you create stunning dance videos with ease. This means our entertainment options will be more diverse, and AI can even play a role in meeting emotional companionship needs. Of course, this also brings new considerations: To what extent should we form "emotional connections" with AI? Will we become overly reliant?

For enterprises, AI is no longer a "future trend" but a "present reality." Whoever can deploy AI infrastructure faster, more securely, and more stably will gain a market lead. This is why companies like **Tata Communications** are working tirelessly to build AI-dedicated "digital highways." My observation is that AI is becoming the "new electricity" for businesses; whoever masters the ability to generate and transmit this power will light up more commercial applications.

In the workplace, AI's impact is a double-edged sword. On one hand, it can boost efficiency and handle repetitive tasks; on the other hand, as Anthropic's research shows, over-reliance on AI might make you "forget how to think" or "lose the motivation to learn new skills." Therefore, learning how to collaborate with AI and using it as a tool to enhance your capabilities, rather than a replacement, will be a crucial skill for all of us in the future workplace.

### Insider's Deep Analysis
From today's news, I see a very clear trend: **AI is evolving from a "tool" to an "experience" and "infrastructure."**

On the consumer side, the innovations from **Tuya Smart** and **Photo Dance** no longer just emphasize AI's "functionality," but rather the "emotional experience" and "creative joy" it can bring to users. This "emotional AI" and "low-barrier creative AI" are key to AI truly entering mainstream life. Previously, people felt AI was difficult and distant; now, through these products, AI has become accessible, even offering personalized emotional connections. Behind this is immense progress in AI models' emotional recognition, voice interaction, and multimodal generation capabilities.

On the enterprise side, the reports from **Tata Communications** and **TrendForce** reveal that the "AI infrastructure war" is intensifying. As AI applications move from laboratories to production environments, enterprise demand for "AI-ready" networks, cloud, and edge computing capabilities is exploding. Especially **TrendForce's** prediction that **ASIC** chips will challenge **GPU** market share indicates that enterprises and cloud service providers are seeking more specialized, high-performance AI acceleration solutions. This "AI infrastructure arms race" will ultimately determine which companies can deploy and scale AI services faster and more affordably.
