### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine you have a super-smart robot butler at home. It doesn't just make your coffee and clean your room; it plans your schedule, manages your finances, and can even detect issues with your home's electrical system. Impressive, right? But at the same time, you might feel a chill down your spine. What if it uncovers too many system vulnerabilities, or does something it shouldn't? What then?

This is essentially a microcosm of today's AI news. This "robot butler" is an AI model, and the US government now feels that before this butler "leaves the factory," they need to check if it might turn into a "terror butler." Concurrently, the big companies producing these robot butlers are teaming up with "Wall Street titans" to market these robots to all businesses, making them not just handle household chores but also rake in big profits for companies. However, these super butlers are incredibly power-hungry, leading everyone to furiously snatch up electricity and land, almost pushing power grids to their breaking point. And amidst this chaotic melee, one player suddenly overtook another in a key metric, forcing a re-evaluation of the global AI battleground.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let's look at the government's major shift in attitude towards AI. The current US Trump administration is reportedly considering issuing an executive order to establish a **formal pre-release review mechanism** for new AI models. This is a complete reversal, as their previous stance on AI was relatively laissez-faire. Why the change? The main catalyst is a super-powerful AI model developed by **Anthropic** called **Mythos**.

<Callout type="info" title="Mythos: The Rise of Hacker-Grade AI">
  Anthropic's Mythos model has demonstrated astonishing capabilities, autonomously identifying **thousands** of significant software vulnerabilities in mainstream operating systems and web browsers. Due to its potential risks, Anthropic has decided not to publicly release this model for now. Experts warn that Mythos could identify and exploit these weaknesses faster than companies can patch them, posing a particular threat to industries like finance that rely on complex, interconnected systems. The US government even plans to provide a version of Mythos to major federal agencies.
</Callout>

This is like your "butler AI" suddenly becoming a "hacker AI"—of course, the government would be nervous! Reports indicate that Anthropic has made **Mythos** available only to a few tech giants and critical software infrastructure organizations for testing through "Project Glasswing." The UK's AI Safety Institute (AISI) also assessed that Mythos's capabilities in cyberattacks are "stronger than any model we have previously evaluated."

This also brings up the security concerns surrounding "AI Agents (Agent AI)." Cybersecurity agencies from the US, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK have jointly issued a guide, urging organizations to treat automated AI systems as a core cybersecurity issue, as these agent AIs are already deployed in critical infrastructure but lack sufficient security safeguards. The guide emphasizes that agent AIs should be integrated into existing cybersecurity frameworks, such as "zero trust" and "least privilege" principles, and particular attention should be paid to risks like **privilege abuse** and **prompt injection**.

Regarding enterprise AI applications, two major AI giants, **OpenAI** and **Anthropic**, are teaming up with Wall Street financial institutions to accelerate the integration of AI across various industries. OpenAI has established a joint venture called "The Deployment Company," which has already raised over **$4 billion** and is valued at **$10 billion**. Their partners include renowned investment firms such as **TPG Inc.** and **Brookfield Asset Management**. Anthropic is following suit, collaborating with companies like **Blackstone Inc.**, **Hellman & Friedman**, and **Goldman Sachs Group Inc.**, aiming to deploy its flagship **Claude AI** system into the core operations of mid-sized enterprises, especially in financial services and healthcare. This demonstrates that AI is no longer just a game for tech companies; financial giants are also "splashing big cash" to accelerate its commercial deployment.

Next, let's discuss AI infrastructure and the compute arms race, which is nothing short of a "power gold rush." To secure future compute supply, **OpenAI** has already signed contracts for a total of **10 gigawatts** of AI computing capacity, several years ahead of their original **2029** target. This massive investment includes the "Stargate" partnership with **Oracle** and **SoftBank** (up to **4.5 gigawatts**), as well as collaboration with **Nvidia** (potentially involving up to **$100 billion** in investment to acquire **10 gigawatts** of data center systems).

Not only that, **Microsoft** also added approximately **1 gigawatt** of data center capacity in the most recent quarter, with quarterly revenue reaching **$82.9 billion**, and plans to double its AI infrastructure scale within two years. Meanwhile, **Google** announced a plan to build its largest AI center outside the US in India. This **$15 billion** investment will create a "gigawatt-scale computing" AI data center ecosystem in Visakhapatnam.

How exaggerated is AI's demand for electricity? A report indicates that an AI-related task can consume **1,000 times** the power of a traditional internet search. The US Department of Energy estimates that data centers accounted for about **4.4%** of total US electricity consumption in 2023, projected to reach **6.7% to 12%** by 2028. Global data center electricity consumption might even exceed **1,000 terawatt-hours (TWh)** in 2026. This unprecedented demand for electricity is even causing the US energy industry to reconsider investments in fossil fuels like natural gas to ensure grid stability and meet the **24/7** operational needs of AI data centers. This undeniably poses a huge challenge to our carbon neutrality goals.

On the geopolitical front, the US-China AI competition is also intensifying. According to the latest data from OpenRouter, for the week of **April 27th to May 3rd**, the total weekly invocation volume of large AI models in China once again surpassed that of the United States. China's large AI models saw an invocation volume of **7.942 trillion tokens**, marking an **81.7%** week-over-week increase. In contrast, US large AI models had an invocation volume of **3.258 trillion tokens**, a **34.6%** week-over-week decrease. This marks the second time China has overtaken the US in two weeks, with **Tencent's Hy3 Preview (free)** ranking first globally with **3.03 trillion tokens** in weekly invocation volume, a stunning **799%** week-over-week surge, while **Kimi K2.6** secured the second spot. This data is no joke; it directly reflects the explosive growth of Chinese AI in global application.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

I think the most direct impact is that AI is no longer just a "little helper" in our work; it's now becoming a "grand steward" influencing every aspect of our lives.

*   **Your work will become more convenient, but also potentially more challenging**: The accelerated deployment of enterprise-level AI means that your daily tasks, such as reports, data analysis, and even code writing, will be completed faster and better with AI's help. **92%** of companies increased their AI spending in 2026, and **87%** of global organizations have applied AI in at least one function.

We need to learn how to collaborate with these more powerful, even autonomous AI agents and understand their safety boundaries.
*   **Your online life will be safer, but also potentially "regulated"**: The government's escalating AI regulation is, of course, aimed at protecting us from AI's potential harms. For example, the joint release of AI agent security guidelines by multiple national cybersecurity agencies is intended to ensure these smart programs don't "go rogue." But this also raises another question: will excessive scrutiny stifle the pace of AI innovation? This will be an ongoing issue requiring careful balance.
*   **Your electricity bill might quietly go up**: Imagine a massive data center being built next door to your home, consuming the equivalent of a small city's electricity every day. As AI data center power consumption grows at an astonishing rate, power companies, to meet demand, either have to spend heavily building new power plants or pass the costs onto ordinary citizens. This is also why data centers are starting to reconsider natural gas power generation, as its connection costs are **over 10 times lower** than solar.
*   **Global AI competition – you and I are witnesses**: China's AI invocation volume surpassing the US is not just a numbers game; it signifies a quiet shift in the global application ecosystem and user habits of AI technology. This competition is not only in technological R&D but also in actual adoption rates and influence.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

What I see behind this is a profound transformation of the AI industry from "research breakthroughs" to "infrastructure-ization."

In recent years, we've marvelled at how intelligent AI models are, capable of writing poetry and painting. But now, the game's focus has shifted to how to massively apply this "intelligence and talent" to the real world, ensuring its stability, security, and sustainability. It's like building a skyscraper: no matter how beautiful the design, if the foundation is unstable, power supply is insufficient, or fire safety inspections are inadequate, it's all just talk.

<Callout type="info" title="AI Infrastructure: The New Moat">
  OpenAI's massive signing of **10 gigawatts** in compute capacity contracts is not just about money; it's a strategic layout. In the age of AI, compute power is the new oil, and stable electricity supply and massive data centers are key for tech giants to build their "moats." Whoever can control sufficient infrastructure will dominate future AI competition. This shift in competition from software capabilities to hardware foundations is a trend every business leader must recognize.
</Callout>

At the same time, regulation and security are no longer just slogans; they are now essential "entry tickets." Dual-use AI models like **Mythos** (capable of both defense and attack) have made governments realize the necessity of intervening in AI models before they "turn bad." This has also prompted the emergence of AI agent governance guidelines. Companies cannot solely pursue the efficiency gains brought by AI; they must also prioritize its security boundaries and responsible deployment.

As for China's overtaking in AI invocation volume, I believe this is a phenomenon worth deep consideration. It might partly reflect China's vast user base and rapid adoption of technology. More importantly, it also foreshadows that future AI development may no longer be dominated by a single culture or technological path but rather a multi-polar landscape of competition and innovation. The importance of domestic AI chip adaptation and self-controllable compute capabilities will only increase under this trend.

### One-Liner Summary

AI is no longer just a "book-smart genius"; it now has to "earn its keep," "abide by laws," and "be self-disciplined," or else its job is on the line, and it might even stir up big trouble! In the future, whoever can deploy AI safely, efficiently, and responsibly will truly command the narrative of this technological revolution.
