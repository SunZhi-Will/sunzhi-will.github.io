### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows 📊

Today's AI news mainly revolves around the following key points:

#### 1. AI Infrastructure and Semiconductor Arm Race Continues to Heat Up 🔥

*   **Amazon Heavily Invests Further in Anthropic**: The battle for AI compute power never ceases. **Amazon** has once again poured significant capital into leading AI startup **Anthropic**. According to reports, **Amazon** will invest an additional **$5 billion** today, with a commitment to potentially increase this to **$20 billion** in the future, building upon their previous **$8 billion** investment. In return, **Anthropic** has pledged to spend over **$100 billion** on **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** technical services over the next **10 years**, including **Amazon's** self-designed **Trainium** and **Graviton** chips, ensuring up to **5 gigawatts (GW)** of computing capacity for training and running its powerful **Claude** models. This also reflects **Anthropic's** explosive business growth, with its annual revenue having surged from approximately **$9 billion** at the end of 2025 to over **$30 billion**.

*   **Google Collaborates with Marvell to Develop AI Inference Chips**: Beyond merely acquiring existing compute power, major tech giants are also actively developing their own AI chips. **Google** is in talks with chip manufacturer **Marvell Technology** with the goal of jointly designing new custom AI chips specifically for "inference" (i.e., running already-trained AI models). These chips aim for "ultra-high efficiency" to reduce the power and hardware costs of running AI models like **Gemini**, and to lessen reliance on **NVIDIA's** expensive GPUs. Reportedly, **Google** plans to produce nearly **2 million** memory processing units, with design expected to be completed next year. This highlights how AI competition has shifted from the software level to a critical battle for hardware and cost-efficiency.

#### 2. AI Applications and Services Are Widely Permeating Life 🌐

*   **Softbank Launches "Dare demo AI" to Lower Barriers**: Japanese telecom giant **Softbank** has launched "Dare demo AI" (AI for Anyone), a service designed to make generative AI more accessible to the general public. The service's key features include no login requirement, no complex prompt input, allowing users to experience popular AI functions like image generation, music creation, and real-time voice translation with just a click of a button. This initiative aims to address the pain point where AI awareness is high (**74%**) but actual usage is low (**57%** reported not using it), especially for users who "don't know how to start" or "aren't sure how to apply it."

Their laser projectors feature "computational visual alignment" and "active obstacle avoidance," capable of automatically detecting and adjusting the display. Interactive Flat Panels (IFPs) integrate their proprietary "Optoma AI" smart platform, offering functions like "Ask AI," "Voice Commands," "AI Drawing," and "Image Search," transforming displays into cognitively capable collaborative partners.
*   **Alibaba Makes Major Foray into Embodied AI and Humanoid Robots**: Chinese e-commerce giant **Alibaba** has set its sights on the "Embodied AI" domain, having already applied for the trademark "Qianwen Xiaojiuwo," covering AI services, chatbot software, and humanoid robots. This marks a strategic shift for **Alibaba** from purely conversational models (like Tongyi Qianwen) to physical interaction interfaces, with related products expected to be unveiled on April 22nd. Their **Amap** maps division has also introduced a quadruped robot named **Tutu**, designed to provide guide services for the visually impaired.

#### 3. AI Safety, Ethics, and Regulatory Challenges Are Increasingly Severe 🛡️

*   **AI "Data Poisoning" Threatens National Security**: China's Ministry of State Security has issued a warning, stating that "AI data poisoning" has become a new threat. Malicious data injected into AI training models not only disrupts market order but also poses systemic harm to national security, data security, and social stability. This attack method is increasingly networked, covert, and cross-border, potentially exploited by hostile foreign forces to fabricate fake news, political rumors, and even influence government and enterprise decision-making data.

<Callout type="info" title="What is 'AI Data Poisoning'?">
Imagine you're training an AI to recognize good and bad apples. If someone intentionally sneaks a bunch of rotten apples into your training data and tells the AI they are "good apples," the result is that the AI will mistake rotten apples for good ones. This is data poisoning: maliciously altering the data an AI learns from, causing it to produce incorrect or harmful behaviors.
</Callout>

*   **Cybersecurity Risks of Autonomous AI Agents**: With the rise of Agentic AI, cybersecurity faces unprecedented challenges. **Google DeepMind's** research has uncovered a new threat called the "AI agent trap," where hidden instructions within webpages are misinterpreted as commands by AI agents. These autonomous AI agents, without sufficient oversight, could inadvertently leak sensitive data or introduce vulnerabilities. According to the 2026 CISO AI Risk Report, **47%** of CISOs have observed unexpected behavior from AI agents, yet only **5%** are confident in their ability to contain a compromised agent. Attackers are also leveraging AI to accelerate network reconnaissance, exploit vulnerabilities, and facilitate lateral movement, such as AI-assisted phishing attacks and polymorphic malware.

*   **AI Search and Copyright Protection Disputes**: The issue of copyright ownership for AI-generated content remains unresolved. The US Supreme Court refused to hear a copyright case involving AI-generated artwork, reaffirming that under current US law, **purely AI-generated works lacking human involvement are not eligible for copyright protection**. Concurrently, the Japan Newspaper Publishers and Editors Association has called on the government to establish regulations for AI search services, expressing concerns that AI services "piggybacking" on news content could infringe copyrights, and noting that under the existing legal framework, rights holders have limited power to refuse content use. The UK government also released its 2026 report on copyright and AI, exploring AI's impact on data access, transparency, and licensing.

#### 4. Tech Giant AI Strategies and Leadership Changes 🍎

*   **John Ternus Appointed Apple CEO, Focusing on AI Transformation**: Tech giant **Apple** has announced that **John Ternus**, currently Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering, will succeed **Tim Cook** as CEO on **September 1, 2026**, with **Cook** transitioning to Executive Chairman. This personnel change is highly significant, with outside observers generally believing that **Ternus** will usher **Apple** into an era of "product and engineering priority," especially in the AI domain. Although **Apple** has lagged behind competitors in AI services, and has partnered with **OpenAI** (ChatGPT) and **Google** (Gemini) to enhance **Siri's** capabilities, Ternus's hardware background suggests **Apple** may emphasize "hardware-driven AI" and "privacy-first AI" more strongly – processing sensitive data on-device, leveraging its powerful proprietary chips and privacy commitment as a differentiating competitive advantage.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a seasoned investigative reporter, today's news actually conceals several deeper industry logics and trends.

Firstly, the **"heavy asset" nature of AI infrastructure** has become extremely clear. **Amazon's** enormous investment in **Anthropic**, and **Anthropic's** reciprocal commitment to spend **$100 billion** on AWS, isn't just a simple equity investment; it's a deep ecosystem binding and compute power lock-in. This signifies that future AI model competition will no longer be merely an algorithmic showdown, but a contest of financial and hardware might. Only companies that own or control vast quantities of advanced chips and data centers will be able to gain an advantage in this "nuclear race" for AI [See also: [The AI World Explodes: From Robot Marathons to Data Center 'Nuclear Races,' Are Tech Giants Ready?](/blog/2026-04-20-020916)].

**Google's collaboration with Marvell to develop inference chips** is another critical signal: **breaking free from NVIDIA's GPU monopoly** is already a consensus among major cloud service providers. While **NVIDIA's** GPUs are powerful, they are expensive and in tight supply. To control costs, optimize performance, and achieve self-sufficiency, every tech giant is embarking on the path of self-developed chips. This is especially true for the "inference" phase, as this is where AI models incur truly massive costs during large-scale deployment. Whoever can minimize inference costs will be able to provide more universally accessible and faster AI services. This is also why **Amazon's** own **Trainium** and **Graviton** chips are being heavily adopted by **Anthropic**.

Secondly, the **"physicalization" and "embodiment" of AI is an inevitable trend**. **Alibaba's** significant bet on "embodied AI" and humanoid robots, demonstrated through its "Qianwen Xiaojiuwo" trademark application, illustrates that AI development is moving from the purely digital world into the physical realm. Previously, AI conversed with you inside a computer; now, AI needs to be able to observe, act, and interact in the real world through a robot's body. This will fundamentally transform multiple industries such as retail, logistics, healthcare, and home services, while also bringing immense business opportunities for hardware suppliers and robot manufacturers.

<Callout type="info" title="What is Embodied AI?">
Embodied AI, simply put, is giving AI a "body." It's no longer just software or a virtual assistant, but through physical vehicles like robots, it can perceive the real world, understand environments, and take action within them. Much like the robot butler in a sci-fi movie, it doesn't just talk to you; it can actually clean your house, pour you water, and hand you things.
</Callout>

Thirdly, the **frontline of AI safety and governance is fully opening up**. China's warning about "data poisoning" and Google DeepMind's revelation of the dangers of the "AI agent trap" both remind us that AI's potential risks are far more complex and profound than we imagine. When AI models and AI agents possess a high degree of autonomy and are deeply connected to networks, traditional cybersecurity defense systems will face immense challenges. This is not merely a technical issue but a massive test on ethical, legal, and national security levels. The regulation and governance of AI by governments and international organizations will be a central topic in the coming years. This also extends our previous discussions on the AI safety model **Claude Mythos** [See also: [AI Unleashes 'Offensive and Defensive' Intelligence! Dangerous Models Alarm Wall Street, Governments Rush New Laws, AI No Longer Just a Docile Assistant?](/blog/2026-04-13-020858) and [AI's Brain Needs to 'Take Care of Home' Too? From Cybersecurity to Nuclear Power, New Challenges for AI Evolution!](/blog/2026-04-12-020430)].

Finally, **Apple's leadership change and AI strategic adjustment** signify that the last tech giant in the AI war is also going all-in. **John Ternus's** appointment as CEO is itself a reaffirmation of **Apple's** "hardware-first" strategy. During the **Tim Cook** era, while **Apple's** market value soared, it appeared relatively conservative in the large AI model domain. Now, Ternus's mission is to integrate more powerful AI capabilities while preserving **Apple's** core privacy advantage, especially in the development of on-device AI. This could mean that future **Apple** products, from iPhones to Vision Pro, will feature deeper AI integration and may rapidly compensate for any deficiencies in cloud-based large models through strategic alliances with partners like **Google**. This transformation will determine **Apple's** market position in the coming AI era.
