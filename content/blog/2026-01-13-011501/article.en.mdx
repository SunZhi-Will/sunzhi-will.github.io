### Simply Put, What Is This?

Let's use some brilliant analogies to turn these seemingly profound tech news stories into easy-to-understand conversations, like chatting with a friend.

*   **Apple Teams Up with Google Gemini AI**: This is like your iPhone's Siri, who used to be a bit clumsy, a dull assistant who just recited information. *   **Artificial Superintelligence (ASI) and Brain-Computer Interface (BCI)**:
    *   **ASI** sounds like science fiction, but you can imagine it as the **AI version of Superman**. Current AI is like a very smart "child prodigy" but still falls short of humans in some areas. ### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

The details of this AI showdown are truly astounding.

First, the **AI partnership between Apple and Google** is a monumental event of the year. Apple and Google issued a joint statement, announcing a **multi-year agreement** to build Apple's next-generation foundational models based on Google's Gemini model and cloud technology. This means that new versions of Siri and future Apple Intelligence features, set to launch later this year, will be powered by Google's AI technology. Apple frankly admitted that, after careful evaluation, they believe Google's AI technology provides the "**most robust foundation**" for Apple's foundational models. According to a Bloomberg report last November, this partnership could be worth approximately **$1 billion (about 1.4 trillion Korean Won)** annually. However, Apple emphasized that all Apple Intelligence operations will occur on Apple devices and within its "**Private Cloud Compute**" system to maintain its long-standing privacy principles. In fact, Siri's update plan had been delayed multiple times, with AI assistant features originally slated for WWDC 2024 postponed until 2026 due to development hurdles, ultimately leading to this collaboration with Google. Analysts, such as **Dan Ives of Wedbush Securities**, hailed this move as "**a major validation moment for Google**" and a "stepping stone" for Apple to get its AI strategy back on track. It's worth noting that Google's AI technology is also a core driver for Samsung's "Galaxy AI."

Next, let's look at the **global AI infrastructure investment boom**. Meta CEO **Mark Zuckerberg** announced the formation of a new infrastructure division, "**Meta Compute**," dedicated to driving its large-scale AI data center construction. Meta plans to deploy tens of gigawatts of power infrastructure by the end of the century, with a long-term goal potentially reaching hundreds of gigawatts. **Santosh Janardhan** will oversee the technical aspects, while **Daniel Gross**, former co-founder of Safe Superintelligence Inc., will lead the newly established **Meta Superintelligence** lab. Meta's capital expenditure for the 2025 fiscal year reached **$72 billion**, with the vast majority allocated to AI data center construction. According to a BlackRock report, AI data centers could consume 15% to 20% of current US electricity demand by 2030, potentially reaching as high as 25%. BlackRock also predicts that AI infrastructure investment will be unprecedented, shifting the global economy from a "light capital" model to a "**capital-intensive growth**" model. Meta has already signed a series of power agreements, securing over **6 gigawatts** of nuclear power supply to meet its AI computing needs. This phenomenon echoes our previous report on the power and talent challenges facing the AI industry [AI World Turned Upside Down: Power, Talent Running Out, Even Glasses Turning AI?](/blog/2026-01-11-012503).

This initiative builds upon the positive outcomes of the 2025 AI Coding Assistant (AICA) pilot.
*   **India** is actively building robust AI infrastructure, with the goal of becoming a global hub for AI application case studies. **Ashish Aggarwal**, Vice President of Public Policy at Nasscom, stated that India is already leading in AI deployment and adoption. Apple's decision to partner with Google ensures its products can quickly jump on the AI bandwagon, rather than spending vast resources catching up on foundational model development. This allows Apple to focus more energy on user experience and hardware-software integration, which are its strengths. For Google, this is undoubtedly a huge victory, demonstrating Gemini's leading position in the industry and solidifying its core role in the mobile AI ecosystem. This also briefly pushed Google's parent company, Alphabet, past Apple in market capitalization, showing investors' emphasis on AI technology.

Whoever controls the largest compute power and the most stable electricity supply will gain an advantage in the AI competition. This also creates enormous business opportunities for the energy industry, particularly in renewable energy and nuclear power, which can provide stable, large-scale power. Especially concerning the chip supply chain, the dynamics of **Nvidia H200 chips** in the Chinese market reflect the **geopolitical game** between the US and China in the tech sphere. This will spark profound philosophical and ethical discussions: how do we ensure AI's development aligns with human well-being rather than spiraling out of control? This is also a core issue in global AI governance.
