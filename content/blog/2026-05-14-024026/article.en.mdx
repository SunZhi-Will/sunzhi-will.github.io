### Simply Put, What Is This?

In essence, today's AI is like a rapidly growing "superbrain" that not only needs to think smarter and more autonomously (that's the Agent) but also requires a powerful body (AI chips) and an endless supply of energy (data centers and electricity) to support its operations.

Imagine our "superbrain" AI now not only answering "What's the weather like today?" but also checking the forecast itself and proactively planning tasks like, "If it rains, call me a taxi and send out a meeting postponement notice." This is the concept of an **Agent**, evolving AI from passively "following orders" to actively "autonomously solving problems," much like having a real assistant in your phone. Google's **Gemini Intelligence** aims to give your Android phone this "prescient" ability.

But to get this "superbrain" moving, it needs immense "muscles" and "energy." AI chips are its muscles, and startups like Cerebras are striving to build more powerful and efficient AI chips, allowing AI to run faster and more effectively.

And these "muscles" and "brains" all need to operate in massive "data centers," which are like AI's "super factories," running 24/7 and consuming an astonishing amount of electricity. This is why I say we are now facing the big question: "Is there enough power?" When AI's demands hit like a tsunami, can our power plants and grids truly keep up?

At the same time, as AI becomes increasingly powerful, everyone is starting to worry: will this "superbrain" get out of control? Will it do harm? Therefore, governments and tech giants worldwide are working hard to establish "traffic rules," which is **AI regulation and governance**, hoping to encourage innovation while ensuring AI's safety and compliance. It's like equipping a supercar with brakes and airbags, allowing it to run fast but also safely.

#### 📈 AI Infrastructure & Chip Development: Cerebras Electrifies the Market, Power Demand Flashes Red

First, we must re-emphasize the **Cerebras** IPO. This AI chip manufacturer successfully listed at **$185 per share**, raising a total of **$5.55 billion** and achieving a valuation of **$56.43 billion**, making it the largest stock market listing globally so far this year. This reflects the investment market's strong demand for AI infrastructure, especially the potential of non-**NVIDIA** AI chips.

Cerebras's success also reaffirms the escalating "compute power race." But behind this "compute power war" lies a huge challenge: **electricity demand**.

<Callout type="info" title="AI Data Centers: Power Giants">
  According to the latest research, data centers in the UK and US already consume **6%** of the national electricity supply. Even more astonishing, global annual investment in data centers approaches **$1 trillion**, and over the past two years, AI-driven energy consumption has increased by **15%**.
</Callout>

These are no small figures! Imagine if we liken AI to a super-eater; its "appetite" is expanding at an alarming rate. The International Data Center Authority (IDCA) points out that when a country's data center electricity consumption reaches **5%** of the national grid, it typically triggers "strong social and political backlash." Now, both the UK and US have already exceeded this threshold.

Power providers are also feeling unprecedented pressure. Experts from the American Energy Association warn that large data centers' demand for grid connections typically requires completion within one to three years, but grid infrastructure expansion projects can take seven years or even longer to finish. This time lag poses immense challenges to grid stability.

In response to this situation, some scientists are even fantasizing about moving energy-intensive data centers to "**space**" to solve Earth's energy and heat dissipation problems. This sounds like science fiction, but it also illustrates the severity of the issue.

This AI "cash-burning" battle, from chips to data centers, is testing our resource limits. #### 📱 AI Application Expansion & Agent Rise: Phones Become "Mini-Butlers"

On the other hand, our mobile world is also being completely transformed by AI. Google, at the Android Show 2026, officially unveiled the powerful features of **Gemini Intelligence**, which will turn Android phones into a "smart system," no longer passively waiting for commands, but actively understanding your needs and seamlessly switching between applications to help you complete multi-step, complex tasks.

For instance, Gemini Intelligence can automatically generate customized widgets based on your description, such as a cycling dashboard tracking wind speed and rainfall, or a weekly high-protein meal plan. It can also intelligently autofill forms, using data from your linked applications to automatically complete fields. Even the speech-to-text function has been upgraded: Gboard's "**Rambler**" feature can instantly remove filler words and repetitions from your speech, making your messages clearer.

These new features are expected to be first deployed on the latest phones like the **Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 8** and **Flip 8**, with a gradual rollout to Pixel and other Samsung Galaxy devices starting this summer. This means your phone is truly about to become a "mini-butler" with thoughts and initiative.

The Chrome browser will also deeply integrate Gemini, transforming into an an AI agent capable of summarizing web content, researching data, comparing information, and even automatically scheduling appointments. This will undoubtedly significantly boost our productivity on mobile devices. This confirms our predictions for AI Agent development in [AI Daily: The AI Battle Royale: Giants Claiming Territory, Chip Spending War, Your AI Butler is Leveling Up! 🤖💸](/blog/2026-05-09-022739).

#### ⚖️ AI Regulation & Governance: Governments Worldwide Treading Carefully

As AI technology gallops ahead like a wild horse, governments worldwide are also striving to rein it in.

<InsightQuote
  type="insight"
  content="AI governance is no longer a future issue but a present regulatory and business imperative. Regulators, boards, clients, and auditors are demanding proof of responsible AI adoption, not just intent."
  author="FinTecBuzz Report"

/>

**China** is frequently taking action on AI regulation. As of April 30th this year, China has cumulatively **868 generative AI services** that have completed filing, and another **530 applications or features** have completed registration. This demonstrates the Chinese government's determination to implement strict management and traceability for AI services.

Meanwhile, the **EU's** "AI Act" is also continuously being refined. The latest "AI Act Comprehensive Amendment" extends the mandatory compliance deadline for high-risk AI systems to **December 2nd, 2027**, and for high-risk AI systems embedded in products regulated by EU law, the deadline is extended to **August 2nd, 2028**. This gives companies more time to address complex compliance requirements. Notably, the Act also adds a ban on "nudifier" applications that generate or manipulate intimate content.

In the **United States**, various states are actively advancing AI legislation. For example, **Colorado** is pushing multiple AI bills targeting areas such as healthcare, therapy, and chatbots, and passed legislation in 2024 to regulate "algorithmic discrimination." This shows that US state governments are adopting a proactive approach in the absence of unified federal regulations.

Furthermore, the financial industry is increasingly prioritizing AI regulation. A report indicates that **72%** of surveyed companies are concerned about AI's impact on compliance, yet one-third still lack a comprehensive AI compliance strategy. This area will be a key focus for future regulation.

#### 🛡️ AI's Role in Industrial Transformation & National Security: Taiwan Finds Its Footing, AI Becomes a "New Weapon"

AI is not only reshaping the tech industry but also becoming crucial for national economic transformation and defense security.

**Taiwan** plays a pivotal role in this wave of AI development. The Executive Yuan is promoting the "**AI New Ten Major Constructions**," no longer just focusing on hardware manufacturing, but aiming to transform Taiwan's semiconductor and ICT advantages into AI computing power, data, and software-driven industrial upgrading momentum. The National Development Fund will invest billions to support startups, with a goal of cultivating **400,000 AI talents** by 2028 and fostering over ten thousand new companies to drive economic transformation with AI.

This means Taiwan is shifting from being a "hidden champion" in the global AI supply chain (primarily providing chip foundry services) to a more comprehensive and intelligent AI innovation hub. However, Taiwan also faces significant challenges, including the enormous electricity demand from AI data centers, international geopolitical risks, and fierce global competition for AI talent.

In the **national defense security** sector, AI's role is also becoming increasingly significant. Military leaders and experts from the US Army Pacific Command emphasized at the 2026 Army Pacific Symposium that artificial intelligence and machine learning are reshaping modern warfare, with the importance of achieving "decision advantage" steadily increasing. The goal of AI is to assist commanders by providing more data and information to make more timely decisions, rather than replacing human command. This indicates that AI applications in defense focus more on their role as auxiliary tools rather than independent combat entities.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

I believe this wave of AI development presents both opportunities and challenges for each of us.

On a personal level, our digital lives will become both "smarter" and more "effortless." Imagine no longer needing to switch between various apps to complete a series of actions like ordering food, navigating, and making payments. The **Gemini Intelligence** in your phone will act like your personal assistant, linking together all your necessary chores and getting them done in one go. This will significantly boost our efficiency and give us more time to focus on more creative endeavors. But at the same time, we need to consider: when AI makes so many decisions for us, will our "autonomy" be diminished? How can our data privacy be ensured? All of this requires tech companies to provide more transparent and secure solutions.

The energy consumption issue is even more pressing; if not effectively addressed, it could trigger new social conflicts and even environmental crises.
