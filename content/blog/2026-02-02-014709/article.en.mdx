### What Happened?

Hey, have you noticed that the AI around us isn't just "listening" anymore? We used to think of it as a smart dictionary or an assistant for drafting documents. But now, it's quietly evolving into a more proactive partner, one that can even "get things done" for you. It's like your barista: in the past, they'd just make what you ordered. Now, they might proactively suggest a new special, or even prepare the presentation you need for today's meeting! This shift from passive to proactive is precisely today's hottest headline in the world of AI.

### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine AI is no longer just that "answer-anything" app on your phone. It's transformed into your **"super-intelligent personal assistant"** üßë‚Äçüíº. This assistant doesn't just understand what you say; it observes your habits, anticipates your needs, and then proactively carries out tasks. This is the core concept of "Agentic AI." It doesn't just offer suggestions; it can "analyze big data, set goals, and take action with minimal human supervision."

And "AI as a Service" (AaaS)? It's like turning these intelligent personal assistants into a **"plug-and-play" luxury cloud service package.** Businesses no longer need to build expensive AI systems from scratch; instead, they can subscribe to these powerful AI functionalities on demand, much like subscribing to Netflix. This significantly lowers the barrier to AI adoption, allowing even SMEs to easily enjoy the efficiency gains AI offers. Simply put, AI is no longer a cool technology confined to labs; it's now truly stepping into our offices and living rooms, becoming an integral part of our lives and work.

The company projects its capital expenditure could grow by **37%** this year, reaching **$56 billion**, with "significant increases" expected in 2028 and 2029 due to AI demand. At the same time, Huang also expressed concerns about the supply of memory chips required for AI workloads.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

AI's shift from "listening" to "doing" has a comprehensive and profound impact on each of us.

**For personal life, it will become more convenient, but potentially more "transparent"** üí°. Imagine your AI assistant actively managing your calendar, planning dinner based on your preferences, or even predicting movies you might want to watch‚Äîall thanks to advances in "Personal Intelligence" and "Agentic AI." But at the same time, this means your personal data is being utilized more deeply. Finding a balance between convenience and privacy will be a challenge each of us must face.

**In the workplace, AI will be an accelerator for "efficiency," but potentially a driver for "layoffs"** üìä. Agentic AI can "automate collaborative activities and business processes," increasing corporate efficiency. For example, AI prediction tools in the banking sector have already "saved hundreds of thousands of hours" for corporate clients. While this is certainly good, it's also estimated that AI could lead to the disappearance of "200,000 banking jobs" in the European Union by 2030. For **Gen Z** youngsters, AI's impact on entry-level positions is already "an immediate economic reality." Without adequate "wage protection or accessible retraining pathways," they may be forced into "lower-quality jobs," leading to "increased debt burdens" and "postponed major life milestones" such as homeownership, savings accumulation, and starting families. This is an issue we previously discussed in [AI Flexes Its Muscles: From Space Warning to Workplace Revolution, Intelligent Agents Are Everywhere!](/blog/2026-01-28-011959).

### Insider's Deep Analysis

I view this wave of AI as essentially a **battle shifting from a "compute power arms race" to "application scenario implementation."** For the past few years, tech giants have poured massive funds into developing more powerful models. Now, the battlefield has shifted to how to convert these models into tangible services and products that generate "stable cash flow." Agentic AI and AaaS are key to this transformation, turning AI from a "genius who only studies" into a "practical earner who supports the family."

Microsoft's **Azure Foundry** and Google Gemini's "Personal Intelligence" update both indicate that these companies are striving to bring AI closer to users, not just by offering a large model, but by building a complete ecosystem. Apple's acquisition of Q.ai is a classic "hardware integrated with AI" strategy, aligning with the trend of AI delving into emotional companionship and personalized experiences, as discussed in our article [AI Doesn't Just Think, It Wants to Be Your Friend: 2026 Kicks Off with an AI Impact Wave from Workplace Transformation to Emotional Companionship!](/blog/2026-01-26-012448). This suggests that future AI experiences will be more seamless, personalized, and deeply integrated into the devices we use daily.

And Taiwan, undoubtedly, is the **"unsung hero"** of this transformation. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang's urging, combined with TSMC's multi-billion dollar capital expenditure plans, once again proves Taiwan's irreplaceable position in the global AI hardware supply chain. Advanced chips and advanced packaging technology, in particular, are the cornerstones driving AI's computational power. As global giants scramble for AI chips, Taiwan's role will become even more critical, bringing a "trillion-dollar business opportunity" to our semiconductor and related component manufacturers.

However, this AI "capital war" is not without its hidden concerns. Major tech companies are relying on issuing massive corporate bonds to support their immense capital expenditure for AI infrastructure, which reminds me of the dot-com bubble era. While this AI boom is supported by "actual revenue growth" rather than mere speculation, this large-scale debt financing could still impact global financial market stability. Investors will continue to monitor whether these massive investments can translate into sustainable profits, thereby justifying their "AI premium."
