Meanwhile, **KDDI** is using **AI agents** to resolve communication service disruptions, meaning future customer service and troubleshooting will be more efficient and intelligent.

Of course, with the rapid development of AI, **AI security issues** are becoming increasingly prominent. **Google's** latest **AI Threat Report** reveals the potential risks of **AI model distillation** and **Agent AI** being exploited by threat actors. Model distillation allows malicious actors to achieve similar effects with smaller models, while Agent AI could be used for automated cyberattacks. This report once again sounds the alarm, urging the industry to establish **responsible AI security standards**.

<Callout type="info" title="What is AI Model Distillation?">
  AI model distillation is a technique where a smaller, more efficient model (student model) learns the behavior and knowledge of a larger, more complex model (teacher model). While beneficial for AI deployment, it could also be exploited by malicious actors to create harder-to-trace, easier-to-deploy offensive AI models.
</Callout>

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a seasoned investigative journalist, connecting these AI news stories reveals several highly critical trends.

Firstly, **the 'globalization' and 'democratization' of AI are irreversible trends**. Microsoft and Google's massive investments, coupled with the French President's emphasis on sovereign AI, all tell us that AI is no longer the exclusive domain of a few Western tech giants. It is spreading globally, with emerging markets, in particular, rapidly increasing their participation. India, as the host of this summit, and its ambitions in AI governance and data infrastructure, all signal its increasingly important position in the global AI landscape [Read More](/blog/2026-02-15-015034).

Secondly, **the era of Embodied AI is truly upon us**. Unitree Robotics' performance at the Spring Festival Gala, along with the prevailing industry view that AI is moving from the digital to the physical world, underscores this point. AI is no longer just virtual computation; it's acquiring a 'body' and executing tasks in the real world. This will profoundly change manufacturing, services, and even our daily lives. However, this also means that the **urgency of AI ethics and safety issues has reached an unprecedented level**. When robots can truly act autonomously, how do we ensure their behavior aligns with human values? This is a very complex issue requiring global deliberation.

Furthermore, **AI's 'application depth' and 'scenario breadth' are growing exponentially**. From rare disease diagnosis to resolving communication service disruptions, from AI music creation to influencing macroeconomic policies, the scope of AI applications far exceeds what we imagined just a few years ago. Businesses viewing AI as infrastructure indicates that AI has transitioned from being an 'optional tool' to an 'indispensable core competency'.

Behind all this, I see that global society's perception of AI is shifting from a 'tech boom' to 'infrastructure development.' Like electricity and the internet, AI is becoming a cornerstone of modern societal operation. And this infrastructure race is not just a technological contest; it's a comprehensive game involving capital, talent, policy, and ethical standards. Whoever can better balance innovation with risk will gain the upper hand in this grand transformation.
