### 📋 Quick Highlights
- OpenAI halts Sora video service, redirecting resources to agentic AI and new model development.
- Arm unveils its first AGI CPU for AI data centers, poised to reshape the AI hardware market.
- Global AI focus shifts eastward to Asia, with China's open-source AI models greatly increasing global influence.
- Apple's Siri 2.0 at WWDC 2026 is expected to deeply integrate AI, possibly with Google Gemini.
- Agentic AI models are revolutionizing diverse industries, from healthcare administration to corporate decision-making, boosting efficiency.
- Internal enterprise AI agents surge by **467%**, raising "shadow AI" cybersecurity risks for companies.
- China's AI chip and robotics tech rapid development accounts for **two-thirds** of global AI robot patent applications.

### What Happened?
Hey there, fellow tech enthusiasts, I daresay, the AI world has been more dramatic than a prime-time soap opera lately! Before, when we chatted about AI, we might have been stuck on "smart chatbots" or "programs that can paint and write." But from yesterday to today, I've observed a crystal-clear trend: AI is no longer just a "book-smart genius"; it's now ready to "roll up its sleeves and get to work"!

This shift from "responsive AI" to "agentic AI" is like your smart assistant evolving from merely answering questions to proactively planning your trips, placing orders, and even managing emails. And behind all this is a dazzling series of strategic moves and technological breakthroughs by global tech giants.

### Simply Put, What Is This?
Let me use a relatable analogy to explain "agentic AI"! Imagine your traditional AI assistant as a "super chatty secretary." Ask her anything, and she'll give you a beautiful response. But what about "agentic AI"? She's like a **"professional manager with decision-making authority."** You just tell her the ultimate goal, like "I need a report on a certain market trend," and she'll go find the data, analyze it, organize it, format it, and even automatically adjust the style to your preferences, then hand you the report directly, without you needing to guide her step-by-step.

The difference here isn't just "smarter"; it's a qualitative leap from "passive response" to "**proactive execution**." It can free us from tedious daily tasks, leaving our time for work that truly requires human creativity and judgment.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
The biggest headline these past few days has undoubtedly been a major move by **OpenAI**. On **March 25, 2026**, OpenAI announced it would discontinue the standalone app and API for its highly anticipated AI video generation service, **Sora**. Saying goodbye so soon after its launch came as a surprise to many. Even a previously announced plan to license famous characters with Disney has been terminated with Sora's exit.

<Callout type="info" title="Sora Departs, Agents Reign">
OpenAI CEO **Sam Altman** stated that the company will shift its focus to **AI agents** and a new AI model named **"Spud."** This signifies that OpenAI is redirecting its vast computational resources from pure content generation to more autonomous task execution capabilities. The reasons behind this, aside from Sora's own immense computational demands, might also include streamlining its product line and preparing for a potential initial public offering (IPO) in the fourth quarter of this year.

This aligns perfectly with the trends we discussed previously in [AI Daily: AI Battle Royale Escalates: Autonomous Agents Invade Enterprises, Tech Giants Splurging, But Meta Layoffs Rumored?](/blog/2026-03-15-015558), demonstrating that AI agents are becoming the new battleground for major tech giants.

On the hardware front, AI's "bones" and "muscles" are evolving simultaneously. **Arm**, on **March 24, 2026**, unveiled its first mass-production chip product: the **Arm AGI CPU**. This chip is designed specifically for AI data centers, built on TSMC's **3-nanometer** process, featuring **136 Neoverse V3 cores**, and aims to meet the explosive demand for computing power from agentic AI. This marks a significant strategic shift, as Arm traditionally relied on intellectual property licensing but is now selling its own chips, directly entering the AI chip competition. **Meta Platforms** will serve as a key partner, with **OpenAI**, **Cloudflare**, **SAP**, and **SK Telecom** among its customers.

Concurrently, the global AI landscape is quietly being reshaped. A report from the Boao Forum for Asia indicates that the epicenter of global AI development is gradually shifting from Europe and North America to Asia. Asian countries, leveraging their massive digital populations, diverse application ecosystems, and clear policy frameworks, are rapidly transforming from AI followers into leaders. Notably, China's open-source AI models are drawing significant global attention. According to **OpenRouter**'s LLM ranking, **six** of the top ten models this week are from China, including **Step 3.5 Flash**, **MiniMax M2.5**, and **DeepSeek V3.2**. China's Xiaomi even topped the list with a **21.4%** market share. There are even reports suggesting that **Composer 2**, a model from U.S. AI code generation startup **Cursor**, was found to be based on the open-source model **Kimi K2.5** from Chinese startup **Moonshot AI (月之暗面)**. This demonstrates China's increasing influence on global AI startups through its open-source AI infrastructure. Furthermore, as of **January 2026**, China accounts for **two-thirds** of global patent applications for AI-driven robotics.

Tech giants are also accelerating the deep integration of AI into their products. **Apple** is expected to unveil significant AI advancements for **Siri 2.0**, codenamed **"Campos,"** at its WWDC conference, scheduled for **June 8-12, 2026**. Even more exciting, this major upgrade for Siri is rumored to deeply integrate **Google Gemini** technology, enabling Siri to handle more complex tasks such as summarizing lengthy documents and editing photos.

The application of AI agents is also revolutionizing vertical industries. Research by security firm **BeyondTrust** reveals that the number of AI agents operating within enterprises surged by **467%** in a single year, many even running without the knowledge of cybersecurity teams, forming "shadow AI workforces." These agents are no longer limited to simple chatbots but can autonomously execute tasks across internal tools and APIs. While this boosts efficiency, it also brings new cybersecurity risks. For instance, **Google** has deployed **Gemini**-powered AI agents to monitor dark web threats, filtering over **10 million** messages daily with an accuracy rate of **98%**.

In the medical field, AI agents are moving from task automation to autonomous administrative workflows, particularly in revenue cycle management (RCM), claims, and pre-authorization processes. Predictive AI and "AI medical records" are transforming data into real-time risk signals. The UK's National Health Service (NHS) is also expected to discuss the ethics, governance, and procurement issues of large-scale generative AI applications at the **Digital Health ReWired 2026** conference. There are even reports that AstraZeneca is establishing an "AI-first" R&D culture, leveraging AI agents to accelerate new drug development, shifting from traditional hypothesis-driven to data-driven discovery models.

The manufacturing and robotics sectors are also seeing a profound impact from AI. By the **end of 2025**, approximately **16,000** humanoid robots will have been installed globally, with projections of reaching **115,000** by **2027**, a nearly **sevenfold** increase in two years. China's high-tech manufacturing sector grew by **9.4%** in **2025**, and industrial robot production increased by **28%**.

**For Businesses**: The widespread adoption of AI agents is like adding countless super-efficient digital employees within a company. They can automatically process millions of data points in the background, boost marketing efficiency by up to **40%**, accelerate new drug development, and even help CEOs make faster, data-driven decisions. However, simultaneously, the cybersecurity risks posed by "shadow AI workforces" remind us that AI management and governance will be an unavoidable issue for enterprises. Ensuring these autonomous AIs are not maliciously exploited and do not cause data breaches will be an urgent priority for every company's IT and cybersecurity department.

### Insider's Deep Analysis
From these recent AI news, I believe we are in an era of "great reshuffle." OpenAI's decision to abandon Sora, a seemingly glamorous video generation application, and pivot towards more foundational and universal AI agents, is actually a very smart strategy. It recognizes that future AI won't just "generate content"; more importantly, it will "execute tasks." It's like building a house: Sora was the beautifully decorated show home, but AI agents are the engineering team that lays the foundation and installs plumbing and electricity. OpenAI CEO **Sam Altman**'s focus on AI agents and the new model **Spud** also hints at their long-term pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).

Arm's first venture into its own chips, directly challenging the traditional AI chip market, is not just a display of technical prowess but also a high-stakes gamble on its business model. While Arm historically relied on IP licensing, it's now "doing it itself," which will put unprecedented pressure on chip giants like NVIDIA and Intel. And **Meta** becoming a core partner reflects the enormous demand for customized AI hardware from hyperscale data centers.

The rise of Asia, particularly China, in the AI domain is an undeniable fact. I've observed China's open-source models gaining increasing international influence, even becoming the foundation for some U.S. startups. This is not only a testament to their technical strength but also represents the advantages accumulated from their data ecosystem and application scenarios. This will promote more frequent global AI technical exchanges but may also trigger more discussions about technological leadership and data sovereignty, an issue we touched upon in [AI Daily: AI Battle Royale: When Tech Giants Clash with Ethical Red Lines, Are Your Job and Privacy Safe?](/blog/2026-03-14-012927).
