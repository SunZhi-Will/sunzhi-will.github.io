- High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) and DRAM costs are skyrocketing, with DRAM prices expected to double by 2026, significantly increasing AI server costs.

### What Happened?
Friends, I have to say, AI's recent development speed is like it's eaten a rocket – new headlines every day! We used to casually chat in coffee shops, wondering, "Will AI take my job?" Now, that question has morphed into, "AI *is* taking my job; what do I do?" This AI surge isn't just affecting our livelihoods; it's unleashing a tidal wave across global electricity supplies and memory chip prices, all thanks to AI's "gargantuan appetite"!

### Simply Put, What Is This?
Imagine we're in the midst of a global digital gold rush. AI is the gleaming gold mine, and everyone wants to stake a claim. But here's the catch: to dig this gold, you need super-powerful "mining machines" (AI chips and data centers). These machines not only guzzle electricity at an astonishing rate, but their "fuel" (High-Bandwidth Memory, HBM) is also becoming prohibitively expensive. What's more, as everyone rushes to mine this gold, our existing work ecosystems and social structures are undergoing unprecedented, bulldozer-like transformations. It's like your home internet upgrading from dial-up to fiber optics – hundreds of times faster – but suddenly your monthly electricity bill explodes, and your neighbor becomes a rich influencer overnight. Don't you think you'd need to figure out how to ride that wave?

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

**1. AI's "Electric Tiger" Crisis: Data Center Power Consumption Beyond Imagination** ⚡
I saw the latest report and was absolutely stunned! The development of artificial intelligence is pushing global data center electricity demand at an alarming rate. In 2024, global data centers consumed approximately **460 terawatt-hours (TWh)** of electricity. But by **2026**, this figure is projected to exceed **1000 TWh**, which is almost equivalent to Japan's total annual electricity consumption.

Think about it: every time we ask **ChatGPT** a question, it consumes roughly **2.9 watt-hours** of electricity, nearly **10 times** more than a single Google search! This "inference" computation model requires thousands of power-hungry **GPUs** to run 24/7. This isn't just a global issue; it's a series of "localized" crises. For instance, **Northern Virginia** in the U.S., known as "Data Center Alley," handles **70%** of global internet traffic, and local power companies are already overwhelmed. Ireland faces a similar situation, with data centers accounting for **21%** of the nation's electricity consumption, projected to reach **32%** by 2026, forcing the government to intervene and restrict new power connections in some areas.

**2. Semiconductor Supply Chain Strain: HBM and DRAM Prices Skyrocket Nearly Fourfold** 📈
Beyond electricity, another headache for AI developers is the soaring cost of High-Bandwidth Memory (**HBM**) and **DRAM**. Research firm **SemiAnalysis** predicts that memory's share of hyperscale data center capital expenditure will surge from around **8%** in 2023/2024 to **30%** by 2026, an almost **fourfold** increase in just four years!

<StatsHighlight
  title="AI Data Center Memory Expenditure Forecast"

The reason behind this is simple: major memory manufacturers like **Samsung**, **SK Hynix**, and **Micron** are shifting significant DRAM production capacity to HBM, as HBM is critical for AI accelerators (such as **NVIDIA H100/H200**). The result is a squeeze on the supply of traditional consumer-grade DDR4 and DDR5 memory, driving prices up instead of down. SemiAnalysis projects that DRAM prices will more than "**double**" by **2026**, and LPDDR5 contract prices have already tripled since Q1 2025!

This impact directly translates to AI server prices, with the cost of **NVIDIA B200** servers, for example, expected to rise by as much as **20%** by the end of this year due to the significant influence of memory costs.

**3. AI's Impact on the Job Market: Economists Shift from Observing to Concerned** 💼
AI's impact on employment has consistently been a hot topic. In the past, economists were skeptical of claims that AI would massively replace human jobs, dismissing them as "AI-washing" for corporate layoffs. Now, however, the situation seems different.

<Callout type="info" title="Economists' Shifting Stance">
Recently, economists have begun to seriously consider AI's potential impact on the job market and worry that policymakers are unprepared. While most have yet to see widespread job losses, AI could lead to rapid economic growth in the next five to twenty-five years, albeit accompanied by greater inequality and the disappearance of millions of jobs.
</Callout>

One study indicates that as much as **70%** of jobs are to some extent "exposed" to AI's influence. This doesn't mean these employees will be immediately laid off, but they will likely face significant changes in their job responsibilities.

Nearly **40%** of HR teams have added extra in-person interviews, while nearly **half** have increased assessments to verify skills and experience. This demonstrates that AI is changing the rules of the hiring game.

**4. Global AI Strategies and Educational Governance: Governments Actively Laying the Groundwork** 🌍
Faced with the AI wave, governments worldwide are also actively formulating response strategies. The **White House** released a "National AI Policy Framework," emphasizing workforce training and education as core components, aiming to guide the responsible development and deployment of AI. Schools are also increasingly prioritizing AI governance over rapid, blind adoption, recognizing the importance of ethics and potential risks.

Taiwan's **Kaihua County** government signed a "AI + Education" county-level strategic cooperation framework agreement with the Zhejiang Provincial Educational Technology Center and released the province's first "AI + Education" action plan. This plan includes five major initiatives: solidifying the digital intelligence foundation, deepening classroom intelligence transformation, optimizing curriculum resources, enhancing teacher and student literacy, and empowering digital intelligence evaluation. Its goal is to create a hub for AI and education integration innovation.

**5. AI Model and Domestic Chip Competition: DeepSeek Adopts Huawei Chips** 🇨🇳
In the AI model arms race, we see giants like **Google Gemini**, **OpenAI GPT**, and **Anthropic Claude** continuously innovating. According to the latest news, **OpenAI's GPT-5.5 "Spud"** has completed pre-training, and **Google** has also released **Gemma 4**, its newest open-source model based on **Gemini 3**.

However, most notably, Chinese AI startup **DeepSeek's** upcoming next-generation AI model, **V4**, is rumored to fully adopt **Huawei's** latest AI chips! This is a very significant signal, as it indicates major progress in China's construction of a domestic AI hardware ecosystem, potentially reshaping the global AI technology competitive landscape.

The **DeepSeek V4** model employs an architecture design with nearly **one trillion** parameters and is expected to incorporate hundreds of thousands of Huawei **Ascend 950PR** chips. This is projected to achieve an inference speed increase of **1.8 times** and boast a context window of up to **one million** tokens. This demonstrates China's solid strides towards a self-sufficient AI chip supply chain.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

I believe this AI wave has profound implications for each of us.

Firstly, **your electricity bill might sting more** 💸. As data center power consumption expands like a black hole, your home electricity bill will likely silently follow suit, potentially even impacting power supply stability.

Secondly, **work skill iteration is accelerating** 🏃‍♀️. Economists used to be unconcerned about AI, but now even they are taking it seriously. This means we can no longer treat AI as an optional tool; we must actively learn and apply it. If you're still stuck in traditional work modes, like using a pager in the smartphone era, you'll easily be left behind. The Robert Half report already highlights that companies are increasingly prioritizing skill-based hiring and stepping up internal training.

<Callout type="info" title="Personal Adaptation Strategies">
Learn AI tools, integrate AI into your daily workflows, and enhance your abilities in problem analysis, high-level planning, and cross-domain collaboration. This will transform you from being threatened by 'AI replacement' into gaining an 'AI-enhanced' advantage.
</Callout>

Thirdly, **the education system will undergo massive changes** 🎓. From the White House to grassroots educational units in Taiwan, everyone is actively promoting the integration of AI and education. In the future, schools will likely focus more on nurturing students' ability to solve problems and think critically with AI, rather than rote memorization. For parents and students, choosing schools that actively embrace AI, fostering digital literacy will become increasingly important.

Fourthly, **the global tech landscape is being redrawn** 🌐. China's progress in achieving self-sufficiency in AI chips means we may see a more diverse and competitive AI hardware ecosystem. For companies reliant on a single supply chain, this presents both challenges and opportunities.

Beneath all this, what I see is a comprehensive "**Digital Survival Battle**."

**Profound Reflections on the Energy Dilemma**: AI data centers' electricity demand isn't just a matter of power supply; it profoundly involves the transition to **green energy**. Ironically, while the world strives to reduce reliance on fossil fuels, AI's energy demands can sometimes push power companies to burn more fossil fuels as an emergency measure. This undoubtedly poses a significant challenge to our "net-zero emissions" goals. The critical task for the next decade will be figuring out how to apply AI for energy optimization, rather than merely consumption.

This is the essence of "**Pi-shaped talent**"—individuals with deep specialized knowledge who can also horizontally connect multiple domains and expertly leverage AI tools.
