### Insider's Deep Analysis
From my observation, today's news reveals several core trends in the AI field:

First, the **"self-sufficiency" battle for AI chips** is unfolding. In the past, **NVIDIA** was almost synonymous with the AI chip market, but now giants like **Microsoft** are developing their own chips, not just to reduce costs, but to control core technology and ensure competitiveness in cloud AI services. This is a strategic shift, meaning the AI chip market will present a more diversified competitive landscape in the future. **TSMC**, as the world's leading wafer foundry, its **3nm process** has become a key determinant in the AI chip arms race.

Second, the **"edge AI" trend** is becoming increasingly evident. The **collaboration between MediaTek and NVIDIA in automotive chips**, and **Apple's consideration of integrating Gemini into Siri**, all indicate that AI is no longer confined to the cloud but is increasingly penetrating our devices, cars, and other "edge" equipment. This will lead to lower latency, enhanced privacy protection, and more diverse application scenarios.
