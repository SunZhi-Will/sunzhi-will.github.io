### 📋 Quick Highlights
- OpenAI has allocated **$4 billion** to establish a new division, expanding enterprise AI applications through the acquisition of Tomoro.
- Microsoft's early investment in OpenAI has now yielded an astonishing **$122 billion** in unrealized gains.
- Tech giants are projected to splurge over **$700 billion** this year on AI infrastructure, igniting a "cash-burning battle."
- Nvidia alone is pouring approximately **$40 billion** into AI infrastructure and various AI startups.
- Gartner predicts that by **2028**, **40%** of organizations deploying AI will adopt AI observability tools.
- Taiwan plays a critical role in the global AI chip supply chain but faces challenges in energy and geopolitics.
- Governments worldwide are now treating AI compute power and energy as national priorities to secure future competitiveness.

### What Happened?
Friends, the hottest topic at the coffee shop recently has probably been AI, right? I've been hearing a lot of discussions lately about how fast AI is developing—is it really going to change our lives, or is it just a bubble? Today, I discovered that the answer to this question might be even more "cash-burning" than we imagined! Global tech giants are pouring massive amounts of money into AI infrastructure at an unprecedented pace and scale. This isn't small stakes; it's a genuine arms race of cold, hard cash!

### Simply Put, What Is This?
Simply put, AI is like an insatiably hungry "super brain" that requires enormous amounts of electricity, high-speed networks, and specialized processors to function. Now, these tech giants are like architects building one "mega mansion" (data center) after another and laying down "super neural networks" (optical communications) for this "super brain," all to help it learn and work faster and smarter. Imagine, we used to build houses focusing on aesthetics, but now it's all about frantically digging deep foundations and reinforcing steel bars, because what's being built on top is a smart city that can think and operate independently in the future! This massive investment isn't just to make AI more powerful; it's to seize the high ground of future technology.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
The most striking news recently has undoubtedly been **OpenAI's** big move. They've established a new enterprise business unit called "OpenAI Deployment Company," which has received an infusion of up to **$4 billion** and was valued at **$10 billion** at its inception. They also directly acquired the AI consulting firm **Tomoro**, instantly gaining **150** engineers and deployment specialists. These experts aren't just armchair strategists; they'll "embed" directly into client teams, providing hands-on assistance to help enterprises implement AI technology, transforming AI from a mere concept into a tool that genuinely creates value.

Meanwhile, **Microsoft's** early investment in **OpenAI** now appears to have been a masterstroke. According to reports, Microsoft initially invested approximately **$13 billion**, and by October last year, this equity stake was valued at around **$135 billion**, representing an astonishing **$122 billion** in unrealized gains! This reminds me of the saying: "High risk, high reward." Microsoft CEO **Satya Nadella** stated in court, "The reason this investment succeeded is that we took a risk." By the end of March this year, OpenAI's valuation had soared to an astounding **$852 billion**. This not only brought Microsoft massive profits but also objectively fueled the explosive growth of the global AI industry.

It's not just OpenAI and Microsoft; the entire tech industry is embroiled in a "compute power scramble." **Alphabet** (Google's parent company), **Amazon**, **Microsoft**, and **Meta**—these giants are expected to collectively spend over **$700 billion** on AI infrastructure this year, a significant increase from last year's **$600 billion**. This means they'll be investing even more capital in building data centers, purchasing super chips, and upgrading networks. To secure this enormous funding, these companies are even frequently issuing bonds, a phenomenon relatively rare in Silicon Valley, which is known for its abundant cash flow. This highlights the immense capital demand driven by AI development.

**Nvidia**, as the undisputed leader in AI chips, is directly investing **$40 billion** into AI infrastructure, with **$30 billion** allocated to AI model startups like OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI. Their CEO, **Jensen Huang**, has stated that their goal is to invest in all excellent foundational model companies, not picking winners, but empowering everyone. Nvidia also plans to invest up to **$3.2 billion** in optical communication companies such as fiber optic manufacturer **Corning**, underscoring their understanding that AI development is inseparable from high-speed, stable data transmission.

At the AI application level, this boom continues to deepen. Authoritative institutions like **Gartner** predict that by **2028**, **40%** of organizations deploying AI will adopt specialized **AI observability tools** to monitor model performance, bias, and output. This is akin to driving a car: we don't just need a dashboard showing speed; we need more precise sensors to monitor engine status and tire wear to ensure driving safety. The transparency, security, and reliability of AI models will become paramount for future governance.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
For ordinary people like us, what does the rapid development of AI mean?

First, it means a transformation in our way of working. As services like the **OpenAI Deployment Company** penetrate businesses, AI will increasingly participate directly in our daily work. High-repetitive, rule-based tasks are likely to be taken over by AI agents, requiring us to learn to collaborate with AI and enhance our creativity and decision-making abilities.

Second, it impacts the global economic landscape we live in. You might not have realized it, but the demand for AI servers and chips has quietly reshaped the map of international trade. Currently, the **United States** imports more goods from **Taiwan** than from **China**, a significant portion of which is chips and servers driven by the AI race. This highlights Taiwan's strategic position in the global supply chain but also brings geopolitical sensitivities.

Finally, there's the energy issue. The operation of AI brains demands astronomical amounts of electricity, posing a huge challenge to global energy supply chains and green energy transitions. As Indian business magnate **Gautam Adani** put it, "Energy and intelligence are now inseparable." Countries worldwide are vying for dominance in AI and compute power, both of which must be considered national priorities. Japan's **SoftBank** has also recognized this trend, launching plans to establish a battery business in Japan to meet the skyrocketing electricity demand brought by AI. This also makes us ponder: Do we have enough electricity? Can green energy development keep pace with AI?

### Insider's Deep Analysis
From an investigative reporter's perspective, what I see behind this is essentially a comprehensive game of technology versus national power. The enormous investments by tech giants are not merely commercial activities; they resemble national-level infrastructure projects, only this time the "roads" and "bridges" are the compute power and data flows of the digital world. This race is no longer just about who has more advanced technology, but also about who has deeper pockets, a more stable supply chain, and more abundant energy.

The rapid popularization of AI also makes the development of "Embodied AI" possible. Imagine robots not only thinking but also interacting with the physical world like humans. The application scope will extend from autonomous driving and financial fraud detection to new drug development and smart manufacturing. This is not just a technological breakthrough but also a profound impact on social ethics and the labor market, just as we explored in our previous article, [AI Daily: AI Battle Royale: Giants Claim Territory, Chip Spending Spree, Your AI Butler is Getting Smart! 🤖💸](/blog/2026-05-09-022739), where AI agent technology is making our "AI butlers" increasingly lifelike.

As AI models play increasingly central roles across various industries, ensuring their stability, fairness, and security becomes paramount. AI observability tools can monitor model performance, promptly detect and correct potential biases, errors, or malicious behaviors. This is crucial for maintaining trust in AI systems and ensuring their adherence to ethical standards. It's like a spaceship on a mission, requiring a comprehensive monitoring system to ensure all subsystems are functioning correctly.
</Callout>

Taiwan's position in the global AI supply chain is even more critical. To secure its AI chip supply, the United States' reliance on Taiwan is steadily increasing. While this brings economic benefits, it also exacerbates geopolitical sensitivities, making Taiwan a "critical node" in this AI war, whose stability affects the lifeline of global technological development. At the same time, the high demand for compute power places immense pressure on electricity supply, a challenge Taiwan must address. According to recent reports, against the backdrop of soaring global oil prices due to conflicts in the Gulf region, Taiwan currently holds approximately **120 days** of strategic oil reserves, providing a crucial buffer in the face of an energy crisis. However, in the long term, securing a stable green energy supply is an urgent task.

### One-Liner Summary
In my opinion, this AI frenzy is not just a technological revolution; it's a global reallocation of resources and a geopolitical reshuffle. AI is no longer just a "book-smart genius"; it now has to "burn through cash to support its existence," and it's intertwined with the world's energy lifeline and national security!
