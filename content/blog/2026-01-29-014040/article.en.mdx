### What Does This Mean for Us?
For ordinary people, AI's transformation from "chatting" to "doing business" means our lives will see more "smart" services. Imagine future doctors, who might have AI assistants like **AlphaGenome**, capable of analyzing your genetic data more rapidly, providing more precise diagnoses and treatment plans. This not only enhances medical efficiency but may also open new avenues for many previously intractable diseases.

In the workplace, AI's influence will also be more profound. Your job content and mine may be redefined by AI. Tasks that are highly repetitive and rule-based will be done faster and better by AI. This might sound a bit daunting, but viewed differently, it also frees us up to engage in more creative, strategic, and interpersonal work. We'll no longer be "work robots," but can dedicate our time to more valuable endeavors. On a societal level, the challenges posed by AI are real. From data privacy, algorithmic bias, to the ethical issues of intelligent weapons, all require governments, businesses, and civil society to collectively participate in discussions to establish responsible governance frameworks. This is like a major test, challenging humanity on how to harness this powerful technological force to benefit all.
