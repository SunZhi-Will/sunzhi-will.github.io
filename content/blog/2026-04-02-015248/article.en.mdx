### What Happened?

Just the other day, chatting with friends at a café, everyone was buzzing about the latest "new gadgets" in the AI world. Sure enough, today's (April 2, 2026) AI news once again left me astonished: **OpenAI** quietly launched a preview of **GPT-5**, while **Google** made **Gemini**'s "personal intelligence" feature even smarter and more user-friendly. Meanwhile, tech giant **Microsoft** hasn't been idle, pouring vast sums into AI; its cloud and AI infrastructure investment in Singapore alone is projected to reach **$5.5 billion** by 2029. All of this makes it feel like the "Age of Discovery" for AI has truly arrived, with sails full, surging forward at unimaginable speed.

However, even as everyone cheers for AI's progress, several serious warnings have emerged. **DeepMind** co-founder **Demis Hassabis** issued a stark warning about the development of "super AI," bluntly stating that this thing could potentially lead to human extinction, and that the global AI race has reached an "out of control" state. It's like a grand tech party with deafening music, everyone dancing excitedly, when suddenly someone steps forward, gravely reminding everyone: "Watch your step; this party might not be as safe as it seems."

### Simply Put, What Is This?

I've noticed that the two main axes of AI development now are making AI "**understand the world better**" and making AI "**more responsible**."

First, the key to making AI "understand the world better" lies in "**World Models**" and "**Embodied AI**." Imagine playing a video game where the characters can only talk to you but don't know how to walk, pick up items, or avoid obstacles – that game would definitely not be fun. Past AI was like this; they were "conversation masters" that could write articles and code, but they were completely blind to the physical laws of the real world, spatial concepts, and how to manipulate objects.

"World Models" aim to give AI a "game engine," allowing it not only to understand text but also physical laws, predict what will happen, and even practice various actions in a virtual world. "**Embodied AI**" takes this a step further, giving AI a "body," like a robot, enabling AI to truly enter the physical world and learn and interact hands-on. This is like AI transforming from a "book-smart genius" to a "book-smart and experiment-savvy practitioner," allowing it to truly understand what "hot" feels like or what "heavy" means.

After all, we want AI to be a helpful assistant, not a "Pandora's Box" that could unleash chaos at any moment.

#### Crisis-Laden AI Safety and Governance

**DeepMind** co-founder **Demis Hassabis's** warning is not unfounded. He mentioned that in the past, he strived to build an AI "technical safety net," for instance, by controlling risks through independent oversight and confidential R&D. But the sudden emergence of **ChatGPT** in 2022 completely changed the game. At that time, tech giants, vying for an early lead, rushed into AI development, causing existing safety review mechanisms to gradually become ineffective and making it difficult for non-profit governance mechanisms to maintain their influence amid fierce business competition.

This has also prompted governments and international organizations to start formulating stricter regulations. For example, the European Union's AI Act will fully come into force on **August 2, 2026**, becoming the world's first comprehensive and legally binding AI framework. The act categorizes AI systems by risk level, from "unacceptable risk" (prohibited) to "minimal risk" (no mandatory obligations), and imposes stringent requirements for high-risk AI systems, including risk management, human oversight, and technical documentation. For businesses, this means AI governance is no longer an optional "checklist" task, but a strategic capability influencing market competitiveness.

2.  **Audit Expectations Shifting to Technical Evidence:** Requires providing AI model cards (documenting architecture, intended use, performance metrics, etc.) and data provenance.

#### The Rise of World Models and Embodied AI

AI is no longer content with theoretical discussions; it's getting ready to "enter the arena." The goal of **World Models** is to enable AI to understand how the physical world operates, predict the development of events, and plan actions based on this understanding. Think of it like giving AI a superpower to foresee the future and simulate reality.

**Google DeepMind** has made significant strides in this area; their **Genie 3** can now generate entire interactive **3D** environments from text prompts. It allows AI to navigate and interact in real-time within these environments at **24 frames per second** in **720p** resolution, maintaining physical consistency for several minutes. This isn't just a video; it's a "simulated reality" you can enter and take action in!

#### AI's Transformative Impact on Industry Applications

AI isn't just changing how we chat; it's quietly revolutionizing industries across the board:

*   **Biotechnology and Drug Development** 🧪: AI is accelerating the efficiency of drug discovery and development. In the past, researchers had to screen millions of compounds; now AI can pre-limit the search scope. AI-assisted workflows are expected to cut early discovery time by **30-40%** and reduce preclinical candidate development time to **13-18 months**, a significant leap compared to the traditional three to four years.
*   **Manufacturing** ⚙️: Collaboration between robots and AI systems is becoming increasingly common. Robots are no longer just executing predefined scripts but can make context-relevant adjustments based on real-time data, moving towards AI-driven autonomous decision-making.
*   **Software Development** 💻: Advanced models like **GPT-5** are already deeply embedded in **Microsoft 365 Copilot** and **GitHub Copilot**, significantly boosting the efficiency of code generation, debugging, and workflow automation. It can handle large-scale projects, extended conversations, and features persistent memory, truly becoming a developer's "digital colleague."

#### Ethical and Legal Challenges Posed by AI

As AI generation technologies become widespread, new ethical and legal issues are emerging, especially concerning "**digital portrait rights**" and "**copyright**":

*   **Digital Portrait Rights and 'Digital Resurrection'** 🎭: Want to bring a deceased actor "back to the screen" in an advertisement? While technically possible, this presents significant legal and ethical challenges. New York State's "AI Ad Transparency and Synthetic Performer Disclosure Act," effective **June 9, 2026**, explicitly mandates clear disclosure if commercial advertisements use AI-generated synthetic performers. Concurrently, laws regarding the use of a deceased person's name, image, voice, or likeness also require consent from heirs or executors.
*   **Copyright Disputes** 📚: Copyright issues surrounding AI training data are sparking an increasing number of lawsuits. For instance, **Anthropic** reached a **$1.5 billion** settlement with authors in September 2025, due to its models being trained on copyrighted content. This highlights the substantial legal risks AI companies face and is prompting lawmakers to consider requiring AI developers to disclose the source of their training data.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

Living in this AI era, every one of us will be affected, albeit to varying degrees.

From a personal perspective, AI is becoming more and more like your "digital personal assistant." **Google Gemini**'s "personal intelligence" feature is a prime example. It can securely link your Gmail, Google Photos, Google Drive, and other applications to provide you with highly personalized responses. For instance, if you want to buy a pair of sneakers, it will recommend options based on your past purchase history, preferred brands, and style. Even if you encounter a layover during travel, it can suggest dining options based on your flight information, walking time, and even your dietary preferences. This means that future interactions with AI will no longer be stiff Q&A sessions, but rather like chatting with a friend who truly understands you.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a veteran investigative journalist, I believe that current AI development is at a crucial turning point, shifting from "**pursuing intelligence**" to "**pursuing utility**" and "**pursuing safety**."

In the past, we marveled at the text generation capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs)—they could write poetry, code, and reports. But as **Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis** stated, LLMs might only be one important component of the ultimate AI system, and technological breakthroughs like "World Models," capable of understanding the physical world and taking action, are the "extremely important" links to ensure seamless collaboration in next-generation AI systems. In other words, AI is no longer a "book-smart genius"; it now has to "descend to Earth" to understand the mundane realities of life, and even "earn a living," or risk losing its job!

This shift from "**data monarch**" to "**real-world master**" is precisely the key to bridging AI's "**embodiment gap.**" After all, no amount of textual description can compare to a real touch or an actual operation. For AI to be truly intelligent, it must have the ability to interact with the real world; this is the cornerstone of building genuine understanding.

However, this global AI race also reveals a kind of "**uncontrolled acceleration**." When commercial interests and national competition become the primary drivers, safety and ethical considerations can easily be relegated. **Hassabis's** warning is very direct: if we don't start seriously thinking about and researching AI's risks now, "yesterday" was already too late. This sense of anxiety is not alarmism, because once AI systems gain stronger planning and memory capabilities, they will bring unprecedented opportunities, but also colossal risks.

I've also observed that behind this massive investment boom lies a significant test for infrastructure. The **$600 billion** in AI infrastructure capital expenditure isn't just about chips and servers; it's an extreme challenge to electricity and cooling systems. When **Google** admits its capacity needs to double every six months, we are forced to ponder how long this AI "surge" can be sustained, and where its limits lie.

Finally, this also portends that future legislation on "**digital personality rights**" will become increasingly crucial. As our daily lives become inseparable from AI, and as our digital doppelgangers become increasingly easy to create, how the law defines and protects our digital identity will be a severe challenge that governments worldwide must confront. This will be a difficult process of balancing innovation with protection.

### One-Liner Summary

AI is no longer just a "book-smart genius"; it now has to "earn a living," "learn to be human," or risk losing its job — and even be careful not to "play with fire"!
