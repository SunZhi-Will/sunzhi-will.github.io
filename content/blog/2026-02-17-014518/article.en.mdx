### What Does This Mean for Us?
With AI developing so rapidly, its impact on us, the general public, is truly multifaceted.

Firstly, take **Meta's smart glasses facial recognition**. If this technology truly matures and becomes widespread, your daily life will become incredibly "smart." Imagine walking down the street, and your glasses automatically identify a friend approaching you, even reminding you what topics you discussed last time. This could make social interactions more efficient. But on the flip side, will your facial information be collected? Could it be misused? These are all questions we need to ponder deeply. Our **privacy boundaries** might be redefined, and this will be a significant issue affecting every one of us.

Secondly, the **formulation of AI regulations** is crucial for all AI users and developers. In the future, when you use certain "high-risk" AI applications (such as medical diagnostic AI or autonomous driving systems), these regulations will ensure they are **transparent**, **explainable**, and **safe**. This not only protects our rights but also provides a solid foundation for the healthy development of the AI industry. Without these norms, the AI industry could very likely descend into chaos, even triggering a crisis of public trust.

<Callout type="info" title="AI Governance: Balancing a Double-Edged Sword">
AI governance is like buckling a seatbelt for AI development; it can protect us from potential risks, yet it might also, to some extent, limit the speed of technological innovation. How to strike a balance between safety and innovation is the answer governments and businesses worldwide are striving to find.
</Callout>

Finally, even if AI leads to layoffs in some parts of the tech industry, the **massive investments** by companies in AI and the introduction of new models also represent new opportunities. This AI revolution, much like the Industrial Revolution, will eliminate some older jobs but also create many new jobs and industries we can't even imagine yet. Therefore, what we need to do is learn, adapt, and embrace this change.
