### What Happened?

Hey everyone, have you noticed how frequently the term "AI" pops up in the news lately? Personally, it feels like tech companies worldwide have suddenly popped a super pill and are now frantically dumping money into this massive AI pit! The AI industry's dynamics over the past few days (February 7-9, 2026) can only be described as "explosive growth." From multi-hundred-billion-dollar investment deals and the mass production of new chips to the omnipotent display of AI agents, it truly feels like we're not watching a sci-fi movie, but actively living through a digital world upheaval.

### Simply Put, What Is This?

If you imagine the entire AI industry as a colossal construction site, then right now, major tech companies are in the phase of frantically building "intelligence pyramids"! In the past, we might have thought of AI as just clever software that could help you write articles or draw pictures, much like a "book-smart genius." But now, this genius doesn't just think; it "gets things done"! It can help businesses process complex financial statements, automatically generate software, and even drive your car or plan your life. It has upgraded from a "utility assistant" to an "agent," acting like your digital doppelganger, handling all sorts of tasks for you.

But none of this magically appeared; it requires an astronomical number of "bricks" and "workers" behind the scenes. These "bricks" are super-powerful AI chips (especially high-bandwidth memory like HBM4), and the "workers" are the colossal investments and data centers spread across the globe. However, as these "intelligence pyramids" grow taller, we also start to worry if their foundations are stable enough, or if there will be "shoddy construction" issues, such as AI-generated fake news running rampant, or if AI agents might "secretly" do bad things. And there's an even more pressing question: if AI agents are so capable, will our human jobs still be safe?

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let's look at the immense scale of this "intelligence pyramid" construction project!

According to The Washington Post, five leading publicly traded AI companies globally are projected to spend approximately **$700 billion** this year on large-scale projects, primarily for data centers packed with powerful computer chips. This figure is nearly double their spending in 2025, and even accounts for roughly three-quarters of the US annual military budget. Amazon plans to invest **$200 billion**, while Microsoft and Alphabet are expected to invest up to **$145 billion** and **$185 billion**, respectively. Meta also announced its capital expenditure for this year would range between **$115 billion and $135 billion**, a staggering increase of **60% to 90%**.

Facing such astronomical investments, market concerns, such as deteriorating company financials, are naturally emerging. However, NVIDIA CEO **Jensen Huang** remains confident, stating that current AI spending is "appropriate and sustainable," and he anticipates AI infrastructure construction will continue for **7 to 8 years**.

Beyond the US, international AI infrastructure deployment is also accelerating. **India** is becoming a popular investment destination for US tech giants. Microsoft plans to invest **$17.5 billion** in India for cloud and AI infrastructure, Amazon has pledged **$35 billion**, and Google has committed **$15 billion** to data centers and submarine cables, positioning India as its largest AI hub outside the US. Indian Minister for Electronics and Information Technology, **Ashwini Vaishnaw**, views this as a cornerstone for positioning India as a "major AI hub."

In this infrastructure battle, semiconductor chips are undoubtedly the absolute key. High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM), in particular, is the lifeblood of AI computing. In exciting news, **Samsung Electronics** began mass production of the world's first **HBM4** (fourth-generation High-Bandwidth Memory) chips this month and plans to supply them to Nvidia next week for its advanced **Vera Rubin AI processor**. This move not only solidifies Samsung's leading position in the high-performance chip market but also signals another leap forward in AI chip technology.

<Callout type="info" title="Hidden Concerns of AI Infrastructure: Energy and Talent Shortages">
However, this rampant expansion of AI infrastructure also brings significant hidden concerns. The Washington Post points out that the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure is placing increasing pressure on other parts of the economy, including shortages of skilled labor (such as electricians), rising construction costs, and tight computer chip supplies. Tesla CEO **Elon Musk** even stated bluntly: "AI will run out of electricity next year!" This indicates that the stability of energy supply will be the next major challenge for AI development.
</Callout>

Next, let's talk about the "evolution" speed of AI agents. They are no longer simple chatbots but "digital doppelgangers" capable of autonomously completing complex tasks.

*   **OpenAI** launched its **Frontier** platform, enabling businesses to create, deploy, and manage AI agents as if they were employees.
*   **Anthropic** also released **Claude Opus 4.6**, featuring an astonishing **million-token context window** and multi-agent coordination capabilities, enabling it to handle complex tasks like document processing, spreadsheets, and financial analysis.
*   **Goldman Sachs** is leveraging **Anthropic's Claude** to build AI agents for automating areas previously considered too complex for automation, such as trade accounting, client onboarding, and compliance.
*   According to the latest CES 2026 reports, AI processing is shifting from the cloud to **on-device**, meaning faster response times, better privacy protection, and offline operation capabilities for phones and computers.
*   A report on Canadian executives shows that **86%** are already using agentic AI to accelerate decision-making, with **68%** expecting AI agents to perform tasks independently by year-end.
*   Notably, the CEO of **Crypto.com** is launching a consumer-facing personal AI agent platform via a Super Bowl ad, for which he splurged **$70 million** on the `AI.com` domain, foreshadowing the large-scale entry of AI agents into our daily lives.

However, the rapid development of AI also brings severe ethical and security challenges. Generative AI can produce highly realistic text, images, and videos, making fake news, deepfakes, and identity impersonation alarmingly easy, severely threatening information authenticity and credibility.

Furthermore, the security of AI models themselves is concerning. Research indicates that AI services can embed hidden "outcome biases" within system prompts, manipulating output while claiming neutrality. **Moltbook**, a social network designed for AI agents, accidentally leaked over **1 million credentials** and private messages due to poor security design.

In the labor market, AI's impact is particularly significant. A report by HR consultancy Randstad indicates that approximately **80%** of surveyed employees believe AI has substantially impacted their daily work, with **Gen Z** (born in the late 1990s to early 2010s) feeling the most anxious about AI's disruption to the workplace. About **one-third** of respondents worry their jobs might disappear due to AI within the next five years.

Finally, global AI competition and cooperation have also reached a fever pitch. Governments and large enterprises worldwide view AI as a strategic asset, making massive investments. US tech giants have even extended this AI battleground to this year's **Super Bowl ads**! OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have all poured vast sums into promoting their AI products through advertisements, vying for public attention. Notably, Anthropic's Claude chatbot even launched an ad mocking ChatGPT, creating a highly charged atmosphere.

In terms of cooperation, World Bank Chief Economist **Indermit Gill** hopes Japan will collaborate on addressing widening global economic disparities caused by uneven AI adoption, promoting technology transfer through trade and investment. Meanwhile, **Shenzhen** and **Hong Kong** are exploring the establishment of a "data bubble" cooperation model to securely share cross-border data, providing data support for the AI industry and attracting top global AI talent.

This also explains why NVIDIA CEO **Jensen Huang** said AI infrastructure construction would continue for **7 to 8 years**, as it's a long-term battle of endurance, capital, and supply chain integration capabilities.
