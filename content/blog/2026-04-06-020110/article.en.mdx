### Simply Put, What Is This?
Remember when using AI felt like "ordering from a menu"? You'd tell it what to do, and it would generate some text or an image for you. Now, AI is evolving from merely "taking orders" to becoming a "master chef" capable of "cooking on its own"! This is what we call "Agentic AI." It doesn't just generate content; it understands objectives, plans steps, and even actively executes tasks. Imagine no longer just telling your computer, "Write me a report," but instead saying directly, "Handle this project from start to finish," and the AI, like a highly efficient colleague, goes off to gather data, analyze it, write, and even interact with other tools, ultimately delivering a finished product.

And the foundation for all of this is what we're discussing today: "hardware" and "applications." If AI is a super chef, then hardware is its kitchen equipment, and applications are the dishes it can create. Currently, this equipment is being upgraded, and the menu is becoming richer, but at the same time, we must also consider food safety issues—which, in this context, means AI ethics and security.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let's look at the very "brain" of the AI world—chips. This "core" strategy is truly heating up.

**Topic 1: Competition and Self-Sufficiency in the AI Hardware Supply Chain ⚡**

Chinese AI company **DeepSeek** recently made headlines with reports that its new model, **V4**, might adopt **Huawei** chips. This isn't just ordinary supply chain news; it reflects China's determination and progress toward self-sufficiency in AI computing power. As global chip supply chains face uncertainty due to geopolitical tensions, this "domestication" trend is like equipping one's own kitchen with a full set of self-made appliances, striving to avoid being "choked off." Reports also indicate that DeepSeek V4 has secured massive orders from Chinese tech giants, which will undoubtedly further boost the development and application of China's AI chips.

Meanwhile, memory giant **SK Hynix** hasn't been idle. To counter strong market challenges from **Samsung** and meet the demands of next-generation AI computing platforms like **NVIDIA's Rubin AI platform**, SK Hynix is redesigning its **HBM4** (High Bandwidth Memory Fourth Generation) architecture. This is akin to chefs vying to upgrade their "ingredient storage systems," ensuring that AI master chefs can access vast amounts of data quickly and smoothly, without "stuttering." This fierce competition in the HBM memory market directly impacts the performance and development of future AI models.

**Topic 2: Deepening AI Applications Across Industries and Impact on Work Models 🚀**

AI's reach has extended into corners we never imagined.

In the **biotechnology** sector, AI is accelerating transformation from drug discovery to personalized medicine. While scientists once spent decades searching for new drugs, AI can now rapidly screen millions of compounds, significantly shortening development cycles. This is like equipping drug research with a super microscope and an accelerator, allowing our health and well-being to be realized much faster.

In the **software engineering** world, AI has sparked a "reverse division of labor" revolution. Previously, a software project required multiple people collaborating, with each responsible for a specific segment. Now, with the rise of "**Agentic AI**," a single software engineer, assisted by AI agents, might independently complete work that previously required an entire team. This is like everyone having a superhuman assistant, shifting from traditional "collaborative division of labor" to "one person multi-tasking," dramatically boosting productivity. Furthermore, AI is also being applied in the more sensitive **military domain**. For instance, the U.S. Department of Defense's "**Project Maven**" utilizes AI for target locking, intelligence analysis, and battlefield management. This means AI isn't just playing a role in our daily lives; it's also becoming an indispensable part of national security strategies, sparking profound discussions about its ethical use.

**Topic 3: AI Ethics, Security, Social Risks, and Regulation ⚠️**

However, the rapid development of AI also brings new ethical and social risks.

In terms of **mental health support**, while AI chatbots can offer initial assistance, if users develop excessive identification with the AI, it could, in some situations, reinforce user delusions or misconceptions. This is an ethical issue that requires cautious handling. We cannot treat AI as a panacea; it is merely a tool, and human care and professional diagnosis remain irreplaceable.

Concurrently, the development of **deepfakes (DeepNude AI)** also presents serious technical and ethical risks. Malicious deepfake content can be used to spread fake news, online fraud, and even cause devastating damage to personal reputations. This is like a chef's knife being taken by criminals to commit evil, forcing us to consider how to regulate the use of these tools.

Precisely because of this, governments worldwide are accelerating the advancement of AI-related regulations. These regulations cover requirements for chatbots to disclose their AI identity, protect children from harmful AI content, and even safeguard university employees from the impact of AI technologies. All of this points in one clear direction: the development of AI must proceed within legal and ethical frameworks.

In the realm of **cybersecurity**, the AI arms race between systems is also intensifying. Hackers might use "**data poisoning**" to attack AI models, causing them to make incorrect judgments, or conduct "**jailbreaking**" attacks to bypass AI's safety restrictions, making it perform unintended tasks. This is like someone trying to tamper with a chef's recipe or make the chef prepare forbidden dishes, posing a threat to the stable operation of society as a whole.

Global AI investment has reached record levels. This is like society as a whole casting a vote of confidence in AI as an "emerging industry," hoping for significant returns. Microsoft's investment in AI has surpassed **tens of billions of dollars**, showcasing tech giants' bullish outlook and commitment to AI.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
For us ordinary people, this means many things. First, your way of working might completely change. Repetitive, routine tasks that you previously spent time on might be handled by AI agents in the future. This doesn't mean you'll lose your job; rather, you'll need to learn how to master these AI tools and become an "AI collaboration expert."

Secondly, our lives will become more convenient, but we might also face more privacy and security risks. AI-assisted healthcare will be more precise, yet your personal data might be more easily exploited; AI-generated content will be richer, but information that's hard to distinguish as true or false will also flood the internet. This is like our smartphones becoming increasingly smart, but simultaneously harboring more cybersecurity issues.

> Personally, I feel we are currently standing at a crossroads, with an AI highway ahead that is full of infinite possibilities but also unknown challenges.

### Insider's Deep Analysis
From an industry perspective, this AI "core" strategy and the deepening of its applications are essentially a global reshuffle of technological power. China's efforts to advance AI computing power self-sufficiency are not just for technology but for national strategic security and supply chain resilience. Meanwhile, SK Hynix's collaboration with NVIDIA demonstrates that in the core AI hardware sector, top players are still pursuing extreme performance to meet ever-growing AI demands.

Behind all this, I see AI evolving from its position as a "tool" into an "ecosystem." This ecosystem includes foundational chips, mid-layer models, upper-layer applications, and the surrounding ethical guidelines and legal frameworks. Future competition will not just be about individual products but about the entire ecosystem. Whoever can integrate these elements most effectively will lead in this AI marathon.

AI is no longer the "good child" that only "generates"; it's now ready to "get its hands dirty and execute," and we all need to be prepared to live and work alongside this smarter, more autonomous "digital colleague"!
