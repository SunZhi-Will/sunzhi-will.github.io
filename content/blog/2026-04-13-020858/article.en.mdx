These mansions must not only be large and robust but also provide an endless supply of power and computing capabilities, allowing all AI to "grow happily" there, and giving Meta enough "real estate" to attract more AI talent and applications.

Grok excels at quick responses and complex logical reasoning, with Elon Musk even claiming it will soon surpass existing models. It's no longer just about "rote memorization" but has learned to think logically and "integrate knowledge," instantly slashing operational costs by **100 times** while performing tasks even better – truly the "power-saving guru" of the AI world.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
Speaking of details, I've unearthed quite a bit from various reports.

First, the issues of AI governance and security have truly put the world on edge this time. **Anthropic**'s latest model, **Claude Mythos Preview**, has drawn urgent attention from both US and UK financial regulators due to its powerful cyber attack and defense capabilities. According to the Financial Times, officials from the Bank of England, the Financial Conduct Authority, and the Treasury are holding urgent consultations with the National Cyber Security Centre to assess the potential vulnerabilities **Claude Mythos Preview** could pose to critical information systems. Reuters also reported that US Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** convened a meeting with top Wall Street bankers to discuss the cyber risks presented by this model.

How dangerous is this model? Anthropic itself stated that it found **thousands** of significant vulnerabilities in operating systems, web browsers, and other widely used software, some of which security researchers had failed to notice for decades. For instance, it uncovered a remote crash vulnerability hidden for **27 years** in the highly secure **OpenBSD** system, and another bug in **FFmpeg** that had existed for **16 years** and had gone undetected despite millions of scans by automated tools.

<Callout type="info" title="Project Glasswing: Using AI as a Spear to Attack AI as a Shield">
To counter the potential risks posed by **Claude Mythos Preview**, Anthropic launched "Project Glasswing." This initiative brings together **11 organizations**, including industry giants such as **Amazon Web Services**, **Apple**, **Microsoft**, **Google**, **JPMorganChase**, and **NVIDIA**, allowing them to use the Mythos model to scan and fix vulnerabilities in their own software, hoping to strengthen global cyber defenses before malicious actors gain similar capabilities.
</Callout>

Meanwhile, China has also taken a significant step in AI governance. Five departments, including the Cyberspace Administration of China, the National Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the Ministry of Public Security, and the State Administration for Market Regulation, jointly released the 'Interim Measures for the Management of AI Personified Interactive Services,' effective from **July 15, 2026**. The measures explicitly stipulate that personified interactive services must not generate content that endangers national security or incites subversion of state power, nor should they provide virtual intimate relationships such as virtual relatives or partners to minors. These regulations also emphasize the importance of protecting the rights and personal information security of the elderly, and strictly control ethical issues in content, for instance, prohibiting the generation of content that encourages self-harm, suicide, or induces emotional dependency.

Next, the "money-burning war" of AI industry competition has entered a fever pitch. Tech giant **Meta** announced colossal investments in AI infrastructure, projecting capital expenditures of **$115 billion to $135 billion** in **2026**, nearly double that of **2025**. Its ten-year plan aims to build AI infrastructure on the scale of **tens of gigawatts**, with a long-term goal of **hundreds of gigawatts** or more. This investment includes constructing multiple super data centers within the United States, such as a **$27 billion** financing agreement with **Blue Owl Capital** for a Louisiana data center, and a **$1.5 billion** investment for a data center in El Paso, Texas. Furthermore, Meta signed a **$21 billion** long-term agreement with cloud provider **CoreWeave** to secure more AI computing power. This spending spree is nothing short of an "AI arms race."

In terms of AI model development, **xAI**'s **Grok** model continues to evolve. As of **April 2026**, Grok's latest version is **Grok 4.20 Beta 2**, showing significant improvements in reasoning ability and response speed, and now offering voice mode and speed/quality modes. Elon Musk even stated that Grok's capabilities are rapidly approaching **Claude Opus 4.6** and are expected to surpass its programming ability before **June**. Furthermore, the **Grok Computer agent** will soon enter large-scale public testing, meaning it will transition from a Q&A assistant to an 'automated office system' capable of directly operating computers.

China's **Zhipu AI** also released its new generation open-source model, **GLM-5.1**, officially launched on **April 10**. Its biggest breakthrough is the ability to work continuously on a single task for **8 hours**, achieving autonomous planning, execution, bottleneck identification, and self-evolution, delivering engineering-grade results. In the professional software development benchmark test **SWE-bench Pro**, GLM-5.1 became the first domestic model to surpass **Claude Opus 4.6**, setting a new global record. Zhipu AI also capitalized on this by increasing GLM-5.1's price by **10%**, matching the pricing of leading international vendors like Anthropic for programming scenarios. This signifies that Chinese AI models are shifting from price competition to value competition.

Finally, in terms of new AI technology applications, a research team at **Tufts University** has achieved an exciting breakthrough. They developed a 'neuro-symbolic AI' system that combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning. This technology can reduce AI energy consumption by **up to 100 times** while significantly improving accuracy. For example, in the classic Towers of Hanoi puzzle test, this neuro-symbolic system achieved a success rate of **95%**, compared to only **34%** for traditional systems. Even more surprisingly, when faced with untrained, more complex variants, its success rate remained **78%**, while traditional VLA models failed completely (**0%**). This development is nothing short of a beacon of hope for the immense energy consumption issues facing the AI industry.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
These AI news developments have profound implications for each of us.

Firstly, **cybersecurity** is no longer just an IT department concern. When AI models can easily discover system vulnerabilities, everything from your personal data and bank accounts to critical national infrastructure faces unprecedented threats. But the good news is that AI can also become a defensive tool, just like "Project Glasswing," using AI to combat AI. This cyber offensive-defensive battle has escalated from a "human vs. human" contest to an "AI vs. AI" showdown.

Secondly, government **regulation** of AI will become increasingly stringent worldwide. China's "Management Measures for Personified Interactive Services" is a clear signal that AI is not above the law. From financial security to content ethics, future AI development will operate within a more defined framework. This means that the AI services we use in the future will be safer and more responsible, but may also face certain restrictions, especially for "emotionally interactive" AI that could raise ethical concerns.

Anthropic's **Claude Mythos** demonstrates that AI's ability to understand and exploit complex systems has reached an unprecedented level. We can't just teach AI what is 'right'; we must teach it 'why it's right' and enable it to self-monitor and prevent 'wrongdoing.'
