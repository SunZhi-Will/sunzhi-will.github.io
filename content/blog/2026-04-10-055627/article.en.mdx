### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine your digital life is no longer a jumble of apps acting independently, but rather an omniscient "super-secretary" silently managing everything behind the scenes, exclusively for you. That's the "personal superintelligence" concept Meta's "**Muse Spark**" aims to deliver. It's no longer just a tool that follows your commands; it actively understands your needs and even anticipates your next move.

And Google's "**Gemma 4**"? Think of it like taking a "Michelin-star recipe" previously accessible only to a few top chefs, and making it completely open-source. What's more, it teaches everyone how to use the simplest tools (even a Raspberry Pi can run it!) to whip up a delicious feast at home. This means AI is no longer the exclusive domain of a few tech giants; everyone can participate and build their own smart applications.

As for Anthropic's "**Mythos**," it's more like a "mind-reading" super detective. It can effortlessly uncover vulnerabilities in all mainstream operating systems and web browsers. This capability is so powerful that central banks worldwide are on edge, urgently summoning top bank executives for meetings, fearing that this formidable skill set might fall into the wrong hands.

In short, AI is no longer the distant future we see in sci-fi movies. It's infiltrating every corner of our lives at an astonishing speed, from the phones in our hands to the entire financial system of nations, leaving no stone unturned.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

This AI feast has been a continuous high point. First, **Meta** today unveiled its latest masterpiece, "**Muse Spark**." This model, developed by **Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)**, established last summer, is the first in Meta's "Muse" series, aiming to achieve "personal superintelligence."

The most impressive aspect of "**Muse Spark**" is that it's natively multimodal, meaning it was designed from the ground up to interpret various types of information (like visuals and text), rather than being patched together later. It boasts features such as multimodal perception, parallel sub-agents, health inference, shopping patterns, and visual encoding. It's already integrated into the **Meta AI** application and is expected to be progressively deployed across all Meta products, including **WhatsApp**, **Instagram**, **Facebook**, **Messenger**, and **AI Glasses**, in the coming weeks. Unlike the previously open-sourced **Llama** model, "**Muse Spark**" initially adopted a closed-source strategy, though Meta indicated future open-sourcing is being considered. The market generally views this as a significant milestone marking **Meta**'s renewed push in the AI domain. To support its ambitious AI blueprint, **Meta** also signed a staggering **$21 billion** AI cloud agreement with **CoreWeave**, securing GPU computing power until **2032**.

Compared to earlier **Gemma** models, **Gemma 4** uses an Apache 2.0 license, truly democratizing local AI. This allows developers to deploy offline, multimodal AI applications on servers, mobile phones, and even **Raspberry Pi**, giving them complete local control. Reports indicate that **Gemma 4** excels in performance efficiency, capable of outperforming models 10 times its size with fewer computational resources. It garnered over **10 million** downloads in its first week, demonstrating significant market demand for efficient, easy-to-use AI models.

However, what's most attention-grabbing, and perhaps a bit unsettling, is **Anthropic**'s latest AI model, "**Mythos**." This powerful model can identify and exploit cybersecurity vulnerabilities in "all mainstream operating systems and web browsers." This capability is so formidable that US Treasury Secretary **Scott Bessent** and Federal Reserve Chairman **Jerome Powell** had to urgently convene a meeting with major bank CEOs to warn them about **Mythos** and the cyber risks it could unleash.

"**Mythos**" was released to a limited group of about **40** tech companies (including **Microsoft** and **Google**) and financial institutions (such as **Amazon**, **Apple**, and **JPMorgan Chase**). This marks the first time **Anthropic** has restricted the release scope of one of its models. These companies will utilize **Mythos** for "defensive security work" under an initiative called "**Project Glasswing**," proactively patching critical vulnerabilities in their systems. During testing, **Mythos** has already identified "thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities." Regulators are particularly concerned that if such a model falls into the hands of malicious hackers, it could usher in a new era of cyberattacks.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

This wave of AI isn't just affecting tech giants; it's profoundly changing our lives and work patterns.

For individuals, personal superintelligence assistants like **Meta**'s "**Muse Spark**" could make our digital lives smoother than ever. Imagine your phone and smart glasses acting like mind-readers, understanding your intentions and automatically completing tasks. It's like having a personal butler always on standby, significantly boosting life efficiency. In healthcare, **UnitedHealth Group** introduced its generative AI partner, "**Avery**," designed to provide members with a simpler, more coordinated healthcare experience and enhance the efficiency of customer service staff. Not to be outdone, the insurance industry sees **Hippo**, an MGA company, launch an AI-driven claims workflow. Its **24/7** conversational AI agent is expected to shorten claims processing times and enable existing staff to handle **30-35%** more claims, effectively increasing operational efficiency.

However, AI's impact on the labor market is an issue we must confront. **Gallup**'s **2026 State of the Global Workplace Report** reveals that global employee engagement has plummeted to a five-year low of **20%**. The report notes that despite enterprises investing **$40 billion** in AI, a staggering **95%** of organizations have seen no measurable impact on profits, and **89%** of executives believe AI has not substantially improved labor productivity.

White-collar jobs, in particular, face a significant threat from AI. **Anthropic** CEO **Dario Amodei** even warned in 2025 that AI could replace up to **half of entry-level white-collar jobs** within the next 1 to 5 years, leading to unemployment rates rising to **10-20%**. However, the report also indicates that blue-collar jobs are relatively less affected, as current AI technology still struggles with complex physical labor requiring flexibility and on-site judgment. This might lead to shorter working hours, fostering new opportunities in the "leisure economy."

Enterprises also face numerous challenges when implementing AI. How to scale AI from repetitive tasks to decision-making, how to conduct effective market promotion, and how to foster cross-departmental collaboration are all questions businesses need to ponder. For instance, **Novartis** is integrating AI capabilities into its business applications to improve user experience, insights, and decision-making.

And cybersecurity risk is paramount. The proliferation of generative AI makes cyberattacks more frequent and complex, such as deepfakes and AI-automated attacks. **IBM** research shows that AI-based cyberattacks surged by **44%** in one year. Even more concerning, **77%** of companies lack basic security systems. Chinese authorities have also issued cybersecurity warnings regarding "tokens" (the smallest unit of information) in AI models, noting that "tokens" could be stolen, forged, or tampered with, posing risks to personal privacy, financial security, and even national data security. As of March this year, China's average daily "token" call volume has exceeded **140 trillion**, an increase of over **1,000 times** since early 2024. This alerts us that while AI brings convenience, it also introduces unprecedented challenges for cybersecurity defense.

This is no longer merely about technological leadership; it's a fight for the future gateway to digital life.
