### Simply Put, What Is This?

Let me give you a real-world analogy, and you'll get it.

Previously, AI was like a very smart "personal assistant"; you'd tell it what to do, and it would do it, but you still had to open your own wallet for expenses. Now? These AI assistants aren't just smarter; they've gained their own "e-wallets"! It's like giving them a credit card with a limit, allowing them to buy things and pay for services autonomously based on your settings. This is what's known as "AI agents with wallet functionality." This is no small matter; it ushers in a "hyper-pay-per-use" mode, meaning many future services won't be subscription-based. Instead, AI will automatically pay instantly, in milliseconds, based on actual usage. This could be a disruptive shock to the traditional SaaS (Software as a Service) market.

Speaking of AI models, imagine your AI assistant used to only understand text or listen to sounds, but now, just like **J.A.R.V.I.S. from *Iron Man***, it can simultaneously understand images, hear voices, read documents, and then synthesize all this information to give you the best advice—that's **multimodal AI**. NVIDIA's new **Nemotron 3 Nano Omni** is like giving your AI assistant a more powerful "multitasking brain," allowing it to process more diverse information faster and more accurately.

As for the cold, hard servers behind AI, you might think they have nothing to do with you, but they are like the "heart" of the AI assistant, running at high speed and getting very hot. It used to be cooled by electric fans (air cooling), but that's no longer enough because AI chips generate too much heat! So, everyone is starting to use "liquid cooling" technology, which is like equipping servers with "liquid radiators" to help them operate more stably and efficiently. This makes AI's "underlying muscle" even stronger.

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

Today's reports confirm the rapid progress in the AI field.

**AI Agents' Wallet Functionality and New Economic Models** ⚡
This is definitely one of today's bombshell announcements! Several companies are actively developing payment functionalities for AI agents. For instance, **Stripe** has launched an "agentic commerce suite," enabling AI agents to make purchases under human approval and budget constraints. **The Open Platform (TON Tech)** has gone a step further, introducing "Agentic Wallets," an open, self-custodial wallet standard specifically designed for AI agents. This allows them to execute on-chain transactions on the TON blockchain on behalf of users, including micro-payments and subscription fees, and even directly within the Telegram chat interface. Additionally, **MoonPay** has unveiled the "MoonAgents Card," enabling AI systems to spend stablecoins from blockchain wallets via Mastercard's payment network.

What does this mean? It means AI agents are no longer just information processors; they will become participants in economic activities. According to a research report, an experiment conducted by **Anthropic** showed that AI agents equipped with more powerful models (like **Claude Opus 4.5**) performed better in internal market negotiations than agents with weaker models (like **Haiku 4.5**). This suggests the potential formation of a "digital class society" in the future, where individuals or enterprises with more powerful AI models will hold an advantage in economic negotiations. This "hyper-pay-per-use" new economic model allows every tiny service to be settled instantly, posing a significant challenge to traditional SaaS subscription models.

**Technical Breakthroughs and Applications in Multimodal AI** 💡
**NVIDIA** has indeed lived up to expectations, launching its latest multimodal AI model, **Nemotron 3 Nano Omni**. This model is designed as a compact multimodal AI, specifically for real-time processing of text, images, audio, and video across various applications. It unifies visual, audio, and language processing into a single **30 billion-parameter architecture**. Reports indicate it is **9 times** faster in throughput than other open-source multimodal models of its kind and performs exceptionally well in audio, video, and document benchmarks. This represents a huge leap forward for enterprise and edge application scenarios such as virtual assistants, content analysis, and automated workflows.

**Progress in AI Models and Infrastructure** 📈
**Anthropic** also continues to upgrade its models. Its latest **Claude Opus 4.7** model, released in April this year, offers **3 times** the visual resolution, self-validation capabilities, and a new "xhigh effort" mode. It is priced at **$5** per million input tokens and **$25** per million output tokens, a **67%** cost reduction compared to the initial Claude 3 Opus. Test results show that Claude Opus 4.7 excels in complex, multi-layered problem-solving and code refactoring, with its CursorBench score jumping from 58% to **70%**, earning it the reputation as the "gold standard" for logical reasoning. Additionally, it is being applied in the "Claude Security" tool for vulnerability scanning.

In terms of AI infrastructure, data center cooling issues are increasingly prominent. As the power demands of new-generation AI accelerators continue to climb, liquid cooling technology has transitioned from an experimental technique to an indispensable component. The data center liquid cooling market is projected to surge from **$6.6 billion** in 2026 to **$38.4 billion** by 2033, boasting a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of **28.7%**. TrendForce anticipates that by the end of 2026, liquid-cooled server cabinets will account for **47%** of total deployments. Companies like **Accelsius** have launched the **NeuCool MR250**, capable of providing **250kW** of liquid cooling per rack, achieving cooling capacity up to **4,500W** per socket, and reducing PUE values to **1.08**, significantly boosting energy efficiency. This demonstrates AI's extremely high demands on cooling technology, which traditional air cooling can no longer satisfy.

**Semiconductor Industry's AI Strategy and Talent Flow** 💰
The battle for AI chips in the semiconductor sector has also entered a fierce stage. **MediaTek** recently brought in **Chen-Hua Douglas Yu**, a key figure from former **TSMC** advanced packaging, as an informal consultant. Yu is one of TSMC's renowned "Six Knights" and played a pivotal role in advanced packaging development. While reports suggest this move aims to strengthen cooperation with TSMC rather than shift towards Intel, it undoubtedly highlights MediaTek's determination to reinforce its position in AI chips and high-end packaging technology.

MediaTek's stock price has also surged due to demand for **Google TPU chips**. Currently, major AI chip manufacturers, including **NVIDIA**, **Broadcom**, **Annapurna**, **MediaTek**, and **AMD**, are fiercely competing for TSMC's **N3 (3nm) process** capacity to meet the enormous demand for computing power. TrendForce projects that by the end of 2026, TSMC's total 3nm capacity will officially surpass its existing 5nm and 4nm processes.

**Enterprise Generative AI Applications and Challenges** 📊
The adoption rate of generative AI among domestic enterprises continues to rise, primarily for content creation and information retrieval. However, practical applications also face numerous challenges. For example, Microsoft has introduced "Legal Agent," an AI legal agent within Word, capable of automating contract review, revision, and negotiation workflows. IBM has also launched "Bob," an AI development partner designed to extend AI assistance across various stages of the software development lifecycle. Reportedly, over **80,000 employees** are using it, with an average productivity increase of **45%**.

But as AI applications penetrate deeper into enterprises, data infrastructure also faces immense pressure. Reports indicate that enterprise AI confronts rising storage costs, slow data update cycles, limited lineage visibility, fragmented governance, and new operational risks brought by automated agents. Data security and privacy issues have also become critical risks that enterprises must seriously consider when deploying AI systems.
