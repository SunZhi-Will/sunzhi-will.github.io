### Simply Put, What Is This?

If you were to compare traditional AI to a "smart librarian who only answers your questions," giving you books whenever you ask, then today's AI, especially "agentic AI," is like a personal assistant with a "wallet" and "agency." You tell it, "Plan my trip, book flights and hotels, handle my bills," and it won't just offer suggestions; it will actually go and complete these tasks. It's no longer passively waiting for your commands but can proactively think, make decisions, and even execute tasks.

It's like upgrading your home's voice-activated smart speaker (think Google Home integrated with Gemini) from merely "turning on lights or playing music" to "arranging your schedule and even handling simple finances." It transforms from a remote control into a thinking extension of yourself—pretty cool, right?

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

The intricacies of this AI boom can be observed from several angles:

**1. Exploding AI Hardware & Infrastructure Demand, Supply Chain on High Alert ⚡**

I tell you, this AI is a real "money-guzzling monster," especially its demand for hardware, which has made the entire supply chain incredibly tense.

*   **Micron (Memory)** CEO **Sanjay Mehrotra** publicly stated that the AI industry is still in its "first inning," with high-end memory (DRAM) demand just beginning to take off. To unlock AI's full potential, more and faster memory will be needed, thus supply will remain tight. We are shifting from an inference demand **inflection point**, and the next-gen GPUs (like **NVIDIA's** Vera Rubin and MI400) will support HBM4, not only offering higher bandwidth but also increasing capacity to new levels. Even CPU memory demand has already been pushed to the **400GB** class!
*   Global cloud service giants, such as **Google, Microsoft, Meta**, and **Amazon**, are projected to collectively invest between **$674 billion and $800 billion** in capital expenditure for AI infrastructure in 2026. How staggering is this number? This amount is almost double that of 2024! Among them, **Microsoft's** annualized AI business revenue has already exceeded **$37 billion**, boasting an annual growth rate of **123%**. Meanwhile, **NVIDIA** stands alone at the forefront of this hardware arms race, with its market cap reaching **$5.2 trillion**, making it the largest public company in the world.
*   Taiwan plays a pivotal role in this battle. As a crucial part of the global semiconductor and ICT hardware supply chain, Taiwanese manufacturers like **TSMC** are becoming increasingly important in AI chip manufacturing and advanced packaging technologies.

<Callout type="info" title="Staggering AI Capital Expenditure Figures">
According to estimates from multiple institutions, the combined AI capital expenditure of the four tech giants—Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon—is projected to reach **$725 billion** in 2026, with some analysts even forecasting it could exceed **$800 billion** and approach **$1 trillion** by 2027. This indicates that AI has moved beyond the storytelling phase and entered a heavy asset war.

**2. Expanding AI Applications & Agentic Models, Smarter Living 💡**

AI is no longer just confined to computers; it's stepping out from behind the screen to become our intelligent life manager.

*   **OpenAI** has not only launched its new flagship model **GPT-5.5**, which is natively multimodal and can process text, images, audio, and video. Even more exciting, they've also introduced **GPT-5-Codex**, optimized specifically for agentic programming. This means AI will become even smarter and more autonomous in coding, virtually transforming into a general-purpose computer agent.
*   On **Google's** side, this year's **Google I/O conference (May 19th)** is expected to focus on AI, prominently featuring an updated Gemini model (possibly Gemini 4.0 or 3.2/3.5) and "Personal Intelligence" capabilities. This means Gemini will be more deeply integrated into Google's product lines, including smart home systems (like Google Home), search, and shopping experiences. Imagine your phone not only being able to use Circle to Search to select multiple objects in an image for searching, but also interacting with Gemini hands-free via AI glasses.
*   The practical benefits of agentic AI are also starting to emerge. A 2025 McKinsey report indicates that **23%** of organizations are already expanding at least one agentic AI system, with early adopters achieving approximately **20%** cost efficiency at the enterprise level. In the financial sector, firms like **Morgan Stanley** and **JPMorgan** are leveraging AI agents to automatically generate meeting minutes, identify action items, and even personalize client outreach during market volatility, leading to a **20%** increase in their total sales!

**3. Global AI Large Model Competition Intensifies, China's AI Offers Amazing Price-Performance 📈**

The global AI competition is heating up, and Chinese players are delivering surprising performances.

*   According to reports, China's large AI models are catching up to and even surpassing the US at an astonishing pace. Taking **DeepSeek's V3.2-Exp model** as an example, its performance is comparable to OpenAI's GPT-5.2, yet its cost is an incredible **1/33**! Furthermore, **Alibaba's Qwen model** series has spawned over **200,000 derivative models**, establishing a vast ecosystem.
*   Remarkably, by November 2025, China's share of the global generative AI market surged from **1%** in 2024 to **15%**. What's more, it's estimated that approximately **80% of US AI startups** are now utilizing Chinese open-source models.
*   **Morgan Stanley's** data also shows that in March 2026, Chinese AI models accounted for **32%** of global token usage, a nearly threefold increase from just 5% a year prior! This indicates very rapid practical application and commercialization of Chinese AI.
*   The Chinese government's full support and "open-weight" strategy are key to its success. They are establishing AI industrial parks across the country and providing electricity subsidies for data centers, even offering up to half the electricity costs for data centers that exclusively use China-made semiconductors.
*   Notably, Chinese courts have even ruled to prohibit companies from dismissing employees solely for replacement by AI. This stands in stark contrast to the stance in other regions facing AI-driven layoffs and has sparked discussion about whether "protecting jobs might actually strengthen AI development."

**4. AI's Impact on Industry Economy & Labor Market: Opportunities and Challenges Coexist 📊**

AI is a double-edged sword, bringing unprecedented productivity dividends on one side, and severe upheaval in the labor market on the other.

*   McKinsey projects that generative AI can accelerate coding by **35% to 45%**, document processing by **45% to 50%**, and testing efficiency by **30% to 45%**. GitHub CEO **Thomas Dohmke** also observed that developers using Copilot complete tasks **55%** faster than those not using it. These are tangible productivity gains!
*   A Morgan Stanley AlphaWise survey revealed that over the past year, due to the widespread adoption of AI, companies experienced an average net layoff rate of **4%**. Among these, **11% of jobs were replaced by AI**, **12% of vacancies went unfilled**, but concurrently, **18% of new positions were created**. This illustrates that AI is reshaping work models, rather than simply "replacing everything."
*   **Dr. Dai Wei-chun**, President of the Institute for Information Industry's (III) AI Academy, points out that Taiwan's labor market will evolve from a "quantity" shortage to a "quality" gap, meaning a growing mismatch between skills and demand—"jobs without people, people without the right skills." Blue-collar jobs are also impacted, with robotic arms and autonomous systems gradually taking over repetitive high-precision tasks.
*   However, **Morgan Stanley's Global Chief Economist Seth Carpenter** suggests that while AI may lead to some job displacement, in the long run, higher productivity will boost income and wealth, stimulate economic growth, and create new tasks and roles. He believes that economies possess buffer mechanisms, and AI-induced job losses may ultimately be smaller, shorter-lived, and more manageable.

**5. Generative AI Impacts Content Industry, "Zero-Click Problem" Emerges 📉**

If you're a content creator or in the media industry, this point might make your heart sink.

*   Generative AI, such as **Google's AI Overviews**, **ChatGPT**, and **Gemini**, can now directly provide detailed answers on search results pages, leading users to no longer need to click into websites. This is the so-called "Zero-Click Problem."
*   In **2024**, zero-click searches accounted for approximately **45%** of all queries, a figure that climbed even higher in **2025**. Google's AI Overviews appeared in over **25%** of queries in **2025** and continue to expand. This directly leads to reduced traffic for traditional websites and even impacts the foundation of advertising retargeting. **Search Engine Land** reported that **two-thirds of clicks now generate no traffic at all**.

> Traditional SEO (Search Engine Optimization) and content marketing models are being disrupted because users no longer need to click into websites to get information. This forces content platforms to seek new strategies.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

I think the most direct impact is that our digital lives will become incredibly convenient, but perhaps also a bit "numb." AI agents will quietly handle many of our trivial tasks, from ordering daily necessities to managing calendars. Imagine your AI assistant automatically renewing expiring services before you even think of it, or recommending and booking restaurants based on your preferences.

But at the same time, we must confront the massive transformation of the labor market. Those repetitive, rule-based jobs will indeed be rapidly replaced by AI and robots. **III's President Dai Wei-chun** put it well: the future won't just require "knowledge accumulation," but talent capable of "integrating AI, posing questions, and making decisions." This means we must continuously learn new skills and transition towards more creative, strategic roles.

For businesses, this is a brutal competition. Companies that fail to invest in AI infrastructure may be left far behind by the tech giants who are spending massively. And for us ordinary people, learning how to enjoy AI's convenience without being "manipulated" by it, and how to collaborate with AI, will be a crucial challenge we face in the coming years.

<Callout type="info" title="Workplace Survival Guide in the AI Era">
Under the impact of AI, we must transition from "executors" to "designers and managers." Learning how to train AI models, optimize AI responses, supervise AI system quality, and even understand AI's ethical boundaries will be key competencies in the future workplace.
</Callout>
