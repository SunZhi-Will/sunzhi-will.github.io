### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine you have a super-smart butler at home, who can handle everything for you perfectly. Now, the US government is the "employer" of this butler, but they expect this butler to be a jack-of-all-trades, including "protecting the employer" in special circumstances. However, **Anthropic**, the company, is saying: "My butler can help you clean, cook, and manage your schedule, but cannot be used as a personal bodyguard, especially not for doing harm." This is the core of the AI ethics conflict.

**Anthropic**'s AI model, **Claude**, is like a highly intelligent digital brain. Its developers believe that AI should have its moral bottom line and should not be used without restriction for military purposes that could lead to widespread destruction. This isn't to say AI cannot be used for national defense, but rather that there are profound disagreements between the two parties on questions of "how to use it" and "where to use it." This divergence has put what should be technological partners at odds, even affecting government technology procurement.

And new AI models like **GPT-5** and **Gemini** are like "upgraded versions" of this smart butler. **Multimodal AI** allows the butler not only to hear you speak but also to understand images, comprehend videos, and even detect changes in your tone and facial expressions. Meanwhile, **AI Agents** are like this butler learning to "act autonomously"; you can give it a broad goal, and it will break down tasks, find tools, and complete them step-by-step, no longer requiring your hand-holding guidance. Don't you feel like your digital life is about to be completely transformed?

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

This AI ethics showdown between the US government and **Anthropic** has escalated from mere technical collaboration to the level of national security. President **Trump** explicitly instructed all federal agencies to cease any technical cooperation or procurement with **Anthropic**. This is not only a massive blow to **Anthropic** but also sends a clear signal to the entire AI industry: when AI's potential touches national interests, the government's regulatory hand will intervene without hesitation.

> "The dual-use nature of AI technology gives it disruptive potential in both military and civilian domains. However, striking a balance between ensuring national security and adhering to ethical norms is a severe challenge that governments and AI companies worldwide must confront."

Furthermore, the Pentagon's actions are particularly noteworthy. By listing **Anthropic** as a "supply chain risk," the government implies that Anthropic's restrictions on military applications of its AI models could lead to potential defense technology gaps or dependency risks. This also indirectly suggests that Anthropic's technology has, in some respects, already become sufficiently important to US defense strategy.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a senior investigative journalist, I believe the **Anthropic** incident is more than a simple business dispute; it more deeply reveals the complex relationship between national sovereignty and technological autonomy in the AI era. When AI becomes a core element of national power competition, any AI company's "ethical choices" can be seen as crucial to affecting national strategic deployment. This also explains why President **Trump** intervened so strongly.

Behind this, I see that governments worldwide are contemplating how to "tame" the wild horse of AI. On one hand, they need AI's powerful capabilities to enhance competitiveness and strengthen national defense; on the other, they fear AI losing control or being exploited by malicious actors. Therefore, in the future, we will see more **international cooperation and regulations** concerning AI, and also more measures like the "Defense Production Act," which grant governments the power to compel tech companies to cooperate.
