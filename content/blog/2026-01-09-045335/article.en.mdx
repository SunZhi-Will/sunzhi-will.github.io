### What Happened?

Today (January 9, 2026), the AI world is simply a "multi-front battle"! Besides our familiar AI assistants getting smarter, they're now even "physicalizing" from the virtual world to walk among us. From major nations scrambling to set AI rules, to companies racing to develop "running and jumping" robots, and a Chinese AI startup shining brightly on the Hong Kong stock market, market funds are still brimming with enthusiasm for AI. ### Simply Put, What Is This?

Today's AI news revolves around several core concepts. Let's explain them with some brilliant metaphors:

1.  **"Physical AI" and "Embodied Intelligence"**: Imagine that previously, AI was like a **super-smart strategist**, only responsible for thinking and strategizing. It could write articles, draw pictures, and analyze data, but it couldn't actually "do" anything. The "physical AI" and "embodied intelligence" now being discussed are like that strategist not only having ideas but also being able to command a group of **"soldiers that can walk, run, and carry things."** These soldiers are AI-powered robots that can understand the physical world and actually execute tasks, such as assembling products in a factory or delivering goods to your doorstep. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang said at CES 2026 that this is the next wave of AI. It's like we mentioned before, your "AI intern" is truly about to [physicalize](/blog/2026-01-08-011829)!

2.  **"Computing Power" Infrastructure**: This is like the AI world's **super power plants and highways**. AI needs vast electricity and computing power to perform calculations and learn. "East Data West Computing" is the Chinese government's strategy to balance resources by having the data computing demands of the east handled by the energy-rich west, much like building a city's power plant in a remote area and then transmitting electricity back to the city via high-voltage lines. In this "AI power race," even Tesla CEO Elon Musk said that China has an advantage in power expansion and will "greatly surpass" other countries in AI computing power.

3.  **"AI Agents" and "Super Apps"**: If you consider AI a versatile toolkit, then "AI Agents" are the **tools in the toolbox that can act on their own**. They don't just wait for your command; they can proactively assess, plan, and complete a series of complex tasks, much like your ["errand boy" gradually evolving into a "presidential assistant"](/blog/2026-01-03-011320). "Super apps," on the other hand, are like **one app that handles everything**, integrating various AI functions so you no longer need to switch between different applications. For example, Google has integrated AI features into Gmail, allowing your inbox to summarize emails and suggest replies.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

For "ordinary people who don't understand code" like us, these advancements mean:

*   **Life will be more convenient, but also potentially more "AI-surrounded"**: From your smart home appliances to mobile apps, AI will be ubiquitous, making your life smarter and more personalized. TCL's collaboration with Microsoft is an example; your [smart glasses](/blog/2026-01-08-011829) might know you better than you know yourself. But this also means your data footprint will be more diverse and extensive.
*   **Workplace reshuffle; evolve or be eliminated**: AI is no longer just a chatbot; it will genuinely enter factories and offices to perform tasks. Previously, "knowing how to code" was an advantage; now, even "collaborating with AI" has become a basic skill. If your job involves highly repetitive, rule-based tasks, you really need to start thinking about how to upgrade your "AI housekeeper" skills, otherwise, your job might not be safe. As we mentioned in [AI Battle Royale: Robot Interns on Duty, OpenAI Secretly Building Hardware ‚Äì Is Your Job Safe?](/blog/2026-01-04-012521), skill upgrading is already urgent.
*   **"Physical AI" will change our world**: When robots can perceive, understand, and operate in the physical world autonomously, they won't just be in factories; they might enter your home, stores, and hospitals. This will be the moment when AI truly "grows limbs" after the digital revolution. In the future, your delivery person might be a robot, and most factory work will be done by robots.
*   **International competition intensifies; are we spectators or participants?**: The competition for AI governance and computing infrastructure is a national-level affair. China is actively promoting global AI governance standards and demonstrating ambition in computing power. This not only affects technological development but could also impact global trade and geopolitics.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

From these latest developments, the AI industry is undergoing a profound transformation from "software-defined" to "hardware and physical world collaboration."

First, **"physical AI" and "embodied intelligence" are the next battlegrounds.** Nvidia's Jensen Huang's prophecy is not groundless; when AI evolves from merely processing information as a "brain" to having a "body" that can actually act, the economic value it creates will be incomparable to digital AI. The actions of Hyundai, RealMan Robotics, and UniX AI all indicate that robots will become the most direct outlet for AI applications, especially in labor-scarce manufacturing. This will be the ultimate solution for **"cost reduction and efficiency increase."** Investors should not only focus on computing chips themselves but also on hardware, sensors, robot platforms, and related data training services that can connect AI with the physical world.

Second, **the competition for computing infrastructure extends from chips to "electricity."** Musk's "electricity theory," while perhaps exaggerated, indeed points out a critical bottleneck in AI development. The enormous energy consumption required for training and deploying large AI models is making electricity supply and cooling technology new strategic resources. China's "East Data West Computing" strategy and its advantage in power generation provide it with unique leverage in the AI computing race. For Taiwan's supply chain, in addition to chips, data centers, power management, green energy, and other related industries will also see new opportunities. Iluvatar CoreX's success in Hong Kong's stock market also illustrates the huge potential of domestic chips in specific markets, especially with national strategic support.

Furthermore, **the commercialization logic of AI applications is changing.** In recent years, corporate investment in AI has primarily focused on "cost savings," but Thoughtworks' survey shows that 77% of large enterprises globally have shifted their AI application focus to "growth and innovation," valuing AI's ability to drive revenue growth, improve products, and open new markets. This means that AI's value is no longer just about improving efficiency but has become a core competitiveness and growth engine for enterprises. Enterprise-level intelligent agents and consumer-facing "super apps" will be two important directions. Whoever can integrate AI functions most seamlessly and understand users best will stand out in the market.

Finally, **the structural impact of AI on the workforce cannot be ignored.** The research revealing "hidden workload" and stagnant wages is a warning sign for businesses and policymakers. If AI merely makes employees more tired and anxious without commensurate rewards, it will have negative consequences. When implementing AI, companies must re-evaluate job responsibilities, quantify "hidden workload," and consider how to redistribute value to ensure that AI's development leads to **"human-machine collaboration for mutual benefit"** rather than "AI exploitation." This also reminds us that the education system must accelerate its transformation to cultivate a new generation of workers equipped with AI collaboration skills, as emphasized in [2026 Is Not the Year of AI Explosion, But the First Wave of Agentic AI Project Elimination](/blog/2026-01-09-012800); AI literacy will become a new workplace prerequisite.
