- The global AI infrastructure race is intensifying, with innovation and regulation progressing concurrently.

### What Happened?
Hey, tech enthusiasts! The AI world has been absolutely thrilling and electrifying these past few days, like a high-stakes drama series! My personal take is that this AI boom isn't slowing down; instead, it's accelerating wildly in every direction. From the wearable devices deeply intertwined with our daily lives, to infrastructure investments easily hitting tens of billions of dollars, and even to copyright disputes affecting creators' livelihoods—every day brings stories that are either astonishing or profoundly thought-provoking. Today, a major piece of news from the publishing world especially made me realize that AI's reach has extended into realms we never even imagined: literary creation and the ethical boundaries behind it.

### Simply Put, What Is This?
Perhaps you feel that AI, being such an an advanced concept, seems a bit distant from us? No, my friend, it's infiltrating every corner of your life at an unimaginable pace. Imagine this: smart glasses, once a 'sci-fi' notion, are now, thanks to rapid AI advancements, moving from your pocket to your face, just like your phone, becoming your personal 'AI assistant.' It's like your personal secretary suddenly 'materialized' as a high-tech accessory on your face, ready to help you with anything—that's what we call the 'Face Revolution'!

On the other hand, as AI, like a super glutton, continuously learns and evolves, it consumes massive amounts of 'data food.' The current problem is that these 'high-quality' data sources are quickly running out—this is dubbed the '2026 Problem.' It's like your pet loving snacks so much that it ate all the provisions in the fridge. You'd have to find new, tasty treats to replenish its nutrition, right? Otherwise, how will it grow big and smart?

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows
Today's (2026-03-22) most striking news is undeniably the announcement by publishing giant **Hachette** to withdraw **Mia Ballard's** novel. The reason? AI content controversy! This utterly shocked me, as it signifies that AI-generated content has reached a level capable of causing confusion and even commercial disputes. This isn't merely a technical issue; it's a microcosm of ethical, legal, and copyright challenges. It also reminds us that as AI gains the ability to 'create,' the value and definition of human originality will face unprecedented scrutiny.

<Callout type="info" title="Copyright Warning for AI-Generated Content">
Hachette's withdrawal of Mia Ballard's novel marks a serious statement from the publishing industry regarding AI-generated content. It warns creators and publishers that while embracing AI tools, they must handle content sources and originality with extreme caution, lest they face severe trust crises and legal repercussions.
</Callout>

Amidst the global AI boom, Taiwan, as a critical component of the AI supply chain, is entering an unprecedented 'super cycle.' Particularly in areas like **AI chips**, **AI servers**, **High Bandwidth Memory (HBM)**, and **optical communication**, Taiwan plays a pivotal role. Seeing these figures, I can't help but think this is not only Taiwan's opportunity but also an indispensable part of global AI development.

Concurrently, major international investments have not ceased. Japan's **SoftBank** is reported to have poured a massive **80 trillion JPY** (approximately **53.5 billion USD**) into establishing AI data centers in the United States. This astronomical investment clearly tells us how immense and urgent the global demand for AI infrastructure is. It's like building a super city; you first need sufficiently strong rebar and concrete, along with utilities like water and electricity, right? Data centers are the 'utilities' of the AI world.

Meanwhile, the AI smart glasses market is also experiencing a 'Face Revolution'! Thanks to breakthroughs in **generative AI** and **multimodal technology**, AI smart glasses are no longer the feature-limited products of the past; they are becoming smarter and more practical. By **2026**, global shipments of AI smart glasses are projected to reach **9.5 million units**. This astounding figure indicates that AI applications are shifting from the bulky cloud to sleek end-user devices, much like how mobile phones evolved from 'bricks' to today's 'smart terminals'.

On the policy and regulatory front, governments worldwide are also hard at work. The US White House has proposed a unified AI legislative framework, hoping to establish nationwide safety measures to address potential biases in AI models, protect children's online safety, and prevent the chaos of fragmented state-level regulations. Concurrently, China has also regulated AI-generated portions within short video content. This demonstrates that governments are striving to put a bridle on AI, this runaway horse, ensuring it gallops forward without harming society. Our previous [AI Daily] has also frequently discussed the importance of regulation, for instance, [AI Daily: AI Melee Week – Chips, Policy, and Ethics, Who Wins?](/blog/2026-03-21-012802).

Finally, regarding the '2026 Problem'—the challenge that high-quality text data might **deplete by 2026**—this is an issue that deeply concerns me. If AI runs out of 'food,' how can it continue to learn and evolve? This will compel the industry to seek new data sources, develop smaller language models, and even promote the advancement of autonomous AI.

### What Does This Mean for Us?
For us ordinary people, what does AI's development signify? Firstly, your next tech gadget might very well be a pair of incredibly advanced **AI smart glasses**. They could help you take photos, navigate, and even translate in real-time, making your life far more convenient. This will transform how we interact with the digital world, much like how smartphones changed everything back in the day.

Secondly, AI's impact on the job market is also different from what we might imagine. You might worry about AI taking your job, but a Japanese survey indicates that AI adoption has actually prompted more companies to increase their demand for digitally skilled personnel, rather than reducing headcount. It's as if AI becomes your 'super tool-person,' allowing you to focus more energy on higher-value, more creative tasks. This also echoes the 'AI agent' trend we discussed in [AI Daily: AI Agents Launch Full Assault! ### Insider's Deep Analysis
From my perspective, this series of AI news reveals several core trends:

1.  **Infrastructure First**: SoftBank's colossal investment demonstrates that no matter how flashy AI applications become, the underlying **computing power** and **data centers** remain hotly contested territory. Whoever controls these 'highways' will gain the upper hand in the AI race. This also connects to our earlier discussion in [AI Daily: A New Chapter in the AI Melee: Computing Power Surges, Robots Rise, AI Agents Steal Jobs!](/blog/2026-03-19-015031).
2.  **Edge AI Rises**: The explosive growth of smart glasses proves the immense potential of combining **edge AI** and **generative AI**. AI is no longer confined to the cloud; it's entering your eyes, becoming an extension of yourself. This is a truly 'immersive' AI experience.
3.  **Data Dilemma and Transformation**: The '2026 Problem' is a warning sign that will force the AI industry to re-evaluate data's value, acquisition methods, and even spur the development of smaller models and efficient training techniques. It's like oil eventually running out; we must find new, clean energy sources.
4.  **Ethics and Regulation Playing Catch-Up**: The White House's legislative framework and Hachette's copyright dispute both illustrate that technological development far outpaces legal and ethical norms. We need faster, more agile regulatory mechanisms to ensure AI develops healthily. This is also an issue we emphasized in [AI Daily: AI Melee Week – Chips, Policy, and Ethics, Who Wins?](/blog/2026-03-21-012802).

Behind all this, I see a clear sign that the AI industry is transitioning from a 'technology showcase' phase to one of 'application implementation' and 'social integration.' This path will have many pitfalls, but also countless opportunities.

### One-Liner Summary
AI is no longer just a 'book-smart genius'; it now has to 'don smart glasses' and venture into the world, while simultaneously 'learning to play by the rules,' or else the 'tasty data' will run out, and then no one can save this gravy train!
