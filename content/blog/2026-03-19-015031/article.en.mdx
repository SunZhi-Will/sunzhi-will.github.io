It's like the coffee shop near my house: whenever they launch a limited new flavor, more people queue up, and the owner will definitely consider raising prices. From what I've seen, both **Alibaba Cloud** and **Baidu AI Cloud** have had to adjust AI compute prices upwards due to explosive growth in AI demand. This is undoubtedly a heavy burden for companies needing vast compute resources to train large models, but it also reflects the industry's intense fervor.

<Callout type="info" title="Why are compute costs rising?">
  Simply put, as more and more companies and research institutions invest in the development and application of large AI models, the demand for compute resources like GPUs (Graphics Processing Units) used for training and operation skyrockets. When supply can't keep up with demand, prices naturally go up. This also fuels greater competition among chip giants, with NVIDIA being one of the biggest beneficiaries.
</Callout>

### Simply Put, What Is This?

If previous AI was a "paper-pusher" scholar, then today's AI is practically a "hands-on" artisan! Before, when we talked about AI, it was mostly software-level applications like voice assistants or image recognition, which felt a bit distant from our daily lives. But now, with the rapid development of "**physical AI**" and "**embodied intelligence**," AI is no longer just code on a screen; it's starting to have a body and actively do things.

Imagine that robot at home that only sweeps the floor. Now, it doesn't just sweep; it can learn to toss dirty clothes into the washing machine or even make you a cup of coffee! That's the charm of embodied intelligence. It can understand the physical world, make judgments, and execute tasks through a robot's body. Just like humans, AI is evolving from the stage of 'acquiring knowledge' to 'applying knowledge'.

AI Agents have become a hot topic, with this technology being deeply applied across various industries, such as:

*   **Smart Cities**: AI Agents can coordinate traffic signals and monitor public safety, making city operations more efficient.
*   **Human Resources Management**: From resume screening to employee training, AI Agents can automate processes, significantly enhancing HR efficiency.

However, like any high-tech advancement, AI also brings new challenges. The "**AI poisoning**" incident exposed by **CCTV's '3·15' Gala** sent shivers down everyone's spine. Simply put, malicious actors 'fed' AI models with incorrect or harmful data, causing the AI to make erroneous judgments or produce inappropriate content after learning. This not only affected data quality but also triggered serious ethical and security concerns, emphasizing the importance of "**data governance**" to prevent AI from falling into a vicious cycle of "**recursive pollution**."

### What Does This Mean for Us?

I think the most direct impact is that our lives and work will become increasingly inseparable from AI, and it will be a 'proactive' kind of AI. Before, AI was a tool we picked up and used; now, AI is like a smart colleague that actively offers suggestions and even helps you complete part of your work.

*   **For individuals**: You might find your smartphone getting smarter, proactively reminding you of important tasks today or helping you book a restaurant. But at the same time, you'll also need to start considering whether your digital footprint and privacy could be overused by these smart AIs. Just like the AI poisoning incident, if our personal data is maliciously exploited, the consequences could be unimaginable.

*   **For the workplace**: Some highly repetitive, rule-based jobs are very likely to be replaced by AI Agents. This isn't alarmism; it's forcing us to think about how to enhance our own indispensability. Personally, I believe creativity, critical thinking, and emotional intelligence will be even more crucial soft skills for humans in the future workplace.

*   **For industries**: Businesses will face immense transformation pressure. Companies that can first embrace AI and deeply integrate it into their business processes will gain a significant competitive advantage. However, blindly following trends while neglecting data governance and ethical security could lead to self-destruction.

This is also why I've consistently emphasized that we must maintain a 'cautious optimism' towards AI. It can bring immense convenience and efficiency, but it's also a double-edged sword that requires our careful handling.

Imagine how much an autonomously learning, self-deciding AI Agent could do in your company? This will be a leap in efficiency and a reshaping of industrial structure.

Concurrently, China elevating 'AI+' to a national strategy reflects the extreme importance major global powers place on AI development. However, while pursuing speed, addressing **data security** and **ethical governance** will be severe challenges that all nations must confront. Finally, judging by the fluctuations in AI-themed fund net values, the market is continually adjusting its expectations for AI.
