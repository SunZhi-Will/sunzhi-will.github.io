### What Happened?

Oh boy, tech enthusiasts, the AI sector today is absolutely buzzing, like a high-speed tech carnival! From the chatbots we use daily becoming more "understanding" of you, to the walking, moving physical robots gaining stronger "hearts," AI's reach is extending into every corner of our lives at an astonishing pace. But simultaneously, this frenzy is accompanied by some profound issues, such as where exactly the ethical red line for AI lies. And even more surprisingly, the construction plans for the "digital brains" that underpin all of this—the data centers—have hit a snag. I feel like we're standing at the threshold of a new AI era, filled with anticipation, yet needing to remain constantly vigilant.

### Simply Put, What Is This?

Imagine your phone is no longer just a mere "obedient tool" but begins to act like a thoughtful personal assistant, not only helping you manage emails and organize photos but also proactively offering suggestions based on your lifestyle, just like a real friend. That's precisely what Google Gemini's "personal intelligence" feature is striving to do.

And OpenAI's GPT-5.4 preview is like giving AI the ability for "interruptible thinking." Previously, chatting with AI was like talking to a smart person with a one-track mind; you had to wait for them to finish their entire thought before you could interject. With the GPT-5.4 Thinking mode, you can interrupt it anytime to adjust its direction, just like discussing a project with a colleague, significantly boosting efficiency. It’s akin to instructing a chef and, if you notice they're cutting the wrong ingredient, you can immediately correct them instead of waiting for the entire dish to be made incorrectly before complaining.

Even cooler, if AI used to be a "virtual brain," it now has ambitions for a "physical body." **Samsung SDI** today announced it will showcase a **full solid-state battery** prototype designed for "physical AI." This might sound abstract, but you can think of it this way: older robots were like vacuum cleaners plugged into a wall, unable to go far. Now, with this high-energy-density, high-safety solid-state battery, future **humanoid robots**, **drones**, and even **smart wearables** can possess a more powerful, longer-lasting "heart," allowing them to truly venture into the real world and assist us with more tasks. This is like equipping your electric car with an ultra-long-range battery, empowering you to embark on a spontaneous long-distance road trip!

### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

One of today's most notable pieces of news is OpenAI's breakthrough in AI models. They officially launched a preview of the **GPT-5.4 Thinking** mode, which endows **ChatGPT** with "interruptible reasoning" and a larger context window. This means users can intervene at any point during the model's response generation to guide the AI's thought process, rather than waiting for it to complete an answer before readjusting, which will significantly reduce the need for repetitive prompting. According to ETIH EdTech News, this update signals AI's shift from purely conversational applications to supporting more complex knowledge work and software development. GPT-5.4 can also support a context window of up to **one million tokens**, processing larger amounts of information, with improved token efficiency. ZDNET even reported in early March that GPT-5.4 has already surpassed human experts in professional work tasks with an **83%** probability.

Concurrently, **Google AI**'s **Gemini model** has received a major update in "personal intelligence." This new feature allows Gemini to connect to users' personal data such as **Gmail**, **Google Photos**, **YouTube**, and **search history**, thereby providing more personalized and relevant responses. For instance, if you're searching for tires, Gemini can suggest tire types suitable for your car model based on your insurance documents in Gmail. This feature is currently available in beta to eligible **Google AI Pro** and **Ultra subscribers**.

In hardware innovation, **Samsung SDI** announced today that it will unveil a **full solid-state battery** prototype developed for "physical AI" applications at the upcoming InterBattery 2026 exhibition. This battery will adopt a **pouch cell form factor** to support lightweight design, with a target of mass production in the **second half of 2027**. They plan to apply this technology to **humanoid robots**, various **robot platforms**, **aerial platforms**, and **next-generation wearable devices**, to meet the high safety, high energy density, and high power output demands of these physical AI applications. Samsung SDI will also showcase ultra-high power uninterruptible power supply (UPS) and battery backup unit (BBU) solutions, which are indispensable for AI data centers, at the exhibition.

However, the rapid development of AI also brings severe challenges in ethics and governance. **OpenAI**'s head of robotics and consumer hardware, **Caitlin Kalinowski**, resigned today. She stated that her resignation stemmed from concerns over OpenAI's agreement with the **U.S. government** to allow its AI technology for military and domestic surveillance. Kalinowski stated on social media platform X that OpenAI did not take enough time to deliberate before agreeing to deploy its AI models into the Pentagon's classified cloud network. She emphasized: "The issues of **unwarranted surveillance** of Americans and **unauthorized lethal autonomy** require more consideration." OpenAI subsequently issued a statement, saying that its agreement with the Pentagon establishes a responsible framework for AI in national security, drawing clear red lines against **domestic surveillance** and **autonomous weapons development**.

At the national strategic level, **China** is actively promoting its "AI+" initiative. The work report of the National People's Congress Standing Committee emphasized that China will strengthen legislative research in areas such as artificial intelligence. Minister of Industry and Information Technology **Li Le-cheng** stated that this year, efforts will be fully directed towards the research and iterative upgrading of next-generation AI products such as **brain-computer interfaces**, **autonomous vehicles**, and **robots**, and AI will be deeply integrated into the manufacturing industry. In China's 14th National People's Congress Fourth Session work report, "AI" was mentioned more than **50 times**, highlighting its determination to view AI as a new engine for economic growth.

In AI talent development, **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** and the **National Applied AI Alliance (NAAIC)** are expanding AI training in U.S. community colleges. This collaboration aims to help community colleges establish AI-focused curricula through faculty training, industry-relevant courses, and certification pathways. Currently, over **300 colleges** have joined the alliance, more than **1,000 teachers** have participated in training programs, and over **31,000 students** have been provided with AI-related learning opportunities.

However, AI infrastructure development also faces challenges. **OpenAI** and **Oracle** have decided to cancel plans to expand a large AI data center in **Abilene, Texas**. The expansion originally planned to add approximately **600 megawatts** of capacity. Sources indicate that negotiations stalled due to funding issues and OpenAI's evolving infrastructure needs. This project was part of the "Stargate initiative" jointly promoted by OpenAI, Oracle, and SoftBank, aiming to build up to **10 terawatts** of computing power and attract up to **$500 billion** in investment. The cancellation of the expansion may create opportunities for other tech companies, with reports suggesting that **Meta Platforms** is considering leasing the expansion space planned for the project.

### What Does This Mean for Us?

These latest advancements in AI have profound implications for each of us.

Firstly, OpenAI's **GPT-5.4** and Google's **Gemini personal intelligence** are changing the way we interact with the digital world. Previously, our use of AI was more about giving commands and awaiting responses. Now, with **interruptible thinking** and **personalized data integration**, AI is no longer a passive tool but more like a partner that actively understands you and collaborates with you. Imagine no longer needing to spend vast amounts of time organizing documents or searching for information, because AI has automatically handled it for you, even anticipating your needs based on your habits. This will significantly boost our work efficiency and convenience in life, akin to having your digital doppelgänger handle various chores.

However, every coin has two sides. The **resignation of OpenAI's robotics lead** serves as a wake-up call, reminding us of the importance of AI ethics and governance. When AI technology becomes powerful enough to influence national security and even life-and-death decisions, we must consider: who sets these rules? How do we ensure AI is not abused? This isn't something tech companies can decide unilaterally; it's a global issue requiring the joint participation of governments, society, and experts. This is also why major nations like China are actively promoting AI legislative research, which is so crucial. If these ethical issues are not properly addressed, the advancement of AI could instead bring enormous social risks, even triggering a crisis of trust. After all, we want AI to be a helper, not a runaway horse. As we discussed in a previous report, [AI Gold Rush's Double-Edged Sword: From Suicide Lawsuits to Computing Power Wars, Tech Giants' Glory and Trials](/blog/2026-03-05-014411), the ethical and safety dilemmas of AI are increasingly apparent.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

Looking at today's AI news, there are a few points particularly worth "insiders'" deep thought.

Firstly, OpenAI's GPT-5.4 and Google Gemini's "personal intelligence" update actually represent a critical step for AI moving from "general intelligence" to "customized intelligence." In the past, we pursued AI that could be omniscient like humans. Now, I believe what's more important is for AI to "understand you" and provide unique value based on your personal context. This strategy of deeply integrating AI into personal digital ecosystems will be a key focus of competition among tech giants in the coming years. Whoever can better utilize your personal data (while strictly adhering to privacy) will be able to offer stickier, more irreplaceable services. This will also drive AI applications to transform from mere "content generators" into more complex "personal decision support systems."

Secondly, the resignation of OpenAI's robotics department head due to the Pentagon agreement is far more than just a senior personnel change. It reveals a huge gap between the speed of AI technological development and the speed at which ethical and governance frameworks are established. AI has moved from the laboratory to the battlefield, from an entertainment tool to the core of national security. When the potential of technology touches ethical red lines, internal corporate divisions and external pressures will only grow. This forces us to accelerate the establishment of international AI governance standards, especially in sensitive areas like **military applications**, **surveillance**, and **autonomous weapons**. Otherwise, AI's "superpowers" could bring "super risks." This also echoes our discussion in [AI World Rumble: Pentagon Clashes with Anthropic, Apple Teams with Google, Labor Market Surges!](/blog/2026-03-06-014549) regarding the ethical boundaries of AI in military applications.

Finally, the **cancellation of OpenAI and Oracle's data center expansion plans**, while seemingly a setback, reveals the complexity and challenges of AI infrastructure construction. The **Stargate initiative's** target of **$500 billion** in investment and **10 terawatts** of computing power illustrates the astronomical "computational power" required to train and run top-tier AI models. The site selection, energy supply, cooling systems, and financing for these ultra-large data centers are all massive engineering undertakings. Such variables may prompt AI companies to rethink their infrastructure strategies, for example, by shifting towards more distributed or diversified deployment methods to reduce single points of failure. Simultaneously, this also highlights **Nvidia's** "key player" status in the AI supply chain, as they even intervene to coordinate leased space to ensure their chips can be utilized.

All of this points to a core idea: the future of AI is no longer just a technological race, but a comprehensive "accelerated" battle concerning **technology, ethics, infrastructure, and talent**. Whoever can achieve balance and leadership in these areas will stand out in this great transformation.
