### Insider's Deep Analysis

From the perspective of a seasoned investigative reporter, I believe today's news reveals several deep-seated trends:

1.  **The "National Team" AI Race Intensifies:** In the past, we might have focused more on the AI wars between tech giants. Now, national-level strategic deployments and resource investments have become key factors determining the direction of AI development. China and India, as populous nations, possess user bases and data volumes that are "rich veins" no tech company can ignore. This "national team" competition will accelerate the popularization of AI technology but may also bring data sovereignty and international tensions.

2.  **Efficiency Gains Coexist with Social Pains:** AI brings unprecedented efficiency; this is undeniable. But the **Block** company layoff case reminds us again that the price of this efficiency gain is the transformation or even elimination of traditional labor. This is not merely a technical problem but a profound societal structural adjustment. Governments, businesses, and educational institutions need to collectively consider how to provide transition opportunities and social security for affected workers; otherwise, AI's progress could trigger more severe social problems.

3.  **Infrastructure Investment is AI's "Underlying Logic":** The massive investments by **NVIDIA** and **Lumentum** in optical and data center technologies clearly tell us that no matter how smart AI's "brain" is, it requires a strong "skeleton" and "nervous system" to support it. Chips, optical communication, cooling technology—these "unseen" infrastructures are the cornerstones of the AI world's operation. Whoever controls the core technologies and production capacity of this hardware holds the discourse power in the AI era. This is also why we emphasized the importance of HBM chips in [The AI Computing Power War Heats Up! GPT-5, Gemini Updates Grab Headlines, But HBM Chip Shortage Worries Giants?](/blog/2026-02-23-014733).

4.  **The Growing Prominence of International Cooperation and Strategic Alliances:** The cooperation between Taiwan and the US in semiconductors and high-tech fields is not just about technological complementarity but also a strategic choice under geopolitical circumstances. At a time when global supply chains face challenges, this trust and cooperation are irreplaceable for ensuring the stable supply of critical technologies and fostering innovation. This also reminds us that AI development is by no means a solo endeavor; international collaboration and alliances will be key to future success.

> What I see behind this is actually a global reshuffling of power and a deep dialogue between technology and humanity. We cannot only focus on AI's "miracles" but must also confront its "challenges" and actively seek solutions.
