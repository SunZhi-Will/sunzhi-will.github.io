Regarding AI ethics and governance, this is also a highly scrutinized topic today. From combating deepfake content to tackling fraudulent advertisements, issues of AI misuse are constantly emerging. Most notably, a dispute exists between the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) and **Anthropic** concerning the **use of autonomous weapons AI**. Concurrently, in content generation, **OpenAI** hasn't paused either, releasing **ChatGPT Images 2.0**. This signifies another leap in the quality and capability of AI-generated images. In the future, we can expect to see even more stunning AI artworks, and perhaps even more realistic virtual content.

Finally, a brand new economic concept is also gradually taking shape: the "**Token Economy**". For large AI models, the basic unit for processing information is a "token"—you can think of it as the "smallest currency" AI uses to process information. Model operations, message understanding, and generation all depend on token consumption. As AI applications become more widespread, the usage of these tokens is growing significantly. This will not only impact the cost of AI services but could even become a new standard for value measurement within the future AI industry chain.

<Callout type="info" title="A Quick Primer on the Token Economy">
A "token" is the fundamental unit that large language models use to process text. It can be a word, a word stem, or even a punctuation mark. When AI models understand and generate text, they calculate and consume resources based on "tokens." Understanding the token economy helps us grasp the cost and efficiency of AI services.
</Callout>

But on the other hand, the ethical issues brought by AI cannot be ignored. This signifies our expectations for AI are rising; it's no longer merely executing commands but possessing "agentic" capabilities within specific domains, autonomously completing tasks.
