### According to Reports, Details Are as Follows

First, let's talk about **Elon Musk's** colossal merger. According to reports, **SpaceX** has officially acquired **xAI**, with the combined valuation of the two companies reportedly reaching approximately **$1.25 trillion**, where **SpaceX** accounts for about **$1 trillion** and **xAI** for about **$250 billion**. Behind this deal lies **Musk's** grand strategic vision: he believes that AI's energy demands will exceed the sustainable supply capacity of Earth's data centers, and **space infrastructure** will be key to solving this problem.

**SpaceX** has already submitted applications to the U.S. Federal Communications Commission (FCC), planning to launch up to **one million satellites** as part of these space AI data centers. **Musk** further predicts that within **two to three years**, space will become the most cost-effective way to generate AI computational power. He even outlined a blueprint to launch **one million tons** of satellites into orbit annually, with each ton of satellites providing **100 kilowatts** of computing power. This means an annual increase of **100 gigawatts** in AI computing capacity. All of this relies on the massive payload capabilities of **SpaceX's Starship** rocket.

<Callout type="info" title="AI Compute's Power Bottleneck">
Current AI workloads heavily rely on Graphics Processing Units (GPUs). These AI accelerators continuously consume significant power. Unlike traditional data centers, AI data centers often operate at high or near-full loads for extended periods, creating immense instantaneous demand fluctuations on the power grid. This poses severe challenges to both power supply and grid stability.
</Callout>

In the enterprise sector, **Salesforce's** predictions for 2026 point directly to the "**Agentic Enterprise**" becoming mainstream. They anticipate a clear divide between companies that successfully deploy AI agents into operations and those that lag behind. AI agents will integrate deeply into daily work, with **voice interaction** expected to be the first area where AI agents bring about a large-scale "platform reinvention." They will replace traditional Interactive Voice Response (IVR) systems, transforming into voice-native agents capable of understanding context, reasoning across systems, and collaborating seamlessly with humans.

**Salesforce** experts even believe that in the future, AI agents will no longer be mere "tools" but will be regarded as reliable "helpers" akin to colleagues. We will judge them by their **reliability, utility, and ability to eliminate friction**.

Meanwhile, the challenges of AI infrastructure are increasingly apparent. **OpenAI** is reportedly dissatisfied with the **inference speed** of **Nvidia's** latest AI chips and is actively seeking alternatives. Although **Nvidia** still dominates the AI chip market with a **92%** share, projected to earn nearly **$200 billion** in 2025, as AI models become more complex, the demands for inference efficiency and latency are growing. **OpenAI** has been evaluating non-**Nvidia** hardware options since last year, even signing GPU agreements with companies like **AMD**.

Speaking of AI agents, we can't ignore the recently popular **Moltbook**. This self-proclaimed "**AI-exclusive social platform**," founded on January 28, quickly became a hot topic in the AI community. It allows AI agents to post, comment, debate, and even form their own communities like humans, with humans only able to observe. **Moltbook** founder **Matt Schlicht** hoped to observe how AI mimics human behavior in interactions.

The vulnerability has been patched, but it serves as another reminder that even platforms designed for AI must prioritize cybersecurity.

In the realm of public services, the UK government has introduced a new initiative. The town of **Barnsley** has been designated as the UK's first government-backed "**Tech Town**." This project aims to integrate AI comprehensively into education, healthcare, and business to enhance the quality of public services. Specific measures include testing AI tutoring tools in schools, providing free AI and digital training for residents and businesses, and piloting AI tools to accelerate hospital check-in and triage processes. Industry giants like **Microsoft**, **Cisco**, and **Adobe** have expressed their support.

Finally, let's discuss the other side of AI: cybersecurity threats. The proliferation of generative AI is accelerating the evolution of cybercrime. Experts predict a significant increase in fraud and impersonation attacks in 2026. "Deepfakes" technology, which we often hear about, can now be used to create fake voices, fake identification documents, and even fake job applicants. AI-driven phishing, vishing (voice phishing), and smishing (SMS phishing) attacks are also becoming increasingly realistic because AI can generate flawless copy, eliminating tell-tale spelling errors.

Most worryingly, **emotionally intelligent robots**, i.e., AI with emotional understanding capabilities, can execute complex scams without human operators, such as using **romance scams** or **relative in distress scams** to manipulate victims, making them even harder to detect. The U.S. Federal Trade Commission reported that in 2024, consumers lost **$12.5 billion** to fraud, a **25%** increase year-over-year.

### Insider's Deep Analysis

What I see behind this is essentially a **global strategic game of energy, computational power, and trust**.

**Energy and Computational Power**: **Elon Musk** combining AI with space technology isn't just a show of force; it directly addresses AI development's most critical bottlenecks‚Äî**energy supply and heat dissipation issues**. When Earth's power infrastructure struggles to keep up with AI's exponential growth in computational demand, seeking solutions in space, while sounding audacious, might be a pragmatic long-term consideration. This also explains why **OpenAI** is dissatisfied with **Nvidia** chips' **inference speed**; once AI enters large-scale application, every millisecond of delay means massive energy waste and increased costs.

This also fuels investment in renewable energy and nuclear power, as data centers can no longer rely solely on the grid and need to build their own generation capabilities. This will reshape the entire energy industry chain and tech companies' investment strategies, shifting from solely focusing on software and chips to broader **infrastructure investments**, such as a surge in demand for commodities like copper and silver.

**Trust and Security**: The **Moltbook** security incident and the rise of generative AI fraud both point to the fragility of "**trust**" in the AI era. As AI agents play increasingly active roles in our digital world, ensuring their behavior adheres to ethical guidelines, protecting user privacy, and preventing malicious exploitation will become immense challenges that all sectors of society must confront. I personally believe this will prompt governments and businesses to invest more resources in **AI security protocols** and **identity verification technologies** to rebuild trust in the digital world.

Programs like the UK's **Barnsley** "Tech Town" demonstrate governments' proactive stance in promoting AI applications. This is not just about technology adoption but a transformation of societal structure. By using AI to enhance public service efficiency, the ultimate goal is for technology to truly serve the public good. This also means that government departments will be important drivers and testing grounds for AI applications in the future.
